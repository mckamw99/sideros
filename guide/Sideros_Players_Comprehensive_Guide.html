Introduction: What is Sideros?

Sideros is a tabletop roleplaying game of fate, identity, and defiance. The Loom of Fate weaves every living creature’s future — destiny as a literal cosmic fabric, maintained by a universe that has opinions about what should happen next. Your character is a thread in that fabric.

This matters because the primary antagonist, Aethar — the Serpent Bearer, a former 12th Circle Comet-Mage — intends to destroy the Loom entirely. Not conquer it. Not control it. End it. The consequences of fate ceasing to exist are not abstract: without the Loom, Void-Husks multiply, bonded relationships dissolve, magical paths collapse, and the world reduces to entropy. Aethar has specific reasons for wanting this. He may not be entirely wrong about the Loom’s flaws. He is wrong about the solution.

The Loom

The Loom is a cosmic structure that predates every civilization, every Kindred, and every recorded history. It weaves probable futures from the Aether — the fundamental energy underlying all matter and magic. Every creature has a Fate Number: a permanent numerical identifier assigned at birth that marks their position in the Loom’s pattern. Numbers that fall near each other on the Loom are drawn toward Fated Bond relationships. Numbers that fall near 13, 66, or 99 carry the Hollow Serpent’s resonance — Aethar’s patron entity and the existential threat at the campaign’s centre.

Paths and Depths

Characters advance through Resonance Depths (1-20, with some Paths extending further). Each Depth represents a deepening of the character’s relationship with their Path’s source of power. A Depth 1 Primal Jarl has just tasted their Wrath State for the first time. A Depth 20 Primal Jarl is a force of nature comparable to the Legendary Beasts that anchor the zodiac. A Calling is chosen at Depth 3 — the specific direction within the Path that defines the character’s long-term development.

The Ashen Coil

The Ashen Coil is Aethar’s organisation. Publicly it operates as a trade syndicate, an academic society, and a network of political advisors. Privately it is a structured intelligence operation with three levels: outer-ring agents who do not know what they are part of, inner-ring operatives who know the mission but not the final stage, and the Triad (Aethar, Serath, Drava Nullsong) who know everything. The Coil produces Void-Husks, manipulates political succession crises, controls Aether-Dampening Ore supply, and actively tracks Fated Bond lineages to prevent the Quint Bond from forming.

The Core Mechanic

Every roll in Sideros uses the same structure. Roll a d20, add your relevant Attribute modifier, add your Mastery Bonus if proficient, and compare to the Threshold the Game Master sets.

Roll d20 + Attribute Modifier + Mastery Bonus (if proficient) vs Threshold Meet or beat the Threshold to succeed. Common Thresholds: 8 (trivial) · 12 (standard) · 16 (difficult) · 20 (very hard) · 25 (extraordinary).

Two types of roll

Active Resonance You are attempting something — picking a lock, recalling lore, persuading an NPC. Equivalent to an Ability Check. Warding Resonance Something is happening to you — resisting poison, staying conscious under mental assault. Equivalent to a Saving Throw. Each attribute has one.

Modifying rolls

Resonant Fortune Roll two d20s and use the higher result. Granted by abilities, favourable circumstances, and Fated Bond relationships. Equivalent to Advantage. Resonant Discord Roll two d20s and use the lower result. Imposed by conditions, environmental penalties, or opposing abilities. Equivalent to Disadvantage.

The Six Resonance Attributes

Your six attributes each have a score (typically 8-20 at Depth 1) and a modifier: (score - 10) / 2, rounded down. The modifier is what you add to rolls, not the score.

Mastery Bonus: +2 at Depth 1. Increases to +3 at Depth 5, +4 at Depth 9, +5 at Depth 13, +6 at Depth 17. Add your full Mastery Bonus to checks in Disciplines where you are Proficient. Double it for Expertise. Equivalent to Proficiency Bonus.

Vitality, Guard Rating &** Loom Tests**

Vitality (= Hit Points) Determined by your Path’s Vitality Die + Endurance modifier per Depth. When Vitality drops to 0, you make Loom Tests. Three successes stabilise you. Three failures mean death. Natural 20 = regain 1 Vitality and stand. Natural 1 = counts as two failures. Guard Rating (= Armour Class) How hard you are to hit in combat. Base: 10 + Swiftness modifier. Armour adds to this. Some abilities grant Guard Rating from other attributes. An attacker’s Strike Resonance (attack roll) must meet or beat your Guard Rating to deal damage.

Weaving — How Magic Works

Magic is called Weaving. Practitioners Weave a Working (spell) by spending an Aetheric Channel (spell slot). Not every Path can Weave.

Aetheric Channels Your magical resource — equivalent to spell slots. Channels come in tiers (T1-T5). Spend a Channel of the required tier to Weave a Working. Recover all Channels on a Deep Attunement. Some Paths recover partial Channels on Brief Attunement. Weaving Threshold The difficulty number for your Workings (= Spell Save DC). Formula: 8 + Mastery Bonus + Weaving Attribute modifier. Your Path specifies which attribute powers your Weaving. Targets make a Warding Resonance vs this number.

Aether-Dampening Ore: Certain locations suppress Weaving entirely — Channels cannot be spent, Workings fail. Dunmarim are immune to Ore. Accord-Makers function at reduced tier rather than failing.

Deep Attunement (= Long Rest) Roughly 8 hours of rest. Recovers all Vitality, all Aetheric Channels, and resets most once-per-rest abilities. Brief Attunement (= Short Rest) About 1 hour of rest. Spend Vitality Dice to recover Vitality. Some Paths (Coil-Stalker, Scion-Pledged) recover their primary resource on Brief Attunement.

PART I: CHARACTER CREATION

The Concept

In Sideros, a character is not built — they are discovered. The mechanical decisions you make in the following steps will be more meaningful if you begin with a sense of who this person is before those mechanics are applied. You don’t need a complete story. You need a seed.

Three questions to answer before proceeding. Write a sentence for each. These answers will guide every decision that follows.

You are creating a character for Sideros — a world in its fourth and most precarious era. The twelve gods fell. The Legendary Beasts who were those gods wander the earth. An organization called the Ashen Coil is actively working to dissolve the seals holding the worst of what the Fall released. The Kallos-Sworn exist to prevent another catastrophe. The Loom of Fate is watching. The Quint Bond prophecy is in motion.

Your character does not need to know any of this. Most people don’t. What they know is their corner of the world — their city, their people, their work, their losses. The larger pattern will find them. That is what the Loom does.

What do you want?

Drives the Path

Not a goal — a want. Safety. Recognition. Understanding. Justice for a specific wrong. The restoration of something lost. To be left alone. To never be left alone again. This want will determine which Path the character has been called to, and which Calling within that Path resonates most strongly. A character without a want is a character without direction. The want doesn’t have to be noble. It has to be real.

What have you lost?

Drives the Origin

Every compelling character in Sideros is defined partly by absence. A home. A person. A belief about how the world worked. A version of themselves. Loss is not backstory padding — it is the pressure that shapes what you’ve become. What your character has lost will inform their Origin, their bonds, and the flavor entries that make them feel like a person rather than a stat block.

What does the Loom want with you?

Drives the Fate Number

The Loom of Fate weaves every soul into the world’s pattern for a reason. You don’t have to know what that reason is yet — in fact, it’s better if you don’t. But consider: does your character feel watched? Do they have a recurring sense that things happen around them, not to them? Do they attract people who seem to need something from them? This intuition will become the Fate Number in Step V. For now, just notice it.

Choose Your Origin

Your Origin is not your Path. It is what came before — the experiences and relationships and choices that made you the kind of person the Path would eventually call. A Comet-Mage with a Merchant Origin brings a particular kind of pragmatism to their formulations. A Primal Jarl with a Scholarly Origin fights differently than one with a Wanderer Origin. The Origin shapes the person; the Path shapes the power.

Your Calling — the specific direction within your Path — is chosen at Depth 3, not Depth 1. This is intentional. The first two Depths of play are the character discovering which aspect of their Path resonates most deeply. You should know your options now (they’re listed in the Path document), but the choice itself happens through play.

Character Advancement

Characters grow through Resonance Depths – the Sideros equivalent of character levels. Depth 1 is where every character begins. Depth 20 is the standard capstone. Some Paths (notably Zodiac Priest) extend into an Epic Tier beyond Depth 20.

Advancement Methods

Method How It Works
Milestone (Recommended) The Game Master decides when the party advances based on story events, session accomplishments, and narrative momentum. Typically 2-4 sessions per Depth in the early game, lengthening as Depths increase. Your safety survey responses have been collected and compiled. The Game Master has run your Fate Numbers. The session works through a structured agenda. You do not need to have your character completely figured out — Session Zero often changes things. That is the point.

Things to Know

Session Zero is not Session One. No combat, probably no dice. It is a conversation, not gameplay. Working correctly means the agenda runs cleanly.

Your character may change after this session. The Fate Number reveal and the Resonance conversation often reframe things. Revision after Session Zero is expected and normal. The Game Master sets the cutoff: typically 48 hours before Session One.

Lines established here are permanent. If something was marked as a Line in the safety survey, it does not return for renegotiation. Not mid-campaign, not in a specific scene. A Line is a Line.

The Game Master holds world canon. Questions about the world are answered by the Game Master. Your character’s story is yours. The world’s lore, history, and mechanics are the Game Master’s domain.

After Session Zero: Update your character sheet with your Kindred-Zodiac Resonance ability. Note any Bond relationships established. Confirm your arrival scenario with the Game Master if it was modified. The next time you meet is Session One.

The Fated Bond System

The Loom of Fate weaves every living creature’s future. Among the millions of threads it sustains, a small number fall into proximity with each other – not by accident, not by choice, but because the pattern the Loom is building requires those specific frequencies together. Most people will live their entire lives and never know their Fate Number. Those who do rarely find anyone within range. Those who do find someone rarely experience the specific shared moment that activates the thread. Those who do rarely complete the acknowledgment before the world separates them.

Finding a Fated Mate is not a feature of a good life. It is an event of such rarity that most cultures have myths about it rather than firsthand accounts. The Coil has been preventing it from happening for fifty years. The Loom has been trying to produce it for centuries. These facts are in direct tension.

The Veiling – Why Most People Never Bond

Most people die without ever knowing their Fate Number. A Stellar Cartographer is required to reliably read it. Cartographers charge for the service. The Coil has spent fifty years bribing, threatening, and occasionally disappearing independent Cartographers. Those that remain often report to the Fang of the Whisper’s Register. Finding a trustworthy Cartographer who will not report your Number to a Coil-adjacent database requires either wealth, connections, or luck.

Even people who KNOW their Numbers face the remaining barriers: finding someone with a compatible Number who also knows their own, experiencing a Kindle Moment together, surviving the Thread State without the Coil or circumstance severing it, and completing the Acknowledgment ceremony before the window closes.

Both present simultaneously. | Each speaks the acknowledgment in the other’s hearing – not scripted, not ceremonial, just true. | A witness (any person; the acknowledgment gains additional Aetheric weight if the witness is a Stellar Cartographer or another bonded individual). | The Aetheric discharge: at the moment of full acknowledgment, candles extinguish within 30 ft, animals within 60 ft go still, and any Weave-capable character within range feels the Loom register the change.

The Resonant Near-Miss

People whose Numbers fall within 6 to 12 points of each other – outside the Paired Bond range but close enough to feel – experience a pull that feels identical to the early Thread State. It is genuine. The Loom is using them as emotional anchors while the real alignment is still forming elsewhere. The Near-Miss is not nothing. It just isn’t the Bond.

Near-Miss Aspect Description
What it feels like Drawn attention. Unusual clarity. The sense that this person matters in a way others don’t. The experience is real. The Loom is not misleading anyone – these are emotionally significant relationships. They simply aren’t the final answer.
What it produces A relationship that may be deeply meaningful, long-lasting, and formative. Many people build entire lives around Near-Miss connections. Some are content. Some always feel something is slightly unresolved.
False resolution The Coil’s Fang of the Whisper deliberately cultivates Near-Miss relationships for identified Quint Bond candidates – arranging that a candidate finds someone “close enough” and settles, while the true alignment never forms. This is their quietest and most effective prevention method.
Detection A Stellar Cartographer can distinguish a Near-Miss from a true Thread or acknowledged Bond. A Near-Miss has no Kindle Moment residue and no Thread-glow. It reads as “proximity without ignition.” Many Cartographers choose not to say this aloud to their clients.
The reckoning When a person in a Near-Miss relationship later encounters their true bond-partner and experiences a Kindle Moment, both understand simultaneously what the prior relationship was and was not. This is one of the more complicated experiences the Loom produces. There is no easy path through it.

The Coil’s Systematic Suppression – Game Master Reference

Aethar has read the Ifá prophecy. He was present when Orunmila fell. He understands what five bonded souls in perfect resonance can do to his plan. The Fang of the Whisper’s secondary mission is specifically structured around this knowledge.

Program Method
The Register A continuously updated record of known Fate Numbers, cross-referenced for proximity clusters. Any pair within ±5 that comes to Coil attention gets a file. Any emerging Triad gets a Priority Flag. Any Quad Bond attempt triggers Triad-level personal intervention from Drava Nullsong.
The Cartographer Program Systematic intimidation, bribery, and occasional disappearance of independent Stellar Cartographers over fifty years. Coil-affiliated Cartographers provide readings but quietly report to the Register. Finding a trustworthy Cartographer is now a meaningful quest hook.
The Separation Mechanism For identified bond pairs: not usually physical murder (Loom disruption is detectable). Fabricated scandal. A compelling job offer in a different city. A message that turns one against the other. A Near-Miss partner introduced at the right moment. Quiet, deniable, and leaves no Aetheric trace.
The Thread Suppression Ritual The most targeted tool. A Coil operative within 10 ft of either Thread party for one uninterrupted hour can apply a ritual that quietly quiets the thread until it fades. Most targets never know it happened – they just find that the pull they felt gradually becomes less urgent over weeks.
The Coil’s Blind Spot Companion Threads (animal bonds – see below) are not tracked by the Register. A companion with a compatible Number can carry information about the bond without the Coil noticing. Serath knows this but has not reported it to Aethar.

The Companion Thread – Animal Bond Mechanics

Animal companions have Fate Numbers. A Stellar Cartographer reads them differently – “like a Number written in a different hand” – but they are real, cosmic, and meaningful. When a companion’s Number falls within range of its partner’s, a distinct form of bond can develop. This is called the Companion Thread.

The Companion Thread is NOT a Fated Bond tier. It runs alongside the Fated Bond system without replacing or advancing it. A companion cannot be the “third soul” of a Triad Bond or the “fifth soul” of a Quint Bond – higher tiers require sapient souls capable of conscious acknowledgment. However, a Companion Thread amplifies any Fated Bond its partner participates in, and it has its own mechanics, formation process, and Aetheric weight.

Companion Number Proximity

Proximity Status What It Means
Within 8 points Companion Thread eligible The companion and partner are cosmically attuned. The Thread can develop through the formation process below.
9-15 points Companion Near-Miss Strong natural affinity – the companion chose this person and keeps choosing them. Not a Companion Thread, but the relationship is unusually deep. No special mechanics, but the Game Master should treat this companion as genuinely attached beyond training.
16+ points Standard companion bond Trained loyalty and genuine affection, but no cosmic dimension. The beast companion system’s Evolution Feats still apply – they just develop through relationship rather than resonance.

Companion Thread Formation

The Companion Thread forms differently from a human Fated Bond. Animals experience the world at a different register of consciousness – they cannot reflect on what they feel or compare it to alternatives. Their acknowledgment is expressed through action, not language.

Stage 1 – Attunement Recognition

Passive. From the moment of first contact, a companion within Number range feels the resonance as simple comfort – an unusual calm around this specific person that does not occur around others. The partner feels a reciprocal sense of being more clearly understood by this animal than they have been by any other. A Stellar Cartographer within 60 ft sees a faint glow around both.

Stage 2 – The Companion Kindle

A specific shared moment that reveals the depth of the companion’s choice. Three types qualify:

Kindle Type Description
The Loom Test Witness The partner reaches a Loom Test (0 Vitality) and the companion stays at their side rather than fleeing to safety, physically guarding the body. Most animals flee dying creatures instinctively. A companion that stays has chosen something over instinct.
The Fight Beyond Capability The companion engages a threat it manifestly cannot defeat in order to protect the partner – not winning, not helping to win, but buying time. The companion continues fighting until physically incapacitated or the threat is resolved. The partner must witness this.
The Three Returns The partner sends the companion away (for its safety, as punishment, to spare it from something) and the companion returns of its own will. Three times. The companion returns despite being sent away three times – the third return constitutes the Kindle Moment. Cannot be manufactured; the partner cannot repeatedly “send away” the companion with intent to trigger this.

Stage 3 – The Witnessed Promise

The partner speaks a promise to the companion in the presence of a witness – not a formal ceremony, but a specific spoken commitment. The companion must be awake and present. The promise does not need specific wording, but it must be genuine and it must include: acknowledgment of what the companion did or chose, a commitment of reciprocal protection, and permission for the companion to leave if it ever chooses.

The permission clause is essential. The Companion Thread cannot form if the companion is bound – it must be a bond between free creatures.

Companion Thread Abilities

Ability Mechanic
Bond Sense (passive, from Thread formation) Both always know the other’s general state – calm, distressed, afraid, injured. Range: 1 mile. The companion cannot communicate language, but it can communicate urgency, direction, and emotional state through the Bond Sense.
Synchronized Instinct (from Witnessed Promise) Once per Moment as a free action (does not cost Act or Instinct), the partner can give the companion one simple command that it will prioritize above all else until the partner’s next Moment. This command overrides training, overrides threat response, and overrides the companion’s own judgment. Use sparingly – companions remember.
The Loom’s Protection (passive, from Witnessed Promise) Once per session, when the companion would be reduced to 0 Vitality, it drops to 1 Vitality and falls unconscious instead. The partner, wherever they are, feels the near-death as 1d6 points of psychic damage that cannot be reduced. No action can suppress this.
Bond Amplification (when partner is in a Fated Bond) A companion with a Companion Thread amplifies its partner’s Fated Bond abilities when within 30 ft. Specifically: the Loom Test conversion (1 failure to success per Pulse) now applies at 60 ft range instead of 30 ft. The companion’s proximity extends the Bond’s reach.
Resonant Presence (Triad or higher partner) A companion with a Companion Thread, when within 30 ft of a Triad-or-higher bonded group, adds its Number’s frequency to the group’s Aetheric field. This does not count toward tier advancement, but it visibly amplifies the field – Stellar Cartographers describe it as “a fifth thread that doesn’t fit the pattern but belongs.” The Coil finds this confusing and has not fully classified it.
Coil Blind Spot (intelligence value) The Fang of the Whisper does not track Companion Threads. A companion can carry a message, an object, or information about a bonded cluster across Coil checkpoints without triggering the Register’s alert protocols. Serath knows this. She has not told Aethar.

When a Companion Thread Is Severed

Cause Effect
Companion death The Bond-Severed condition applies as if a Fated Bond member died – -2 to all Active Resonances for 1d6 months. The world becomes slightly less real. The permission clause in the Witnessed Promise does not mitigate this: the companion chose to stay.
Partner death The companion does not become a Void-Husk equivalent. Animals do not conceptualize death the same way. But the companion changes – Accord-Walkers and Stellar Cartographers note that a Thread-bonded companion after partner death carries the Number’s resonance differently, as if holding it in trust.

The companion may still choose to stay. The Loom notes the release. The partner’s Fate Number shifts very slightly – less than 1 point, detectable only by a Master Cartographer.

Disciplines Reference

Disciplines are Sideros’s equivalent of skills. When your character attempts a task requiring particular training or expertise, you make an Active Resonance: roll d20 + relevant Attribute modifier. If you have Mastery in that Discipline, also add your Mastery Bonus. Without Mastery, you add no bonus beyond the Attribute modifier.

Discipline Attribute What It Covers
Athletics Forcefulness Climbing difficult surfaces, swimming in currents, jumping for distance or height, any feat of sustained physical effort, grappling, breaking objects
Intimidation Forcefulness Threatening someone into compliance, projecting physical menace, coercing through implied force, interrogation through fear
Acrobatics Swiftness Keeping balance on unstable surfaces, tumbling past enemies, staying upright when knocked, performing agile stunts
Sleight of Hand Swiftness Pickpocketing, prestidigitation, planting objects on others, palming items, subtle manual work without being seen

Primal Essence Reference

Primal Essence is the soul’s fundamental Aetheric orientation, rolled at character creation on 1d6. It is not alignment – it is more fundamental than values. An evil Alpha is still Alpha. A compassionate Omega is still Omega. Essence shapes how the Fated Bond recognition feels and has mechanical weight in some Path and Bond abilities.

Roll Essence Frequency Bonus Narrative Expression
1 Alpha ~1 in 6 + 2 Resonance when leading a group (Disciplines, Active Resonances) *Leaders, initiators, vanguards. Not necessarily domineering – some lead by example, some by force, some by being the first to speak. On Fated Bond recognition: feel **“seen for the first time.”*
2-5 Beta ~4 in 6 None (most reliable, most adaptable) *The backbone of every institution. Most characters are Beta. The Kallos-Sworn’s institutional memory is primarily Beta. Essence is rolled, never chosen. It represents the soul’s resonance orientation as determined by the Loom – not something the character decided about themselves. Players who strongly dislike their result can discuss with the Game Master, but the Loom’s weave is what it is.

✶ ✶ ✶ THE WORLD OF SIDEROS World Primer | Page XX | For Players

✶ THE WORLD OF SIDEROS ✶ Sideros is in its fourth era — the Shattered Age. The twelve gods of the Pantheonic Council fell when Ophiuchus was corrupted by the Hollow Serpent and shattered. The remaining twelve gods chose to dissolve themselves into vast Aetheric presences rather than dissolve entirely: they became the Legendary Beasts. They no longer govern — but they remember, and they recognize mortals whose soul-frequency resonates with their domain. ✶ THE LOOM OF FATE ✶ The Loom is a cosmic structure predating every civilization. It weaves probable futures from the Aether. Every creature has a Fate Number (1d100, permanent) marking their position in the Loom’s pattern. Numbers that fall near each other are drawn toward Fated Bond relationships. Numbers near 13, 66, or 99 carry the Hollow Serpent’s resonance. The primary antagonist, Aethar the Serpent Bearer, intends to destroy the Loom entirely. ✶ KEY FACTIONS ✶ Ashen Coil Aethar’s organization. Publicly: trade syndicate & academic society. Privately: structured intelligence operation pursuing the Five Stages. Three layers: outer-ring agents (unknowing), inner-ring operatives (partial knowledge), Triad (full knowledge). Produces Void-Husks, controls Ore supply, tracks bond lineages to prevent the Quint Bond. Kallos-Sworn The twelve-house order preventing another Pantheonic catastrophe. Each house corresponds to a zodiac sign and a Legendary Beast. Centuries-long intelligence war against the Coil. Their institutional memory is primarily Beta. Arch-Oracle Lyra has calculated the Loom seal’s failure timeline. Three Conclaves The world’s three Weaving scholarly organizations. Their combined Aetheric pressure is the only thing keeping the Crystalline Core sealed. Shifting Horizons (Aethar’s former Conclave), Earthly Core, and Veil-Walkers. Destabilizing them is Aethar’s Stage 1 objective. Loom of Fate org. A Beta organization — patient, networked, invisible. Weaves fate at an institutional level. Fated Bonds form between souls in resonance regardless of alignment. The Ashen Coil’s internal bond is genuine, real, and one of the reasons they’re dangerous. Order of Pure Reason Dunmarim-affiliated. Anti-Aetheric ideology. Controls Aether-Dampening Ore production. Banned Weep-Stone import. Active conflict with the Coil over Ore supply chain control. ✶ THE QUINT BOND PROPHECY ✶ Five souls within 1 point of each other on the Fate Number scale. Never recorded in written history. The Ifá scripture names them “the Five Who Remember What the Gods Were.” Serath has identified 3 of 5 and withheld this information from Aethar for six weeks. The Fang of the Whisper’s true secondary mission is to prevent these five from ever occupying the same location simultaneously. ✶ LEGENDARY BEASTS &** ZODIAC SIGNS ✶** Each Beast was once a god. Zodiac Attunement requires encountering your sign’s Beast within 300 ft (it must perceive you) and surviving with your Fate Number intact, then reaching Depth 4.
