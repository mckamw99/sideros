[b]Also called:[/b] Kurgan-kin, Bog-Walkers, the Patient Hunters

[h2]Lore[/h2]
The Lizard-Folk of the Great Kurgan Bog are not related to the Serpent-Kin of K'uh-la'an — a common surface-dweller misconception based on superficial appearance. The Serpent-Kin are an ancient civilization with deep Dream-Fish connections and centuries of institutional sophistication. The Lizard-Folk are [b]nomadic hunters who have survived the Kurgan Bog by being more patient than everything that wants to kill them.[/b] Different cosmological relationship, different culture, different everything except the scales.

The Bog is one of the most dangerous environments in Sideros — the Stygian Scorpion makes its lair in the Sunken Peat-Cavern beneath it, Mycelian Brood spores drift through the lower cave systems, and the ground itself is unreliable. The Lizard-Folk have adapted to this by developing an exquisitely precise threat-assessment capacity: [b]they can read environmental conditions the way a Moana-kin reads the ocean.[/b] The Bog speaks in pressure changes, smell gradients, vibration patterns, and the behavior of creatures three strata below them. A Lizard-Folk clan moves through the Bog like a hand through water — leaving no trace, reading everything.

[h2]Kindred Traits[/h2]
[b]Bog Predator — Core Trait[/b] — [i]Passive[/i]
Lizard-Folk have Hold Breath (up to 15 minutes), swim speed 30 ft, and leave no tracks in natural terrain (swamp, forest, caves) unless they choose to. They have Resonant Fortune on Stealth Active Resonances in natural environments and cannot be tracked by mundane means in natural terrain. [b]Ambush predator[/b]: when a Lizard-Folk attacks a creature that has not yet acted in combat (surprise round), they deal +2d6 damage and the target must make an Endurance Warding Resonance (Threshold 13) or be Resonance-Locked for 1 Pulse — the shock of the strike outpaces the target's ability to respond.

[b]Environmental Reading[/b] — [i]Passive[/i]
Lizard-Folk read the natural world as fluently as others read text. They automatically know: current weather for the next 6 hours, the presence and approximate number of living creatures within 300 ft in natural terrain, whether any creature has passed through this area in the last 24 hours (and roughly how large), and whether any food or fresh water is within 1 mile. [b]In the Kurgan Bog specifically[/b] (or any swamp terrain): this range doubles, they can detect Mycelian Brood spore density, and they know the location of the nearest Stygian Scorpion secondary tunnel entrance if within 2 miles.

[b]Scorpion-Kin Understanding[/b] — [i]Passive[/i]
Generations of proximity to the Stygian Scorpion's lair has given Lizard-Folk partial resistance to the Heart-Sting Toxin: they make Warding Resonances against it with Resonant Fortune, and even on a failed save, they suffer only half the normal duration. [b]More significantly[/b]: Lizard-Folk can communicate basic intent with the Stygian Scorpion's lesser spawn (the scorpion-creatures that patrol the Bog's lower strata) — not language, but clear non-aggression signals. Scorpion-spawn will not attack a Lizard-Folk unprovoked, and a Lizard-Folk can redirect spawn pursuit toward other targets by expending a minute of careful behavioral signaling.

[b]Regeneration[/b] — [i]Passive[/i]
Lizard-Folk can regrow lost extremities over time — fingers in 1 week, a hand in 1 month, an arm in 3 months. They regain 1 Vitality at the start of each Pulse while below half their maximum Vitality — the Bog-adapted metabolism continuously attempting repair. [b]This regeneration stops[/b] if the Lizard-Folk has taken fire damage in the current Pulse (fire disrupts the biological repair process) or if they are Aetheric-Depleted at Stage 3 or higher (the body's resources are too depleted to sustain both function and regeneration simultaneously).

[h2]Playing this Kindred[/h2]
The Lizard-Folk is the party's survival specialist and ambush predator. In wilderness and dungeon environments they are nearly undetectable before they choose to act, and the first strike when they do act can be devastating. Regeneration changes how you play damage — a Lizard-Folk player can afford to be more aggressive because the body repairs itself between engagements. The fire vulnerability to regeneration is the balancing cost: a Lizard-Folk who absorbs fire damage has to play wounded until they take a Brief Attunement away from fire. Scorpion-Kin Understanding is the most unusual social ability in the game — the ability to negotiate with ambush predators creates infiltration options other Kindreds simply don't have.
