[h2]Lore[/h2]
The Dunmarim did not discover Aether-Dampening Ore because they were mining for it. They discovered it because they needed to survive. In the immediate aftermath of the Pantheonic Fall, the raw divine aether bleeding from the fallen gods made the deep earth [b]violently unstable[/b] — geological events, magical surges, and random Aetheric discharges made underground living genuinely lethal. The Dunmarim, already established deep in the mountain roots, found that certain ore deposits were unnaturally calm. Animals didn't flee from them. Aetheric events didn't happen near them. The ore was a refuge before it was a weapon.

This origin matters because it explains the Dunmarim's relationship with their monopoly. They don't guard the ore because they want power over magic-users. They guard it because for the first three centuries after the Fall, [b]the ore was the only reason they were alive.[/b] The protectiveness calcified into culture before the danger passed. By the time the world stabilized, the Dunmarim had built their entire civilization around the principle that the ore was theirs and outsiders could not be trusted with it. Lady Capricorn enforces this not from greed but from four thousand years of collective trauma.

The Dunmarim are exceptional craftspeople not because they have more innate talent than other Kindreds, but because [b]craft is what you do when you cannot rely on magic.[/b] Every non-magical solution to every problem, refined over centuries of necessity. Their metalwork is the finest in the world. Their architecture has stood for millennia. Their written records are the most complete historical documents in existence — they kept writing even when the Aether was trying to kill them.

[h2]Kindred Traits[/h2]
[b]Ore-Sense — Core Trait[/b] — [i]Passive[/i]
Dunmarim can detect Aether-Dampening Ore, Black Iron, and all other significant mineral deposits within 60 feet without any Active Resonance — this is passive, continuous, and cannot be suppressed. They also always know if they are underground, how deep, and in which cardinal direction they are traveling relative to the nearest surface. [b]In the Stillness[/b], this sense intensifies rather than quieting — the total Aetheric absence is as clear and readable to them as a map.

[b]Aether-Hardened[/b] — [i]Passive[/i]
Generations of living adjacent to Aether-Dampening Ore has made Dunmarim biology partially resistant to Aetheric forces. [b]All Workings that target a Dunmarim have their Working Threshold reduced by 2[/b] — they are simply harder to affect magically than other Kindreds. Additionally, Dunmarim cannot be Soul-Swayed (Charmed) by any Working of tier 3 or lower. They have Resonant Fortune on all Warding Resonances against magical compulsion effects. [b]The cost[/b]: Dunmarim can never cast Workings themselves. They have no Weaving Attribute and cannot take any Path that requires Aetheric Channels as a core feature. They may take Zodiac Attunements that produce non-Channel effects.

[b]Master's Hand[/b] — [i]Passive[/i]
Dunmarim begin play with Mastery in two craft Disciplines (choose from: Blacksmithing, Stonecutting, Gemcutting, Engraving, Brewing, or Engineering). When a Dunmarim crafts an item using Mastered tools, the crafting time is halved and the item's quality is automatically one tier higher than the materials used would produce. [b]A Dunmarim smithing with common iron can produce uncommon-quality work.[/b] A Dunmarim crafting with Black Iron produces items the rest of the world considers impossible.

[b]Stone's Endurance[/b] — [i]Passive[/i]
Dunmarim Vitality Dice are d10 rather than d8. They have Resonant Fortune on all Warding Resonances against poison, disease, and Aetheric Depletion. They also have Resonant Fortune on all Warding Resonances against being moved against their will — shoved, knocked prone, telekinetically displaced. [b]The Khalkotauros's energy runs in Dunmarim blood regardless of their zodiac sign[/b] — Taurus Dunmarim feel this kinship most acutely, but all Dunmarim share the foundational stubbornness.

[b]Clan Record[/b] — [i]Passive[/i]
Every Dunmarim carries their clan's oral and written history back at least ten generations. They have Mastery in History Discipline for all events relating to their clan and the Stone Kingdom of Dunmar. When they encounter a forgery, a false claim about Dunmarim history, or a document that has been altered, the Game Master must tell them immediately — it is an automatic recognition, not a roll. [b]Additionally[/b]: one Dunmarim per generation is designated the Clan-Keeper — the one responsible for maintaining and extending the record. A Dunmarim who is their generation's Clan-Keeper has Resonant Fortune on all Resonance and Attunement Active Resonances when speaking to someone about matters of history, debt, or obligation.

[h2]Playing this Kindred[/h2]
The Dunmarim's signature constraint — no Aetheric Channels — is not a punishment. It is a character. A Dunmarim in a world where everyone else uses magic is someone who has solved every problem magic solves through craft, endurance, and stubbornness instead. They are the most physically resilient Kindred and the most magically resistant. In a party, a Dunmarim is the one who is still standing when the Aetheric chaos has knocked everyone else down — and who has already finished repairing their armor while the mages are recovering. The Aether-Hardened limitation creates natural party dynamics: the Dunmarim goes where magic fails and stays when magic turns dangerous.
