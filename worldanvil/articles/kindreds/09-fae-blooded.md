[h2]Lore[/h2]
The Fae-Blooded are what happens when Otherworld bargains compound across generations. A single Tuathan ancestor who traded something significant with an Otherworld entity — time, memory, a child's name, the color of the sky at a specific moment — begins a lineage in which the Aetheric boundary between the material world and the Otherworld becomes progressively thinner. By the third or fourth generation, children are born with Noctilith-pale eyes, hair that moves without wind, and an instinctive awareness of things that aren't quite present. By the sixth or seventh generation, [b]the Fae-Blooded child is not entirely in either world.[/b]

This is not metaphor. The Fae-Blooded exist in a state of perpetual partial translucency — they are fully present in the material world but always partially perceivable from the Otherworld as well. Fae entities can see them regardless of concealment. Otherworld spirits sometimes speak to them unbidden. The Otherworld Gate Faerie Mound that opens at twilight opens more easily for a Fae-Blooded than for anyone else — because from the Otherworld's perspective, [b]the Fae-Blooded are already halfway through.[/b]

[h2]Kindred Traits[/h2]
[b]Twilight Nature — Core Trait[/b] — [i]Passive[/i]
Fae-Blooded exist partially in the Otherworld at all times. They can perceive Otherworld entities, spirits, and phenomena that are invisible to other Kindreds — this requires no Active Resonance, it is simply how they see. They can also communicate with these entities. [b]As an Instinct[/b], the Fae-Blooded may briefly step further into the Otherworld — becoming partially translucent for 1 Pulse. During this Pulse: they can move through creatures (not objects) as difficult terrain; attacks against them have Resonant Discord; but they cannot make physical attacks themselves. They return to full materiality at the start of their next Moment. [b]The Otherworld always notices when they step in[/b] — the Concordance rolls privately to determine whether anything there notices and investigates.

[b]Noctilith Resonance[/b] — [i]Passive[/i]
Fae-Blooded and Noctilith (Moon-Stone) resonate at the same frequency. They can charge Noctilith by holding it for 1 hour under any light conditions (not just moonlight) — the stone absorbs their partial Otherworld nature rather than the moon's energy. Charged Noctilith handled by a Fae-Blooded has its Working effects enhanced by one tier. [b]Additionally[/b]: Fae-Blooded can use Noctilith as a focus for any illusion Working, halving the Aetheric Channel cost. They were practically born for this material.

[b]Seasonal Attunement[/b] — [i]Passive — varies by birth-tree[/i]
Like all Tuathan tradition practitioners, Fae-Blooded align with a birth-tree from the Celtic Tree Zodiac. During their birth-tree's season, all Active Resonances have Resonant Fortune. Outside their season, they have Resonant Discord on Warding Resonances against Otherworld entities — the season's misalignment makes their partial Otherworld nature harder to protect. [b]At the solstices and equinoxes[/b], all Fae-Blooded experience a moment of perfect seasonal alignment regardless of birth-tree — for 24 hours they have Resonant Fortune on all Active Resonances and can open any Otherworld threshold they know of without the standard ritual.

[b]Fae Bargain[/b] — [i]Active · Ritual[/i]
Once per session, a Fae-Blooded may attempt a formal bargain with an Otherworld entity through the Faerie Mound or any known Otherworld threshold. The bargain can request abilities, information, passage, or assistance beyond the character's current capacity. [b]The Concordance determines the cost[/b] — always something personal, time-limited, or memory-affecting. The Fae-Blooded may refuse after hearing the cost. Costs examples: 24 hours without speaking; forgetting one specific memory until the next solstice; becoming visible to all Otherworld entities for one week; the entity holds a favor to be called in at any time. [b]Unresolved bargains accumulate[/b] — a Fae-Blooded with more than three outstanding unpaid Fae Bargains begins experiencing involuntary Otherworld incursions during sleep.

[b]Iron Weakness[/b] — [i]Vulnerability[/i]
The Otherworld half of a Fae-Blooded's nature is vulnerable to iron — specifically, unworked or minimally worked iron (not Black Iron, which has been sufficiently refined). Contact with unworked iron causes discomfort; [b]weapons of unworked iron deal +1d4 damage[/b] against Fae-Blooded. They cannot willingly hold unworked iron for more than a Pulse without making an Endurance Warding Resonance (Threshold 12) or dropping it. This weakness is not widely known outside the Tuathan Archipelago — Fae-Blooded who travel often learn to conceal it. The Otherworld entities who know about it, however, use it freely.

[h2]Playing this Kindred[/h2]
The Fae-Blooded is the Kindred of accumulated consequence. Their power comes from bargains — generational ones already paid, and new ones they choose to make. The Fae Bargain system is the game's most explicit risk-reward mechanic: you can get almost anything, but you always pay something personal. The iron weakness and the lowest Vitality Dice in the game mean a Fae-Blooded player has to be thoughtful about positioning. They are not fragile in the way an untrained Human is fragile — they have the Twilight Step and seasonal Resonant Fortune. But they cannot absorb punishment the way a Wolf-Shifter or Dunmarim can. They survive by being somewhere else when the blow lands.
