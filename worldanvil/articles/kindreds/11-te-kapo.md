[b]Also called:[/b] the Void-Readers, Dark-Seers, the Blind Ones (misnomer), Obsidian-Born

[h2]Lore[/h2]
The Te Kapo are called "the Blind Ones" by surface cultures who don't understand them, and the name reveals more about surface culture than about Te Kapo. They are not blind. They have functional eyes — large, pale, adapted for bioluminescent light. What they have chosen, as a civilization, is to [b]develop perceptual capacities that require darkness to access.[/b] The surface world's constant light pollution — stars, fire, Aetheric fields, the glow of populated settlements — drowns the subtle signals that the Te Kapo have trained themselves to read for generations. When they come to the surface (rarely, reluctantly, usually under urgent circumstances), they find it disorienting in the way a person with exceptional hearing finds a loud room disorienting.

The Kore-kore perception — the ability to sense the Hollow Serpent's movements through its void-within-void signature — took their civilization generations to develop. It is not a gift or a mutation. It is the result of [b]deliberate, multigenerational, systematic training in the perception of absence.[/b] They began by learning to perceive the dark zodiac signs (the negative space around each constellation). They progressed to reading the Aether's absences rather than its presences. The Kore-kore emerged from this practice the way any skill emerges from sufficient depth of attention — not as a revelation, but as a natural extension of everything that came before.

[h2]Kindred Traits[/h2]
[b]Dark-Zodiac Perception — Core Trait[/b] — [i]Passive System[/i]
Te Kapo begin play with access to the Kore-kore perception system (see Mechanics Codex, System VII) and two dark-zodiac Discipline abilities of their choice (from: Kore-ahi/Aries, Kore-kōrero/Gemini, Kore-roto/Scorpio, or Kore-kore/Ophiuchus detection). They gain one additional dark-zodiac ability at Resonance Depths 5 and 10. [b]In complete darkness[/b], all Te Kapo dark-zodiac abilities function at double range. In bright light (sunlight or equivalent), dark-zodiac perception is suppressed entirely — the signal is drowned. [b]Blindsight 30 ft[/b]: Te Kapo navigate by echolocation-adjacent spatial awareness in darkness — they are not impeded by it and cannot be surprised in dark environments.

[b]Absence Reading[/b] — [i]Passive[/i]
Te Kapo perceive what is not there as clearly as other Kindreds perceive what is. They automatically detect: hidden doors, concealed passages, Aether-Veiled creatures, invisible objects, and the negative space left by something that was recently removed. [b]No Active Resonance required[/b] for any of these. The Concordance tells a Te Kapo player when any of these are present within 60 ft. They also detect the emotional residue of spaces — not precise psychometry, but a general quality: "this room has held fear recently," "something important was decided here," "this passage has been avoided for years."

[b]Bioluminescent Marking[/b] — [i]Active · At will[/i]
Te Kapo skin carries bioluminescent markings that can be controlled consciously — dimmed to nothing or brightened to dim-light levels (covering a 10-ft radius). The markings' patterns convey meaning within Te Kapo culture: specific configurations signal emotion, intent, identity, and warning. Surface-dwellers cannot read the patterns without extensive Te Kapo contact. [b]Communication[/b]: Te Kapo can communicate complex ideas silently through bioluminescent pattern — any Te Kapo within sight can receive full semantic content. This cannot be intercepted or detected by non-Te Kapo without specialized training.

[b]Light Sensitivity[/b] — [i]Vulnerability[/i]
Te Kapo in bright light (sunlight, Daylight Working, or equivalent) have Resonant Discord on all Perception-related Active Resonances. Their dark-zodiac perception is fully suppressed in bright light. [b]Sudden bright light[/b] (a torch lit in darkness, a flash Working) requires an Endurance Warding Resonance (Threshold 12) or the Te Kapo is Sight-Severed for 1 Pulse. They adapt to moderate light (candlelight, overcast day) without penalty over the course of an hour. [b]Many Te Kapo who work surface missions[/b] carry specialized eyewear crafted from Obsidian Deep minerals that filters light intensity — a practical solution their civilization developed centuries ago.

[h2]Playing this Kindred[/h2]
The Te Kapo is the party's intelligence asset in the dark. Every dungeon, every cave, every underground approach becomes their home environment. The Absence Reading passive makes them the most reliable trap-finder and hidden-door detector in the game — not through rolls, but through perception. The light sensitivity is real and matters : a Te Kapo player should think about light management as a character concern, not a nuisance. The eyewear solution is available in-world and should be obtained early. The bioluminescent communication system creates natural party-within-party dynamics — Te Kapo can coordinate silently in a room full of enemies.
