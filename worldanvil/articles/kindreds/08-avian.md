[h2]Lore[/h2]
The Avian are not merely bird-people. They are a Kindred for whom [b]elevation is a moral condition as much as a physical one.[/b] In the Chinvat cosmological framework, height above the earth correlates with proximity to Asha — the cosmic principle of truth and ordered existence. The highest settlements in the world (Justice-Rock Village, Ahura's Perch itself) are Avian because the Avian built them there deliberately: to be as close to truth as the physical world permits. The Wind of the Unblinking Gaze that makes falsehood impossible to speak at Justice-Rock is not a spell someone cast. It is the accumulated Aetheric pressure of a culture that has treated truth as a physical force for four thousand years.

The Avian's relationship with the Great Roc (their Legendary Beast, the former God of Divine Law) is the most direct Kindred-Beast relationship in the world. Other Kindreds venerate their Legendary Beasts from respectful distance. The Avian study the Great Roc's flight patterns to adjudicate legal disputes — the angle of its banking turns and the rhythm of its wing-beats correspond to a system of jurisprudential precedent that Avian judges have been developing since before the Pantheonic Fall. [b]The Great Roc does not know it is a legal reference system.[/b] This may be the most poignant fact about the post-Fall world.

[b]The Southern Sea Circuits:[/b] Avian scouts serve as the primary navigators and advance reconnaissance force for the Southern Sea trade corridor between Khem-Ma'at, K'uh-la'an, and the wider world. Their combination of flight, extraordinary vision (Raptor's Eye ignores cover from elevation), and truth-sense makes them uniquely effective at identifying hazards, scouting safe anchorages, and detecting the Ashen Coil's interdiction operations before surface ships encounter them. The escalating activity of the Abyssal Leviathan's spawn in the southern approach routes has made Avian scout reports the most valuable intelligence commodity on the Southern Sea Circuits — merchants who can secure an Avian scout for a voyage pay 10× the standard navigator's rate, and consider it fair.

[h2]Kindred Traits[/h2]
[b]Winged — Core Trait[/b] — [i]Passive[/i]
Avian have functional wings granting a fly speed of 30 ft. They cannot fly while wearing heavy armor (wings cannot generate sufficient lift). In medium armor, fly speed reduces to 20 ft. [b]Updraft[/b]: once per Pulse while flying, the Avian may gain altitude rapidly as a free action — rising up to 20 ft without expending movement, carried by natural air currents their Aetheric attunement reads instinctively. When diving from at least 20 ft height to make a melee Strike Resonance, the strike deals +1d6 additional damage from momentum. [b]Avian cannot hover[/b] — they must move at least 10 ft per Pulse while airborne or begin to descend.

[b]Asha-Touched Truth Sense[/b] — [i]Passive[/i]
Avian always know when they are being told a deliberate lie — not a mistaken belief or a withheld truth, but an active falsehood. This is not a roll. The Game Master tells the Avian player privately when a character has lied to them directly. [b]They may choose not to act on this knowledge[/b] — knowing someone has lied and proving it are different things, and the Avian legal tradition is specific about evidence requirements. Additionally, Avian have Resonant Fortune on all Warding Resonances against deception-based Workings and Soul-Swayed conditions.

[b]Judicial Authority[/b] — [i]Active · 1/Deep Attunement[/i]
Once per Deep Attunement, an Avian may invoke their Asha-given judicial authority in a social setting. All creatures present must make a Resonance Warding Resonance (Threshold 14) or be compelled to answer one direct question truthfully before they can choose how to frame their answer. This is not magical compulsion — it is the Aetheric weight of Asha pressing on the soul. Creatures with Resonant Fortune on deception Warding Resonances make this roll with Resonant Discord instead. [b]Void-Husks and creatures with dissolved Fate Numbers are immune[/b] — Asha cannot reach what the Loom no longer holds.

[b]Raptor's Eye[/b] — [i]Passive[/i]
Avian vision is extraordinary — equivalent to looking through a magnifying glass at long range. They can read text at 300 ft, identify a creature's face at 500 ft, and detect motion at 1 mile in clear conditions. In combat, they can perceive fine details of opponents (weapon types, equipment condition, visible injury) without any Active Resonance. [b]Ranged Strike Resonances[/b] made from elevation (at least 20 ft above the target) ignore half and three-quarters cover — the Avian's ability to read angles makes obstacles less effective against their aim.

[b]Chinvat Oath-Binding[/b] — [i]Active · Ritual[/i]
An Avian who witnesses an oath sworn between two parties may formally invoke the Chinvat binding — a 10-minute ritual requiring the Avian's active participation. The oath becomes Aetherically binding: the Game Master tracks compliance, and any party who breaks the oath's specific terms immediately receives the Dread-Struck condition for 24 hours as Asha's displeasure manifests. [b]This works on willing parties only[/b] — an oath cannot be forced. But an Avian's mere presence in a negotiation, known to be capable of this, shifts the social dynamics of any agreement they witness.

[h2]Playing this Kindred[/h2]
The Avian's central dynamic is the weight of knowing the truth in a world built on convenient fictions. The truth sense is always on. The Avian always knows when people are lying. What they do with that knowledge — the discipline to stay silent, the judgment to choose the right moment, the legal precision that separates knowing from proving — is the entire character. The wings matter too : Avian naturally take the high ground, literally and figuratively. A party with an Avian member has aerial reconnaissance, a built-in truth detector, and someone who can bind oaths with Aetheric weight. They also have someone who will absolutely notice when the local lord is lying about why they need the party's help.
