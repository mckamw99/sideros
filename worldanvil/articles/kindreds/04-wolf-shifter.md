[b]Also called:[/b] Uji, the Two-Skinned, Omote-Ura-Born, Pack-Kin

[h2]Lore[/h2]
The Wolf-Shifters are not humans who can turn into wolves, or wolves who learned to walk upright. They are a Kindred for whom the distinction between these two things [b]never existed in the first place.[/b] The Omote (human surface-self) and the Ura (wolf instinct) are not in conflict — they are two expressions of a single continuous being, the way a river is simultaneously the still water near its banks and the current at its center. The philosophy of balance between these states, called the Uji Way, has produced two distinct cultural expressions: the [i]Pine Shadow Clan[/i], which emphasizes Omote discipline, and the [i]Deep Earth Clan[/i], which honors Ura heritage.

What outsiders consistently misunderstand about Wolf-Shifters is that [b]the wolf form is not the dangerous one.[/b] A Wolf-Shifter in full wolf form is operating on pure instinct — they may be feral, but they are comprehensible. A Wolf-Shifter caught between forms, with their Omote-Ura Gauge unstable and unbalanced, is genuinely unpredictable because neither self is dominant and each is fighting the other for control. The Clan elders teach that the only time a Wolf-Shifter is truly dangerous to their own people is when they have denied the Ura for so long that it erupts without warning.

The Shōgun-Kami — nature spirits governing each clan's totem tradition — are not gods and not Legendary Beasts. They are a third category: ancient nature intelligences who predate the Pantheonic Fall and exist outside the zodiac framework entirely. [b]Every Wolf-Shifter has a Shōgun-Kami connection through their clan.[/b] Whether they cultivate it is a choice. Whether it cultivates them is not.

[b]The Grove of Fallen Stars:[/b] Deep within Mori-Ōkami's taiga lies the Grove of Fallen Stars — a sacred, iridescent sanctuary where celestial objects have struck the earth across millennia, leaving craters filled with bioluminescent vegetation and star-metal traces. The Grove is the primary habitat of the [b]Star-Metal Stag[/b], the Legendary Beast of the Sagittarius domain, and the Wolf-Shifter clans consider it under their protection as a matter of sacred obligation. This creates an active and bitter conflict with Dunmarim prospecting expeditions, who covet the Grove's star-metal deposits and the shed antler fragments of the Stag itself — a component of the Resonant Binding ritual and an extraordinarily valuable crafting material. The Dunmarim approach is methodical and persistent; the Wolf-Shifters respond with territorial violence. Neither side has communicated to the other what they actually need the star-metal for. A party that understood both motivations could potentially broker access — but the Dunmarim would need to admit they know about the Resonant Binding ritual, and the Wolf-Shifters would need to admit the Grove's Aetheric significance, which they regard as sacred secret.

[h2]Kindred Traits[/h2]
[b]Omote-Ura Gauge — Core Trait[/b] — [i]Active System[/i]
Wolf-Shifters use the [b]Omote-Ura Gauge[/b] (see Mechanics Codex, System II) — a 10-segment tension meter between human discipline and wolf instinct. Begin each session at 5 (Balanced) or clan default (Pine Shadow: 4, Deep Earth: 6). The Gauge shifts based on actions taken in play. Omote bonuses (1–4) enhance social and tactical abilities. Ura bonuses (7–9) enhance physical and primal combat. Full Ura (10) causes loss of conscious control. Full Omote (1) suppresses the wolf form but causes the wolf to surge reflexively when reduced to 0 Vitality, jumping to 7.

[b]Shifting Form[/b] — [i]Instinct (no cost)[/i]
As an Instinct, the Wolf-Shifter may shift between human, hybrid (humanoid with wolf features — ears, claws, enhanced senses), and full wolf form. [b]Human form[/b]: all social Disciplines at full capacity. [b]Hybrid form[/b]: +1d6 slashing on unarmed strikes, +10 ft movement, Resonant Fortune on Perception Active Resonances. [b]Wolf form[/b]: movement 50 ft, +2d6 slashing on bite, cannot speak or use tools, full scent tracking. Shifting does not cost an Act — it is an expression of nature, not a decision to execute. Shifting moves the Gauge +1 toward Ura.

[b]Pack Resonance[/b] — [i]Passive[/i]
Wolf-Shifters are attuned to the presence of their pack — which in adventuring contexts means their Bonded allies. They always know the general direction and emotional state of any Bonded party member within 1 mile, regardless of obstacles. [b]Additionally[/b]: when a Bonded ally within 60 ft drops to 0 Vitality, the Wolf-Shifter's Gauge jumps +2 toward Ura immediately. This is involuntary. The pack instinct to protect is not a choice — it is a biological reality. A Wolf-Shifter who knows this about themselves can plan around it. One who doesn't can endanger the very allies the instinct is trying to protect.

[b]Shōgun-Kami Bond[/b] — [i]Active · 1/Deep Attunement[/i]
Once per Deep Attunement, a Wolf-Shifter may spend 10 minutes in ritual prayer to their clan's Shōgun-Kami. The Kami grants one [i]Primal Boon[/i] — a temporary ability that lasts 24 hours based on the Kami's nature. [b]Pine Shadow Kami boons[/b] are tactical: enhanced tracking, pack coordination, environmental awareness. [b]Deep Earth Kami boons[/b] are primal: enhanced strength, regeneration, temporary immunity to fear. The boon must be earned — the Kami will not grant it to a Wolf-Shifter whose Gauge is at Full Ura (the instinct cannot hear the Kami when it is screaming).

[b]Primal Senses[/b] — [i]Passive[/i]
Wolf-Shifters have superior scent tracking — they can identify any creature by scent, track a creature by scent trail up to 24 hours old, and detect emotional states through scent (fear, aggression, deception) with a DC 10 Attunement Active Resonance. In human form, these senses are diminished but present. In hybrid or wolf form they are fully active. [b]Scent cannot be blocked by Aether-Veiled (Invisible) conditions[/b] — a Wolf-Shifter with active scent tracking always knows an invisible creature's location within 30 feet.

[h2]Playing this Kindred[/h2]
The Wolf-Shifter's core experience is managing a tension you cannot fully control . The Gauge shifts whether you want it to or not. The Pack Resonance surges when allies fall regardless of tactical sense. The question is not "how do I avoid the Ura?" — it's "how do I position myself so the Ura surge goes where I need it?" A Wolf-Shifter played well is someone who has made peace with their own nature — not suppressing the Ura, not surrendering to it, but knowing when to let it forward and when to hold it back. The Clan matters enormously : Pine Shadow characters experience the Gauge as a discipline practice. Deep Earth characters experience it as an ancestral call. Same mechanics, completely different fiction.
