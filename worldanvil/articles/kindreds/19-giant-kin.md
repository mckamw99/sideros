[h2]Lore[/h2]
The Giant-Kin stand between seven and eight feet tall and weigh between 280 and 340 pounds, and they carry this mass with an ease that suggests their skeleton is doing something other skeletons are not. The Boreal naturalists have a theory — that the Giant-Kin's bone density is partially Aetheric, a residual effect of whatever giant lineage produced them. The Giant-Kin do not find this theory interesting. They are aware their bones are unusual. They have always been aware. The bones were there before the theory.

The Boreal Expanse's Norse cultural tradition treats the Giant-Kin as the oldest people of the north — not the first to arrive, but the first to be there, in the sense that the landscape itself seems to have been made for people their size. The Runic Knight tradition emerged specifically from Giant-Kin warriors who discovered that the rune-marks appearing naturally on their skin at puberty could be activated rather than merely observed. [b]Every Giant-Kin carries their runic inheritance in their skin. Not all of them choose to wake it up.[/b]

In the Duchy Compact outside the Boreal Expanse, Giant-Kin occupy an unusual social position — too large to be ignored, too rare to be common, and too self-contained to be easily categorized. They are not aggressive by default. They are simply operating at a scale that others find confrontational whether or not confrontation is intended. A Giant-Kin who has spent time in cities has learned to make themselves smaller in manner if not in body — to move carefully, speak quietly, and choose their moments. [b]The Primal Jarl tradition is disproportionately populated by Giant-Kin who got tired of making themselves smaller.[/b]

[h2]Kindred Traits[/h2]
[b]Giant's Might[/b] — [i]1/Brief Attunement[/i]
As an Instinct, enter Giant's Might for 1 minute. While active: melee Strike Resonances deal +1d6 damage; Forcefulness Active Resonances have Resonant Fortune; count as Large-sized for grappling, shoving, and determining whether you can wield oversized weapons. The Giant-Kin's Aetheric bone-density expresses itself fully for a short duration rather than its normal partial expression. Giant's Might ends early if the Giant-Kin falls unconscious.

[b]Runic Inheritance[/b] — [i]Always Active + Activated[/i]
Natural runic marks appear on the Giant-Kin's skin at puberty — shaped like the Runic Knight's carved runes but organic, expressing the individual's sign and lineage rather than a trained selection. These marks are cosmetic unless activated. 
[b]Runic Activation (1/Deep Attunement):[/b] Spend 1 minute in meditation to activate one rune from the following list — the benefit lasts 24 hours: 
 • [i]Rune of Storm (Ild):[/i] Resistance to fire and cold damage 
 • [i]Rune of Stone (Stein):[/i] +1 Guard Rating; cannot be knocked prone 
 • [i]Rune of Cloud (Skye):[/i] Resonant Fortune on Swiftness Warding Resonances; +5 ft speed 
 • [i]Rune of Hill (Haug):[/i] Resistance to all non-magical damage (lasts 1 minute, not 24 hours — too costly to sustain)

[b]Large-Adjacent[/b] — [i]Always Active[/i]
While technically Medium for rules purposes, the Giant-Kin's size produces several Large-equivalent effects at all times: carry capacity equals a Large creature's; reach is 10 ft rather than 5 ft for unarmed strikes and natural weapons; can wield weapons sized for Large creatures with Resonant Discord (rather than being unable to wield them at all). Standard Dungeon Constraint rules still apply — the Giant-Kin cannot navigate tunnels built for Medium creatures without crouching, and some structures require explicit Concordance rulings about access.

[b]Stone's Endurance[/b] — [i]1/Brief Attunement[/i]
When taking damage, use a Reflex to reduce that damage by 1d12 + Endurance modifier — the Aetheric bone-density absorbs what it can. Usable once per Brief Attunement. This is the trait the Trench Keepers of the Maw recruit Giant-Kin for specifically. Repeated structural damage to a defensive line is survivable when the front-line fighters have bones that behave like partially Aetheric material.
