[b]Homeland:[/b] Ashélands of Orun-Ayé · diaspora communities in Port Damaris and the Aquarian Archipelago
[b]Primal Essence:[/b] All Essences — shaped by patron Orisha's nature
[b]Also called:[/b] the Orisha-Marked, Ashé-Born, the Living-Divine

[h2]Lore[/h2]
The Ashé-Touched are not a separate Kindred in the way a Dunmarim is separate from a Human — they are Humans whose [b]Ashé has awakened[/b]. Ashé is the living cosmic life-force that flows through all matter, but in certain bloodlines — those with persistent Orisha connection — it flows with unusual visibility. An Ashé-Touched individual bears [i]divine traits[/i] inherited from their patron Orisha: Shango-Touched may crackle with faint electricity under emotional duress. Yemaya-Touched have eyes that shift to deep blue in water. Oshun-Touched are unnaturally warm to the touch, their skin carrying a faint honey scent. These traits cannot be suppressed and cannot be hidden for long.

This is not magic. The Comet-Magi, who insist everything must be categorized within their Aetheric framework, have spent a century trying to understand Ashé as a variant Aetheric phenomenon and failed. [b]Ashé is not Aether.[/b] It predates the Aetheric system. It predates the Pantheonic Fall. It is the force that was flowing through the world when the gods were still gods, and it continues to flow now that they have become beasts. The Orisha are still present in Ashé. What looks like biology [i]is[/i] biology — but it is biology that contains a fragment of a fallen god.

The Order of Pure Reason's anti-magic field targeting Ashé-Touched is not an accident of collateral damage. It is the Order demonstrating, correctly, that they understand the threat: [b]if you cannot suppress Ashé, you cannot control the Ashélands.[/b] The Ashé-Touched cannot be disarmed in the way a Comet-Mage can be disarmed by removing their Resonant Geode focus. Their power is in their blood. You would have to remove the blood.

[h2]Kindred Traits[/h2]
[b]Orisha Mark — Core Trait[/b] — [i]Passive System[/i]
At character creation, choose a [b]patron Orisha[/b]. This determines the Ashé-Touched's divine trait (visible biological marker), their domain bonus, and their deepest vulnerability. 
[b]Eshu-Elegbara[/b]: Crossroads trait (eyes flicker between colors). Domain: never lost in transition spaces; Resonant Fortune on all rolls at thresholds and beginnings. 
[b]Ogun[/b]: Iron trait (faint metallic sheen on skin under stress). Domain: Mastery in all metal tools; +1d6 force on melee strikes in righteous conflict. 
[b]Shango[/b]: Thunder trait (hair stands during emotion; faint static). Domain: Resonant Fortune on Warding Resonances against lightning; once per Pulse, strikes deal +1d8 lightning. 
[b]Yemaya[/b]: Ocean trait (eyes shift blue in water; perfect swimming). Domain: swim speed 40 ft; breathe water; Resonant Fortune on healing-related Active Resonances. 
[b]Oshun[/b]: Honey trait (warm skin, sweet scent, bees never hostile). Domain: Resonant Fortune on Resonance Discipline checks; once per Deep Attunement, cause a creature to speak one truth they are withholding. 
[b]Oya[/b]: Storm trait (hair moves without wind during distress). Domain: Resonant Fortune on Warding Resonances against death effects; can speak to and receive information from the recently dead (within 10 minutes of death). 
[b]Obatala[/b]: White trait (hair white from birth; calm aura). Domain: Resonant Fortune on all Warding Resonances against Soul-Swayed; presence suppresses aggression (creatures in 10 ft radius must make DC 13 Warding Resonance to initiate violence). 
[b]Orunmila[/b]: Fate trait (faint golden eyes). Domain: Resonant Fortune on all Active Resonances involving destiny, divination, and the Loom; can ask the Concordance one yes/no question per session about a character's Fate Number.

[b]Living Ashé[/b] — [i]Passive[/i]
Ashé-Touched Workings cost one fewer tier of Aetheric Channel when used within the Ashélands or at an Orisha shrine — the ambient Ashé supplements their personal reserve. [b]The Order of Pure Reason's anti-magic field deals 2d8 psychic damage to Ashé-Touched immediately upon entering it[/b] — this is not suppressible. Outside the field, all Ashé abilities function normally. This damage cannot be reduced. It is not a magical effect — it is a biological one, the field trying to suppress something that is part of the Ashé-Touched's cellular structure.

[b]Orisha Resonance[/b] — [i]Active · 1/Deep Attunement[/i]
Once per Deep Attunement, the Ashé-Touched may call upon their patron Orisha's direct attention for 1 minute. During this time, all Domain abilities (from Orisha Mark) double in potency and range. The Orisha is not possessing — they are [i]present[/i]. NPCs within 60 feet who are familiar with Ashé tradition will recognize the Orisha's presence and react accordingly (fear, reverence, or both). [b]This is distinct from the Ashé-Medium's Full Possession[/b] — the Orisha is present but not in control. The Ashé-Touched is still themselves, amplified.

[b]Community Root[/b] — [i]Passive[/i]
Ashé flows strongest through connection. Whenever an Ashé-Touched completes an Act that directly benefits another creature (healing, protecting, sharing resources, completing a communal obligation), they immediately restore 1 Aetheric Channel of the lowest available tier. This happens automatically, without Sustained Resonance or intent. [b]The Ashé-Touched cannot deliberately trigger this[/b] — the act must be genuinely for the other person, not instrumentally for the Aetheric Channel restore. The Concordance makes this judgment. Players are encouraged not to be precious about it — genuine communal impulse is usually obvious.

[b]The Ifá Thread[/b] — [i]Passive (Orunmila-Marked only)[/i]
Ashé-Touched with the Orunmila patron are aware of the Ifá tradition's 4,000-year persistent bond — the chain of initiated Babalawos and Iyanifas who maintain active contact with Orunmila's distributed consciousness. They begin play as an initiated practitioner (Babalawo or Iyanifa, their choice). This grants: access to Ifá divination (the complete 256-Odù system, usable as a once-per-session ritual taking 1 hour to ask a complex question and receive a layered answer); the ability to add their Resonance modifier to any Active Resonance made to understand a person's true motivation; and [b]awareness of the Ofun Meji void-configuration[/b] — they automatically recognize it if they see it and feel profound unease (they may not read it).

[h2]Playing this Kindred[/h2]
The Ashé-Touched's core experience is being visibly divine in a world that doesn't know what to do with that. The divine trait cannot be hidden. Everyone who looks at them long enough will notice something. The patron Orisha shapes personality as much as mechanics — an Ogun-Touched approaches problems through craft and righteous conflict; an Oshun-Touched approaches them through relationship and sweet persuasion; an Oya-Touched is always aware of what's ending and what's crossing over. The Order of Pure Reason damage is not optional — playing an Ashé-Touched means accepting that there is a major faction in the world that your biology makes dangerous to be near. This is a feature of the character, not a flaw in the design.
