[h2]Lore[/h2]
The Moana-kin do not have a homeland in the way other Kindreds do. They have [i]routes[/i]. The concept of a fixed home — a place you return to because it is where you are from — is a philosophical position that the Moana-kin regard with respectful incomprehension. [b]Home is the last island where you were genuinely welcomed.[/b] The next island is where you're going. The ocean between is where you actually live. This is not rootlessness — it is a different relationship to roots, one that runs through water currents and star-paths rather than soil.

The Waka-tōhora Navigator-Priests who lead Moana-kin society are not priests in any religious sense. They are the ones who know the Ara Moana — the twelve star-path system that maps the zodiac onto navigational reality. Their authority comes from the capacity to find land in featureless ocean, to predict weather three days out from wave patterns, and to read the deep swell's memory of distant events. [b]In a world where most Kindreds look up at the zodiac, the Moana-kin navigate by it.[/b] This gives them a relationship to cosmic forces that no Comet-Magi has ever fully understood — they don't study the stars, they use them.

[h2]Kindred Traits[/h2]
[b]Ara Moana Attunement — Core Trait[/b] — [i]Passive System[/i]
Moana-kin begin play with access to the Ara Moana navigation system (see Mechanics Codex, System VI). They gain two Ara Moana abilities at character creation (choose from: The First Step, Deep Reading, Triangulation, Shelter Sense, Season Reading, or Horizon Sight) and one additional ability at Resonance Depths 5, 10, and 15. [b]These abilities function in any environment[/b] — ocean, underground, spirit realms, time-distorted spaces — anywhere that movement exists to read. In the Mirror Sea void zone, all Ara Moana abilities fail. A Moana-kin who enters the Mirror Sea and exits alive gains permanent Mirror Sea Sense: they can detect the boundary from 100 miles away.

[b]Ocean-Born Body[/b] — [i]Passive[/i]
Swim speed 40 ft and can breathe water. Moana-kin do not experience seasickness, cannot be disoriented by wave motion, and have Resonant Fortune on all Warding Resonances against being knocked prone or moved against their will while on water or underwater. [b]Weather Reading[/b]: Moana-kin can predict local weather up to three days in advance with complete accuracy by spending 10 minutes observing the ocean, sky, and wind — no Active Resonance required. On land, this range drops to 24 hours. This is not a Working. It is observation refined into instinct by generations of necessity.

[b]Pre-Fall Star Charts[/b] — [i]Active · 1/Session[/i]
The coral walls of Hōkūle'a Atoll carry star charts that predate the Pantheonic Fall — observations of the zodiac forces before they became Legendary Beasts. A Moana-kin who has studied these charts (requiring access to the Atoll, which requires surviving the ocean journey there) carries their memory. Once per session, they may consult this memory to ask the Concordance one question about what a zodiac force [i]was[/i] before the Fall — the original divine nature, not the current beast form. The answer is always accurate and always incomplete: the charts recorded what the navigators observed, which was physical and environmental, not theological.

[b]Waka Community[/b] — [i]Passive[/i]
Moana-kin do not travel alone if they can help it. When a Moana-kin takes the Help action/Act to assist another character, both the Moana-kin and the assisted character gain Resonant Fortune on the roll — not just the recipient. The Moana-kin's experience is enhanced by the act of sharing it. [b]Additionally[/b]: any vessel the Moana-kin navigates moves at 1.5× its normal travel speed and requires one fewer crew member than its standard complement — their navigation efficiency reduces operational burden across the whole ship.

[h2]Playing this Kindred[/h2]
The Moana-kin's defining characteristic is always knowing where they are and where everything else is going. The Ara Moana abilities — Triangulation especially — make them the party's most reliable information-gatherer in environments other characters find disorienting. They are the Kindred who remains calm in the dungeon, the spirit realm, and the time-distorted Otherworld because they are always finding their reference points. The Mirror Sea is their one blind spot — and a Moana-kin who has survived it carries that experience as both a scar and a superpower. Consider letting Mirror Sea Sense emerge naturally through play rather than starting with it.
