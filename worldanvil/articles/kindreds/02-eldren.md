[h2]Lore[/h2]
The Eldren are not ancient in the way stones are ancient. They are ancient in the way [b]a language is ancient[/b] — still spoken, still functional, but carrying within every word the ghost of a meaning that has been slowly, irreversibly, losing precision for four thousand years. They are the survivors of Kallos. They carry in their blood what their ancestors understood as science and now practice as religion — mantras that are degraded Precursor equations, mudras that are simplified operation gestures for a global magical grid their civilization built and lost.

The Eldren do not know this. The Siddha Masters who teach in the Scattered Temples believe they are transmitting spiritual truth. They are. But the spiritual truth [i]is[/i] the science — the equation and the mantra are the same thing perceived through different cultural frameworks, four thousand years apart. An Eldren who encounters a Ceque-Keeper walking instruction from Elethorn will feel, without being able to articulate why, that they are [b]reading a version of their own prayers in a language they almost recognize.[/b]

Eldren lifespans extend to several centuries. They age visibly but slowly — an Eldren of two hundred looks perhaps fifty by human standards. Their relationship with time is consequently different: they plan on decade-long scales naturally, they remember events that Humans regard as ancient history as personal experience, and they struggle with the urgency that short-lived Kindreds take for granted. [b]The most dangerous thing about the Eldren is that they are right about most things and know it.[/b] The most dangerous thing to the Eldren is that being right doesn't always matter in time.

[h2]Kindred Traits[/h2]
[b]Kallos Blood — Core Trait[/b] — [i]Passive[/i]
Eldren carry degraded Precursor science in their cellular memory. They begin play with [b]Mastery in Aetheric Lore and one Siddha Tradition[/b] (choose: Mesha, Vrishabha, Mithuna, Karkata, Simha, Kanya, Tula, Vrishchika, Dhanus, Makara, Kumbha, or Meena). This Siddha Tradition grants them one Ambient Working from that sign's domain — usable without Aetheric Channels. The mantra doesn't fully make sense to them. It works anyway. [b]They can also read and write the Precursor script[/b] — a language that appears in the ruins of Elethorn and in certain suppressed records in Argent Spire.

[b]Attunement Sense[/b] — [i]Passive[/i]
Eldren perceive active Aetheric fields without effort. They always know when any Working is being enacted within 60 feet, when an Aetheric Artifact is present within 30 feet, and when a grid node (active, dormant, or reactivating) is within 300 feet. This is not Detect Magic — it requires no Act and no Aetheric Channel. It is simply how Eldren perceive the world. [b]In the Stillness of Dunmar, this sense goes quiet[/b] — Eldren find this experience deeply disorienting, like suddenly losing a sense they didn't know they had.

[b]Ancestral Resonance[/b] — [i]Active · 1/Deep Attunement[/i]
Once per Deep Attunement, an Eldren may enter a brief trance (1 minute, requires stillness) and access a fragment of their civilization's collective memory. Ask the Concordance one question about the pre-Fall world — the answer will be accurate to Era I or Era II but may be fragmentary, symbolic, or filtered through the Eldren's cultural framework. [b]Questions about the Night of Kallos always return emotionally distorted.[/b] The Eldren cellular memory carries that grief in a way that corrupts precise recollection.

[b]Long Memory[/b] — [i]Passive[/i]
Eldren have Resonant Fortune on all Active Resonances involving history, ancient languages, pre-Fall locations, and the origins of any institution more than 200 years old. They also have Resonant Fortune on recognizing forgeries — documents, artifacts, or claims about historical events. [b]However[/b], they have Resonant Discord on Active Resonances involving technology or cultural practices that emerged within the last 100 years. They genuinely struggle to update their model of the world at speed.

[b]Fey Resilience[/b] — [i]Passive[/i]
Eldren are immune to mundane disease and have Resonant Fortune on all Warding Resonances against Corrupted (Poisoned) conditions. They do not need to sleep — they enter a four-hour meditative trance that provides all the benefits of a full night's rest. During this trance they remain aware of their surroundings. [b]An Eldren in the meditative trance counts as performing meditation for Tapas Point recovery[/b] — they are always, in some sense, practicing.

[h2]Playing this Kindred[/h2]
The Eldren's mechanical identity is deep knowledge and Aetheric sensitivity at the cost of present-world agility . They are the Kindred best positioned to understand what is happening cosmically — and worst positioned to adapt quickly when the situation changes. In play, an Eldren character should feel like someone who is always right about something you didn't know was relevant . Their Long Memory limitation (Resonant Discord on new things) is not a punishment — it is a character note. The Eldren who overcomes this through a Human or Wolf-Shifter ally's sense of urgency is one of the most interesting relationships in the game.
