[b]Homeland:[/b] Terra Firma urban sewers · Argentum undercity · Aurelia subterranean districts
[b]Primal Essence:[/b] Beta-dominant · community survival through collective resourcefulness
[b]Also called:[/b] Scavengers (pejorative), Sewer-Kin, the Overlooked, Kuri-folk

[h2]Lore[/h2]
The Kuri are what happens when a Kindred is pushed to the margins of every major civilization simultaneously and survives anyway. They have been called scavengers, saboteurs, and vermin by every surface culture in Terra Firma for as long as anyone can remember. [b]They have been all of these things, when survival required it.[/b] They have also been engineers, architects of underground infrastructure, brilliant improvisational chemists, and the only Kindred who fully understands how every major city in Terra Firma actually functions from the inside out — because they live in the parts the city's builders didn't think anyone would inhabit.

The Kuri saboteurs in Aurelia's sewers are not simply attacking the Leonine Empire out of malice. They are the political expression of a Kindred that has been systematically excluded from every institution the surface world has built, has watched those institutions claim dominion over the spaces the Kuri call home, and has run out of patience for polite approaches. [b]The Kuri are not villains.[/b] They are a Kindred making rational choices about an irrational situation. A Kuri character who has left the sewer networks to travel the wider world carries this context — the knowledge of what they are to most of the world's institutions, and the choice about what to do with that knowledge.

[b]Port Operations:[/b] Beyond the urban sewer networks, Kuri operatives are the primary saboteurs currently disrupting the Western Sea Lanes trade corridor. Their Infrastructure Knowledge — the innate ability to identify every weak point, unmonitored gate, and structural vulnerability in any built environment — makes them extraordinarily effective at infiltrating port facilities, tampering with cargo manifests, and compromising docking infrastructure in ways that are difficult to detect and harder to prevent. Whether these operations are coordinated by a central Kuri authority, carried out by independent cells acting on shared grievance, or sponsored by external factions (the Ashen Coil, the Shadow-Council of Port Damaris, or rivals of the Leonine Empire) is an open intelligence question that the Loom of Fate has been unable to resolve. The Kuri, characteristically, have provided no clarification.

[h2]Kindred Traits[/h2]
[b]Improvisation Mastery — Core Trait[/b] — [i]Passive[/i]
Kuri can craft functional tools and weapons from salvaged materials with a 10-minute work period. The improvised item functions as a standard version of that item type for 24 hours before degrading. [b]More significantly[/b]: when using improvised, repurposed, or unconventional tools for an Active Resonance, Kuri have Resonant Fortune rather than the usual Resonant Discord that improvised tools impose on other Kindreds. They have spent their entire existence making inadequate materials adequate. [b]Kuri also begin play with Mastery in one of[/b]: Engineering, Alchemy, Demolition, or Stealth Discipline — their underground survival has forced expertise in at least one technical field.

[b]Infrastructure Knowledge[/b] — [i]Passive[/i]
Kuri have an instinctive understanding of built environments — sewers, aqueducts, foundations, load-bearing structures, hidden passages, and maintenance access points. In any city they have spent at least one week in, they know: the fastest unobserved route between any two points, three ways to cause significant structural disruption, the location of the primary water and waste infrastructure, and where the city's institutions store things they don't want found. [b]In cities they haven't visited[/b]: Resonant Fortune on Active Resonances to find hidden passages, structural weaknesses, or underground access in built environments.

[b]Overlooked[/b] — [i]Passive[/i]
Kuri have spent generations being ignored, dismissed, and actively not-looked-at by surface civilizations. In urban environments, they can move through crowds, markets, and public spaces without drawing attention — not invisibility, but the social invisibility of the marginalized. [b]Mechanically[/b]: Kuri are never the first target of hostile attention in a group situation (other party members will always be approached first unless the Kuri has specifically drawn attention to themselves). Stealth Active Resonances in urban environments are made with Resonant Fortune. Institutional guards and officials have Resonant Discord on Perception checks to notice a Kuri behaving normally in urban public space.

[b]Toxin Resistance[/b] — [i]Passive[/i]
Kuri digestive and immune systems have adapted to the contents of urban sewer systems over generations. They have complete immunity to mundane diseases and food-borne toxins. Against magical poisons and Void-energy Corrupted conditions, they have Resonant Fortune on the Warding Resonance. [b]They can also safely eat and drink things that would kill other Kindreds[/b] — not pleasant, but possible. In survival situations underground, a Kuri will always find something nutritionally adequate to sustain themselves from the environment.

[b]Darkvision and Small Frame[/b] — [i]Passive[/i]
Darkvision 60 ft. Kuri are Small-sized, granting them access to spaces and passages that Medium-sized Kindreds cannot enter. [b]In combat[/b]: Kuri can move through the space of any Medium or larger creature (treating it as difficult terrain). They have Resonant Fortune on Swiftness Active Resonances to escape grapples. Their smaller frame means they deal slightly less damage with heavy weapons — they cannot use Heavy-tagged weapons effectively (Resonant Discord on Strike Resonances with them) but suffer no penalties with Light and Finesse weapons.

[h2]Playing this Kindred[/h2]
The Kuri is the party's urban infiltration specialist and the character with the most interesting relationship to institutions. Every institution in Terra Firma has an opinion about Kuri. Playing into that friction — the guard who won't look at you, the merchant who talks past you, the noble who doesn't notice you leave the room with something important — is where Kuri characterization lives. Infrastructure Knowledge in familiar cities is genuinely campaign-altering : a Kuri who has spent a week in Argentum knows three ways to structurally disrupt the Order of Pure Reason's headquarters. Whether they use that knowledge, and how, is the character. The lowest Vitality Dice alongside the Fae-Blooded means positioning and the Overlooked passive matter enormously — a Kuri who is noticed in combat is in trouble; a Kuri who is never quite noticed is nearly untouchable.
