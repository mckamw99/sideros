[h2]Lore[/h2]
Where the Beast-Descended carry a Legendary Beast's frequency in their blood while presenting as largely humanoid, the Beast-Kin carry it in their [i]form[/i]. They are unmistakably animal-aspected — fur, scales, feathers, chitin, or shell expressed through a humanoid skeletal structure. The morphology is not random. It follows the zodiac. [b]A Cancer-aspected Beast-Kin has the Adamant Crab's coloring, its carapace-like skin, and the way it moves that suggests something that would be more comfortable moving sideways if pressed.[/b] A Sagittarius-aspected Beast-Kin has antlers and the stag's ranging, searching quality of attention.

The Beast-Kin are found across the world in scattered communities — they have no single homeland because no single culture produced them. The best current scholarship suggests they emerged in the centuries after the Pantheonic Fall as the zodiac's newly distributed power found unexpected expression in bloodlines that had sustained contact with Legendary Beast territories. The Arcadian naturalists call this Resonant Drift. The Kallos-Sworn call it fascinating. [b]The Beast-Kin call it Tuesday.[/b]

Beast-Kin are distinct from Wolf-Shifters in a crucial way: the Wolf-Shifter's animal nature is fluid, shifting between Omote and Ura states. The Beast-Kin's animal nature is fixed — they are always fully themselves, with no suppressed form and no threshold to maintain. What you see is what they are. The Primal Accord tradition finds this philosophically significant. The Beast-Kin community finds the Primal Accord tradition's interest mildly flattering and occasionally exhausting.

[h2]Kindred Traits[/h2]
[b]Natural Weapon[/b] — [i]Always Active[/i]
All Beast-Kin have an unarmed strike that uses the sign's natural weapon — claws, horns, stinger, beak, shell-slam. The natural weapon deals 1d6 + Forcefulness modifier damage and counts as a finesse weapon. At Resonance Depth 5, damage increases to 1d8. At Depth 11, 1d10. The weapon's damage type matches the sign's Legendary Beast (see table). It cannot be disarmed. It does not need to be drawn.

[b]Beast Sense[/b] — [i]Always Active · Passive[/i]
The Beast-Kin's senses match their sign's animal — acute hearing, low-light vision, scent-tracking, or echolocation depending on the sign. All Beast-Kin have Darkvision 60 ft and Resonant Fortune on Perception Active Resonances using their primary sense (sign-dependent — sight for Sagittarius and Leo, smell for Aries and Scorpio, hearing for Gemini and Aquarius, etc.). Additionally: Beast-Kin are recognized by their sign's Legendary Beast as kin — not bonded, not protected, but recognized. The Beast will pause before attacking a Beast-Kin of its own sign. One Pulse of pause. That can be enough.

[b]Sign Morphology[/b] — [i]Sign-Dependent[/i]
Each sign produces a specific physical capability beyond the natural weapon and senses. See the morphology table below. This trait is passive and always active — it is part of what the Beast-Kin is, not an ability they activate.

[b]Feral Instinct[/b] — [i]Always Active[/i]
Beast-Kin cannot be surprised. Their animal senses process threat faster than conscious thought allows — at the start of any encounter, the Beast-Kin always acts in the first Pulse regardless of Pulse Order roll (they still roll — the roll determines position within the first Pulse, not whether they act). Additionally: Resonant Fortune on Pulse Order rolls.
