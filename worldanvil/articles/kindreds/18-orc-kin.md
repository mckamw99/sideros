[b]Also called:[/b] Replacing: Orc / Half-Orc · Stateless, dispersed across the Duchy Compact
[b]Aspects:[/b] Medium, No Fixed Homeland, Alpha-aspected (common)

[h2]Lore[/h2]
The Orc-Kin have no nation, and this is not an accident of history — it is the result of a specific historical event that the Duchy Compact's official records describe in three sentences and the Orc-Kin oral tradition describes in three hours. The short version is that the Orc-Kin held a territory in the pre-Compact era, held it well, and lost it not to war but to a treaty that the other signatories honored in legal terms and violated in practical ones. [b]The Orc-Kin response to this was not to fight — they had already won the war — but to disperse. If a homeland can be taken, do not have one.[/b]

Four hundred years later, Orc-Kin are found in every major settlement in the Duchy Compact as soldiers, laborers, guards, pit-fighters, and mercenaries. They are consistently paid less than their skill warrants and consistently perform above what their employers expected. This has produced a specific cultural attitude: work harder than anyone expects, trust the people you work alongside, and know that the institution you're working for will find a way to shortchange you eventually. Plan accordingly.

What the Compact underestimates, consistently, is the Orc-Kin's endurance. Not just physical — ideological. [b]A people who lost their homeland and chose dispersal over decline have a specific relationship with survival that manifests in combat, in negotiation, and in the long view of any situation.[/b] The Orc-Kin who joins an adventuring party has thought about how it ends. They have planned for when it goes wrong. They are usually the last one standing, and they are rarely surprised to be.

[b]The Ashen-Sworn:[/b] Not all Orc-Kin chose the path of dispersal and integration. A significant population — called the [b]Ashen-Sworn[/b] by other cultures, and something unprintable by the Dunmarim — rejected the dispersal strategy entirely and instead chose consolidation under Ashen Coil influence. Operating as warbands in the Aries March and the contested frontier zones of Elethorn, the Ashen-Sworn fight for territory, resources, and the Coil's strategic objectives simultaneously. They are not ideological converts to the Hollow Serpent's philosophy — most care nothing for Aethar's four-stage plan. They fight because the Coil offers what the Duchy Compact never did: a homeland, a banner, and an enemy they're permitted to fight openly. This makes them effective and unpredictable allies for the Coil, and a complicating factor for Orc-Kin characters who must navigate a world that assumes their Kindred's martial reputation comes entirely from the Ashen-Sworn. The Orc-Kin who fights alongside the party carries the weight of a reputation they didn't build and didn't choose.

[h2]Kindred Traits[/h2]
[b]Relentless Endurance[/b] — [i]1/Deep Attunement[/i]
When reduced to 0 Vitality, drop to 1 Vitality instead. The Orc-Kin does not fall. They decide when they fall, and this is not the moment. Usable once per Deep Attunement — the endurance is real, not infinite. After using this ability, the Orc-Kin has Resonant Discord on all Active Resonances until the end of their next Moment — the body registered the blow even if the will refused it.

[b]Savage Attack[/b] — [i]Always Active[/i]
When scoring a critical hit with a melee Strike Resonance, roll one of the attack's damage dice one additional time and add it to the damage total. The Orc-Kin's strikes carry a weight that precise technique does not fully explain — something about the commitment behind the blow compounds its effect.

[b]No Fixed Roof[/b] — [i]Always Active · Social[/i]
The Orc-Kin's stateless existence has produced comprehensive adaptability. Mastery in one of: Athletics, Intimidation, Survival, or Insight (choose at character creation — reflecting which survival skill the Orc-Kin's specific background sharpened). Additionally: Resonant Fortune on Warding Resonances against being Dread-Struck. [b]A people who chose dispersal over defeat have a specific relationship with fear.[/b] They know it. They have planned for it. It does not own them the way it owns people who have never had to make the choice.

[b]Adrenaline[/b] — [i]Always Active[/i]
When the Orc-Kin uses Combat Surge (their extra Act, available to Solar Guard and similar Paths) or takes an equivalent high-intensity action, they may move up to their full speed as part of that action without the movement counting against their normal movement for the Moment. Additionally: the Orc-Kin recovers 1 Vitality at the start of each Moment while in combat — the body prioritizes survival above everything else when the situation demands it.
