[b]Primal Essence:[/b] Alpha-Beta hybrid · Centaur-Kin who lead are Alphas; those who map are Betas — both are necessary

[h2]Lore[/h2]
The Centaur-Kin are the philosopher-nomads of the Sagittarian Steppes, and the word "philosopher" is not decorative. Their oral tradition is one of the most rigorous intellectual cultures in Sideros — sustained inquiry into cosmology, ethics, the nature of the zodiac forces, and the question of what mortality owes to the cosmos it inhabits. The difference between a Centaur-Kin philosophical debate and a Comet-Magi Conclave dispute is that the Centaur-Kin have it while running at thirty miles per hour across open grassland. [b]Movement is not incidental to Centaur-Kin thought. Movement is the condition under which thought becomes clear.[/b]

Their relationship with Lord Sagittarius is closer than any other Kindred-ruler bond. Lord Sagittarius is himself a nomadic philosopher whose Alpha-Beta hybrid nature produces genuine curiosity rather than performative inquiry. The Centaur-Kin consider him the best human ruler alive — not because he agrees with them, but because he [i]listens[/i] and then changes his mind when presented with a better argument. That capacity is, to the Centaur-Kin, the highest virtue a two-legged creature can demonstrate. Their first contact with the Avian Bird-Folk has generated intense interest: [b]here is another Kindred for whom elevation produces clarity.[/b] The philosophical exchange is ongoing and both sides are finding it unexpectedly generative.

[h2]Kindred Traits[/h2]
[b]Equine Form — Core Trait[/b] — [i]Passive[/i]
Centaur-Kin are Large-sized creatures with a movement speed of 50 ft. They cannot use standard mounts (they are the mount) but may carry one willing Small or Medium rider on their back without Endurance cost. [b]Charge[/b]: when moving at least 30 ft in a straight line before a melee Strike Resonance, deal +2d6 bludgeoning damage and the target must make a Forcefulness Warding Resonance (Threshold 14) or be knocked prone. [b]Limitations[/b]: Centaur-Kin cannot fit through doorways or passages designed for Medium creatures (most dungeon architecture). Staircases are difficult terrain. The underground encounters that other Kindreds navigate freely are genuinely constrictive for Centaur-Kin — which is part of why they rarely venture there.

[b]Stellar Cartography[/b] — [i]Passive[/i]
Centaur-Kin never get lost under an open sky and always know the precise time, date, season, and their exact geographic location from stellar observation. They have Mastery in the Aetheric Lore Discipline specifically for zodiac-related knowledge and can identify any star-aspected Aetheric Artifact by observation alone. [b]More significantly[/b]: Centaur-Kin who study the Sunken Observatory's records (requiring access, which requires a quest) can access pre-Fall stellar observations — the same type of data the Moana-kin's ancient star charts hold, from a continental rather than oceanic perspective. These two Kindreds combining their stellar records would produce the most complete picture of the pre-Fall zodiac available to any mortal culture.

[b]Running Dialogue[/b] — [i]Passive[/i]
Centaur-Kin think more clearly in motion. When they move their full movement speed in a Pulse before using a Discipline or Weaving ability, that ability has Resonant Fortune. They also have Resonant Fortune on all Active Resonances for Persuasion and Insight when the conversation takes place while moving (walking, riding, traveling) rather than seated or stationary. [b]Stationary overthinking[/b]: when forced to remain stationary for more than an hour (in a cell, staked out, in a meeting that won't end), Centaur-Kin have Resonant Discord on all Acuity and Attunement Active Resonances. They need to move to think clearly.

[b]Natural Archer[/b] — [i]Passive[/i]
Centaur-Kin with ranged weapons (bows particularly) do not suffer the normal movement penalty for firing while moving — they can move their full speed and make a ranged Strike Resonance in the same Moment without Resonant Discord. The stable platform their body provides makes this natural. [b]Additionally[/b]: once per Pulse, a Centaur-Kin may make one ranged Strike Resonance as an Instinct rather than an Act — not an extra attack, but the ability to respond to movement with an arrow faster than most Kindreds can decide to aim. This reflects the Steppe hunting tradition where prey is rarely stationary.

[h2]Playing this Kindred[/h2]
The Centaur-Kin's central experience is being magnificently suited to open environments and genuinely constrained by closed ones. The Large size, 50-ft speed, and Charge ability make open terrain combat extraordinary. The inability to use standard dungeon architecture is a real limitation that creates natural party dynamics — the Centaur-Kin waits outside, covering exits, while smaller Kindreds go through the door. The Running Dialogue mechanic is a character note as much as a rule: Centaur-Kin are at their best in motion. A stationary Centaur-Kin is mildly impaired. One who has been running for half an hour before a difficult conversation will outperform their stationary self in every way.
