[h2]A World of Warring Zodiacs[/h2]
Sideros — known in ancient and scholarly texts by its formal name, [i]Astrolan[/i] — is a world without a single cosmic law. There is no universal pantheon, no one true north. The laws of the cosmos are [b]regional[/b]: each culture is a living testament to its own zodiac, and the truths these traditions hold are not merely different but fundamentally incompatible. Travel far enough and the rules of reality themselves begin to read differently. Every compass points to a different true north, and every one of them is correct where it stands.

[h2]The Pantheonic Fall[/h2]
Four thousand years ago the twelve zodiac gods governed the cosmos as the Pantheonic Council. When the thirteenth force corrupted and shattered the order, the surviving twelve did not simply die — rather than dissolve, they broke themselves into vast Aetheric presences. What they left behind were the [b]thirteen Legendary Beasts[/b], the undivided remnants of their truth, and a divine signal fragmented into [b]thirteen cultural interpretations[/b] across the world's peoples.

The Beasts no longer govern. But they still recognise mortals whose soul-frequency resonates with their domain — and a person so recognised is changed by it.

[h2]The Thirteen Signs[/h2]
Sideros reckons by thirteen signs: the twelve familiar constellations and a thirteenth, [b]Ophiuchus[/b] — the sign with no month, whose sacred days fall in the brief interval between the months of Sagittarius and Capricorn, marked only by those trained to watch for them. A character's zodiac is not a belief they hold. It is the cosmic fact they were born inside.

[h2]The Aether and Weaving[/h2]
Magic in Sideros is the art of [b]Weaving[/b]: a practitioner weaves a [b]Working[/b], drawing on opened [b]Aetheric Channels[/b]. The anchor truth of the world's magic is simple — [i]the Loom weaves fate; practitioners weave the Aether. The same act, at different scales.[/i] Magic and craft sit side by side here: in a world where a Weaver can lift a stone, civilisations did not always need to invent the crane. The result is an Iron Age and High-Medieval world threaded through with the impossible, from clockwork empires to low-tech cultures of immense magical depth.

[h2]The Loom of Fate and Fated Bonds[/h2]
Every living soul carries a [b]Fate Number[/b], a measure of cosmic resonance tracked by the patient, near-invisible network known as the [b]Loom of Fate[/b]. Where two or more souls resonate closely enough, a [b]Fated Bond[/b] may form between them — rare by design, indifferent to alignment or allegiance, and binding in ways the bonded come to understand only over time. Bonds form between two and five people; they are not chosen so much as recognised.

[h2]The Central Threat[/h2]
Against this fractured cosmos stands the [b]Ashen Coil[/b] — not a political faction but a cosmic heresy. Where every other power in Sideros lives inside one zodiac's truth, the Coil has stepped outside the warring zodiacs entirely. At their head is [b]Aethar, the Serpent Bearer[/b], who believes the only mercy left to a sundered world is to unmake it and begin again. He is wrong — but he is not a fool, and he cannot simply be argued out of it. The work of opposing him falls, as it so often does in Sideros, to the few whose choices tip a world balanced on a knife's edge.

[h2]The Four Senses of Sideros[/h2]
The setting is built to evoke four things at the table:
[ul]
[li][b]Wonder[/b] — every horizon offers a new truth.[/li]
[li][b]Agency[/b] — the world sits on a knife's edge, and individuals tip the scales.[/li]
[li][b]Aspiration[/b] — you can be anything, love anyone; heroism appears in unexpected places.[/li]
[li][b]Hope[/b] — connection and courage can forge a better future even in a broken world.[/li]
[/ul]

[i]The Loom weaves all. Your thread is your own.[/i]
