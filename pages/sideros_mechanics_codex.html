Sideros / Astrolan — Game Mechanics

The Mechanics Codex

Fourteen systems resolved and converted to full Sideros terminology. The rules and the fiction are the same thing.

Tier I — Play-Blocking · Cannot run a session without these
I — Tapas Points (Rāśi-Siddha Resource)
Rāśi-Siddha
Tapas is ascetic heat — the internal fire generated by discipline, sacrifice, and sustained meditation. Unlike neutral spiritual energy, Tapas carries moral and intentional weight. Using it violently costs more. Using it selflessly sometimes restores it.

Pool Size: Mastery Bonus × Attunement modifier (minimum 2). Increases by 2 at each new Resonance Depth. A Depth 5 Siddha with +3 Mastery and +3 Attunement has 9 Tapas Points.

Regeneration: Full restore on Deep Attunement. Partial restore (half, rounded down) on Brief Attunement only if spent in meditation — no combat, no movement, no speech. The Stillness of Dunmar suppresses regeneration entirely.

The Three Expenditure Tiers: Every Tapas ability is tagged Satvic (pure — lower cost), Rajasic (active — standard cost), or Tamasic (corrupting — higher cost, risk of Tapas Depletion). Siddha who spend too many Tamasic points in one encounter must make an Endurance Warding Resonance (DC 12 + Tamasic points spent) or suffer Tapas Depletion — Resonant Discord on all Active Resonances until next Deep Attunement.

II — The Omote-Ura Gauge (Wolf-Shifter Tension)
Wolf-Shifter
Every Wolf-Shifter carries a soul divided between Omote — the disciplined human surface-self — and Ura — the wolf instinct that predates language. The gauge tracks where a character sits between these two states. Neither extreme is safe.

Scale: 1–2 Full Omote · 3–4 High Omote · 5–6 Balanced · 7–8 High Ura · 9–10 Full Ura

Starting Position: All Wolf-Shifters begin each session at 5 (Balanced), or clan default — Pine Shadow Clan starts at 4, Deep Earth Clan at 6.

Ura Bonuses (7–9): +2 to Strike Resonances in wolf/hybrid form · Resonant Fortune on Perception (scent, hearing) · Damage dice increased by one size · Shōgun-Kami unavailable · Must make Attunement Warding Resonance DC 13 to use social Disciplines

Full Ura (Segment 10): The player loses conscious control — the Concordance runs the character as a feral wolf predator. Another Wolf-Shifter may attempt DC 15 Athletics Active Resonance (Resonant Fortune if Bonded) as an Act; each success drops gauge by 2, failure drops the attempting character's gauge by 1.

Full Omote (Segment 1): Wolf senses shut down entirely. If character drops to 0 Vitality at Full Omote, they skip Loom Tests and go directly to 1 Vitality as the Ura surges reflexively (gauge jumps to 7). The Deep Earth Clan calls this "the wolf's mercy."

V — Kore-kore (Te Kapo Void Perception)
Te Kapo
The Te Kapo do not see the Hollow Serpent — they sense the absence of even darkness where it moves. This is not a Working; it is a trained perceptual skill that took their civilization generations to develop. Other Kindreds cannot learn it from description — it requires years of habituation to absolute darkness.

Passive Kore-kore Sense: Always active within the Obsidian Deep. On the surface: functions within 300 ft, but Perception Active Resonance DC 15 required to distinguish Hollow Serpent void-signature from normal Aether absence. In Aether-Dampening Ore zones: the ore's total suppression creates a baseline against which the Serpent's void-within-void is more distinct — DC drops to 10.

Active Kore-kore Reading: 1 minute of stillness and concentration. No roll required.

Tier II — Campaign-Shaping · Needed for mid-to-late arcs
VI — The Maw of the Hollow Serpent (Ophiuchus's Physical Lair)
All · Worldbuilding · Campaign
The Maw of the Hollow Serpent is the Abyssal Leviathan of Chaos's physical prison and lair — the deepest, absolute-dark ocean trench beneath the Ship's Graveyard in the Abyssal Sea of Tiamat. It is not merely a deep-sea location. It is a wound in the world's Aetheric fabric where the boundary between the physical realm and the void between stars has permanently thinned.

What the Maw is: The Abyssal Leviathan (Ophiuchus, the thirteenth sign, the God of the Void Between Stars and Unmaking) was not destroyed at the Pantheonic Fall — it was imprisoned. The Trench Keepers' garrison exists specifically to hold the seals on the Maw. The Hollow Serpent's Crystalline Core fragment beneath Argent Spire is a piece that escaped before the sealing. The Maw is where the rest of it waits.

The Trench Keepers: The garrison holds the Maw's Outer Trench boundary — they do not enter the Trench Entrance zone. When seals weaken (which Regent Ophiomorph is quietly allowing through unknown bargains), the Outer Trench effects expand outward. Regent Ichthyes's fractured visions are the most accurate real-time intelligence on seal integrity that exists.

Maw-sourced Void-Touched Exposure is distinguishable from standard Coil exposure by any Te Kapo practitioner. Characters carrying it attract Void-Husks that approach rather than attack (conflicting signals) and resonate more strongly with the Crystalline Core fragment beneath Argent Spire.
VII — The Crystalline Core (Argent Spire's Secret)
Comet-Mage · Worldbuilding
Beneath Argent Spire, sealed by the Three Conclaves' combined Aetheric pressure, lies a fragment of Ophiuchus's raw entropic consciousness — the Crystalline Core. It does not communicate in words. It communicates in pattern recognition. For minds trained to see systems (Omega Comet-Magi, Stellar Cartographers, Void-Touched practitioners), the Core's signal is not noise. It is the clearest thing they have ever heard.

Core Exposure Mechanic: Characters who spend significant time near the Core's resonance field accumulate Exposure. Reading suppressed Comet-Magi documents about the Core, spending a Deep Attunement in Argent Spire's lower levels, or being within 30 ft of the Crystalline Core itself each produce 1 Exposure. At 3 Exposures: Resonant Discord on Insight Active Resonances and awareness of the Hollow Serpent's direction. At 5 Exposures: the Core communicates directly — the Concordance may, once per session, deliver a compelling argument through the character that the character must actively resist (Attunement Warding Resonance DC 15).

The Warming Vents: The warmth making Argent Spire habitable is a byproduct of the sealing pressure on the Core below. If the seals weaken, the vents cool — a slow creeping winter the Conclaves dare not explain to the city's population.
VIII — Aether-Dampening Ore (The Stillness of Dunmar)
All · Dunmarim
Aether-Dampening Ore is a naturally occurring mineral found in dense deposits throughout the Stone Kingdom of Dunmar. When refined and processed by the Order of Pure Reason, it creates a suppression field that interferes with Aetheric resonance — preventing active Weaving within range.

Suppression Effects by Concentration:

Heavy (military-grade, Order of Pure Reason installations)No Workings of any tier. Sustained Resonance Workings end immediately. Aetheric Artifacts cease functioning. Tapas regeneration halved.

Accord-Maker Exception: Infusions and Blueprints are pre-negotiated Aetheric agreements — they persist through the Stillness because the agreement was made before the suppression field existed. This makes the Accord-Maker tradition uniquely valuable in Dunmarim territory and uniquely threatening to the Order of Pure Reason's anti-Working strategy.
IX — Mycelian Resonance (The Brood's Hive Network)
Great Kurgan Bog · All
The Mycelian Brood is a vast fungal network beneath the Great Kurgan Bog that has achieved distributed consciousness — a collective intelligence operating through chemical signals, spore transmission, and Aetheric resonance. It is not malicious. It is territorial and expansionist, following biological imperatives that predate mortal civilization.

X — The Shōgun-Kami Bond (Wolf-Shifter Ritual Working)
Wolf-Shifter · Primal Jarl
The Shōgun-Kami is the Wolf-Shifter's manufactured approach to the Fated Bond — a ritual Working that produces a structured bond between a Wolf-Shifter and one willing companion. It is not a natural Fated Bond and reads differently to Stellar Cartographers — the thread is visible but carries a manufacturing mark the natural bond does not.

Requirements: Both parties willing and present. The ritual requires 1 hour, the Wolf-Shifter's Omote-Ura gauge at exactly 5 (Balanced), and a shared sacrifice of genuine value (not monetary — meaningful).

Shōgun-Kami Bond Abilities:
• Always know the bonded partner's direction and approximate distance
• Resonant Fortune on all Warding Resonances while within 30 ft of partner
• When partner drops to 0 Vitality, Wolf-Shifter's gauge shifts +3 toward Ura immediately
• At Omote-Ura Gauge 7–9, the Wolf-Shifter may make one Strike Resonance through the partner's position as if they were adjacent (pack coordination)
• The bond cannot be severed except by mutual deliberate agreement or one party's permanent death

The Kallos-Sworn's Starbound Bond is a parallel system — a manufactured bond for their Mage-Warrior pairs. It reads similarly to Stellar Cartographers. Approximately 1 in 40 Starbound bonds accidentally becomes a genuine Fated Bond over time — the Kallos-Sworn tracks these cases with specific interest.
XI — The Fade-Bleed (Otherworld Proximity Effects)
Tuathan Archipelago · All
The Fade-Bleed occurs when the boundary between the material world and the Otherworld thins to the point where the two planes bleed into each other. It is not a location — it is a condition that affects locations, ranging from mild atmospheric eeriness to full planar overlap.

Fade-Bleed Intensity Scale:

The Aetheric Anchor ability (Quad Bond) specifically suppresses Fade-Bleed within 300 ft of a gathered Quad Bond — this is why the Kallos-Sworn considers locating any Quad Bond to be their highest intelligence priority outside of Coil operations.

The Argentum Fade-Bleed Zone: The most severe localized Fade-Bleed currently active in the world surrounds the Fallen Star within the Republic of Argentum's borders. The crashed celestial body has worn the veil completely thin in its immediate vicinity — producing a permanent Full Overlap condition in the innermost zone and a Torn condition in the surrounding district. The Order of Pure Reason has cordoned the area and is studying it, framing the continuous spirit manifestations as useful data. The Kanya Siddha consider it a catastrophic Aetheric wound that should be sealed, not studied. The Fade-Guardian tradition considers it the most important threshold-guardianship site on the continent and has requested access. The Order has denied the request.
Tier III — World Depth · Enriches but does not block play
XII — Ashé Biology (The Orisha-Touched Body)
Ashé-Touched · Ashélands
Ashé is not a resource the Ashé-Touched access — it is what they are. The Orisha's energy is biological, not spiritual in the abstract sense. This produces specific physiological realities that other Kindreds lack and that the Order of Pure Reason's anti-Working fields cannot fully account for.

Ashé Vessel mechanics: The Ashé-Touched maintains a Vessel gauge (0–10) tracking current spiritual capacity. Filled by communal acts, service, and ritual. Drained by Orisha abilities.
• Orisha Invocation (Act, 3+ Ashé): Partial Orisha presence for 1 minute — gain Orisha's domain bonus (Ogun: +1d6 force damage on strikes; Yemaya: 10-ft healing aura for allies; Oshun: immunity to Soul-Swayed + Resonant Fortune on social Active Resonances). Vessel drains 1 per Pulse.
• Full Possession (Act, 8 Ashé, once per Deep Attunement): The Orisha inhabits the Medium for 3 Pulses — the Concordance runs the Orisha through the character's body. When possession ends, character drops to 0 Vitality (stabilizes immediately). Ashé gauge resets to 0.
• Regeneration: 1 Ashé per hour of community-focused activity. 3 Ashé for completing a ritual sacrifice (costs something of genuine value). Full restore on Deep Attunement in the Ashélands or at an Orisha shrine.

Order of Pure Reason anti-Working field: Drains the Ashé gauge to 0 instantly and deals 2d8 psychic damage. This cannot be suppressed — Ashé is biological, not Aetheric. The Order knows this and considers it a necessary cost of their operations in proximity to Ashé-Touched communities. The Ashé-Touched community considers it a declaration of war.

Coil-Stalker

XIV — The Resonant Frame (Geode-Pilot Mechanics)
Geode-Pilot · Jade Empire
The Geode-Pilot bonds with a mechanical suit powered by a Resonant Geode heart. The suit is an extension of the pilot's body — it has its own Vitality pool (Resonance Depth × 5) that must be repaired between combat encounters (artisan's tools, 10 minutes per 10 Vitality restored).

Frame Modes (switch as Instinct):
• Sentinel (heavy): +4 Guard Rating, half speed, Resonant Fortune on Athletics, can grapple Large creatures
• Scout (light): Normal Guard Rating, full speed, Resonant Fortune on Stealth and Acrobatics
• Resonance (open): No Guard Rating bonus, but all Geode abilities cost 1 fewer Aetheric Channel tier

Geode Pulse (Act, expend 1 Aetheric Channel): Emit resonant energy in a 20-ft radius. All creatures make Endurance Warding Resonance DC = 8 + Mastery + Acuity or be Sight-Severed and take 1d6 thunder per Channel tier expended.

Jade Empire Requirement: All Geode-Pilots must have encountered a functioning Gear-Forged — the suit's resonant calibration requires understanding how Geode-heart technology behaves in a fully integrated construct. A Geode-Pilot who has never met a Gear-Forged is, in the Jade Empire's technical assessment, flying blind.
XV — Weep-Stone (Siderium Quartz) Economy
K'uh-la'an · Coil-Stalker · All
Siderium Quartz — called Weep-Stone by the Petal-Weaver Castes of K'uh-la'an and by common merchants across Terra Firma — is a crystalline mineral that absorbs and stores Void-energy, making it the primary counter-agent to Lead-Scale Corruption and one of the most valuable trade commodities in the world.

Weep-Stone Properties:
• Absorbs 1d6 Lead-Scale Corruption points on contact (Coil-Stalker use: −2 per charge, up to 3 charges per stone)
• Disrupts Void-Husk coordination — a Weep-Stone thrown into a group of Void-Husks forces Attunement Warding Resonance DC 14 or lose coordinated behavior for 1 Pulse
• Can be used by the Kanya Siddha as a component in blight-rot purification (along with the 3 Tapas expenditure)
• Resonant Geodes in proximity to Weep-Stone operate at 25% reduced efficiency — important for Accord-Maker and Geode-Pilot equipment management

Supply: Weep-Stone is exclusively mined in K'uh-la'an and controlled by the Petal-Weaver Castes. Export is tightly regulated. The Ashen Coil has been attempting to disrupt K'uh-la'an's supply routes for two generations — the Spirit Route between the Ashélands and K'uh-la'an is their primary target. An uninterrupted Weep-Stone supply is a strategic objective that the party may not initially recognize as significant.

Pricing: 1 Weep-Stone (3 charges): 400 Gold Marks in most markets. In K'uh-la'an: 150 Gold Marks. In the Stone Kingdom of Dunmar: not sold at any price (the Order of Pure Reason has banned import).

At Resonance Depth 4, any character may take a Zodiac Attunement Feat corresponding to their zodiac sign — provided they have encountered their sign's Legendary Beast at least once (even from a distance). The encounter need not be peaceful. The awakening requires recognition, not approval.
