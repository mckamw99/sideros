Sideros / Astrolan — Character Creation Step VI

Origins

Where you came from before the Path found you. Your Origin gives you Disciplines, equipment, and a specific relationship with the world that no amount of training can replicate.

Scholarly Origins produced characters who entered the world through institutions of knowledge — anywhere that preserved, studied, or transmitted understanding across generations. These characters know how to find information, how to evaluate it, and how to use it. They tend to underestimate how much they don't know.

Comet-Magi Initiate
Scholarly · Argent Spire

You trained at Argent Spire — not long enough to reach a significant Circle, but long enough to absorb the Conclaves' framework and leave with something they didn't intend you to keep.

Discipline Masteries
Aetheric Lore, History
Tool / Language
Calligrapher's supplies · One of: Old Kallosian, Eldren script, or Conclave notation
Origin Feature — Formulation Fragment
You retain a suppressed Comet-Magi Formulation — a Working the Conclaves consider too dangerous to teach formally. Once per Deep Attunement, attempt to express it: Working Threshold check DC 15. On success: produce a 2nd-tier Working effect (agreed at character creation). On failure: the fragment destabilizes, dealing 1d6 psychic damage.
Equipment
Scholar's pack, Comet-Magi initiate's robe, personal Formulation Ledger with notes, 15 Gold Marks
Personality Traits
I quote from texts most people have never heard of — and I know this is irritating. I collect small facts the way other people collect wounds.
Ideals
Knowledge is the only thing that outlasts catastrophe. The truth is worth knowing even when it costs you peace.
Bond
A mentor at Argent Spire taught me something I'm still working out.
Flaw
I underestimate things I can't quantify.
Hooks
  • Why did you leave before completing your training?
  • Which Conclave did you align with — and do you still agree with their position?
  • What is in the suppressed Formulation fragment you retained?
Siddha Acolyte
Scholarly · Temple Tradition

You were raised in or drawn to one of the Twelve Scattered Temples — not as a full Siddha, but as a student who absorbed the tradition's rhythms, mantras, and cosmological framework.

Discipline Masteries
Religion (Siddha tradition), Medicine
Tool / Language
Herbalist's supplies · Classical language of your temple's sign-culture
Origin Feature — Temple Blessing
Your sign's Temple has acknowledged your training. Once per Deep Attunement, spend 10 minutes in meditation to invoke your sign's temple blessing — a minor Working effect tied to your sign's domain requiring no Aetheric Channel. Concordance determines the specific effect (Mesha = weapon enhancement; Kanya = remove Corrupted; Vrishchika = read emotional history of touched object; etc.).
Equipment
Prayer beads, healer's kit (10 uses), traveler's clothes, your sign's temple's foundational mantra text, 10 Gold Marks
Personality Traits
I find stillness in crisis — which unnerves people who are not still. I treat silence as a complete answer to difficult questions.
Ideals
Discipline is the only freedom that cannot be taken. The mantra is more honest than the practitioner.
Bond
My teacher's final lesson was unfinished when they died.
Flaw
I dismiss practical problems that could be solved with less dignity.
Hooks
  • Which temple did you train at — and why did you leave?
  • Do you still practice the mantras? What do they mean to you now?
  • What does the temple want from you that you haven't delivered?
Loom Analyst

Stellar Observer
Scholarly · Celestial Study

You spent years watching the sky from a tower, a hillside, or the back of a cart moving between observation points. You think in patterns that span centuries. Most conversations feel short.

Discipline Masteries
Aetheric Lore, Perception
Tool / Language
Navigator's tools (star charts, astrolabe) · One additional language
Origin Feature — Celestial Memory
You have memorized the current position of every major celestial body. Under open sky: always know the exact time, date, season, and geographic position — no tools required. Underground or indoors: know precisely how long you've been away from the sky. The Centaur-Kin recognize this as a lesser form of their Stellar Cartography — they are not wrong. Additionally: Resonant Fortune on Aetheric Lore Active Resonances related to astronomical or zodiac timing.
Equipment
Navigator's tools, a personal star log spanning several years of observations, traveler's clothes, 12 Gold Marks
Personality Traits
I think in longer patterns than most conversations allow. I note celestial events even when no one asked me to.

Bond

Flaw

Hooks

  • Your star log contains an anomaly from six months ago that you haven't been able to explain.

Merchant Origins produced characters who moved through the world's economic layer — not necessarily as wealthy traders, but as people who understood how value flows, where it concentrates, and who controls its movement. These characters know people in useful places and understand that most conflicts are ultimately about resources.

Silk Road Guild Operative
Merchant · Trans-Continental Trade

The Silk Road Guild officially connects every major trading hub in the world. Unofficially, it also moves information, people who need to disappear, and goods that don't appear on manifests. You know which version of the Guild you worked for.

Discipline Masteries
Persuasion, Deception
Tool / Language
Navigator's tools · Trader's Common (trade pidgin, understood at all major ports)
Origin Feature — Guild Contacts
In any city or major trading post, find a Guild contact within 1d4 hours. The contact can provide: current commodity prices; local political summary; one night's safe house; hiring intelligence. The contact operates on mutual benefit — not loyalty. Asking for something that risks them costs a favor. The Concordance tracks what favors you owe.
Equipment
Guild signet ring, merchant's scale, letter of credit worth 50 Gold Marks (redeemable at any Guild office), fine clothes, 25 Gold Marks
Personality Traits
I price everything, including things that aren't for sale. I form alliances before I form friendships — both can become the other.
Ideals
A fair deal is one both parties walk away from having gained something. Movement is safety — anything stationary can be surrounded.
Bond
Someone on the route still owes me a debt I intend to collect.
Flaw
I trust negotiation over direct action past the point of usefulness.
Hooks
  • Are you still a Guild operative — or did you leave? If you left, did they let you?
  • Drava Nullsong used Silk Road Guild infrastructure to build the Fang of the Whisper. Do you know something about that?
  • What was the last shipment you moved that you've been trying to forget?
Dunmarim Master-Smith
Merchant · Stone Kingdom Craft

You trained under a Dunmarim master-smith — the most rigorous craft tradition in the world. You may not be Dunmarim yourself, but you spent enough years in the Stone Kingdom to absorb their approach to material and work. Your hands know things your mind hasn't caught up with yet.

Discipline Masteries
Athletics, History (material culture)
Tool / Language
Smith's tools, Mason's tools · Dunmarim (spoken and written)
Origin Feature — Master's Hand
When you craft using your Mastered tools, the result is always one quality tier higher than the materials would normally allow. Additionally: identify the origin, approximate age, and quality of any worked metal or stone object in 1 minute. This includes Aetheric Artifacts made of metal or stone — you know they've been worked even if you don't know how.
Equipment
Smith's tools, a masterwork personal weapon or tool (worth twice standard price), Dunmarim letter of reference, 15 Gold Marks
Personality Traits
I respect work and have strong opinions about work done badly. I see the construction of everything I encounter.
Ideals
Good work is its own argument. Bad work is too. The craft outlasts the craftsperson — make it worth outlasting you.
Bond
I made something for someone important. They died before receiving it.
Flaw
I take longer than the situation allows when quality matters to me.
Hooks
  • Who was your master-smith, and why did you leave their workshop?
  • The Dunmarim are suspicious of anyone who takes their craft knowledge out of the Stone Kingdom. Are they right to be?
  • Your masterwork piece — what is it, and what does it mean to you?
Port Damaris Shadow-Council Agent
Merchant · Underground Economy

Port Damaris runs on a dual economy — the legitimate merchant layer the Duchy Compact recognizes, and the Shadow-Council layer that actually controls the city. You worked for the latter. You know how the city really works, which is not the same as how it appears to work.

Discipline Masteries

Tool / Language
Thieves' tools · Port Damaris cant (coded trading language, recognized in any underground economy)
Origin Feature — City Infrastructure
In any city you've spent at least a week in, you know its hidden infrastructure. Upon arriving in a new city, after 24 hours of observation, identify: one safe house, one official susceptible to bribery (and their price), one black market contact. The Concordance provides these as specific NPCs — not abstractions.
Equipment
Thieves' tools, dark common clothes, Port Damaris Shadow-Council token, 30 Gold Marks (mixed denominations, nothing traceable)
Personality Traits
I notice exits before I notice decor. I don't confirm or deny things unless I have to.
Ideals
The law protects people who can afford it. Understanding someone is not the same as trusting them.
Bond

Flaw
I assume the worst about institutions before they've had a chance to disappoint me.
Hooks
  • Why did you leave Port Damaris — or are you still technically employed?
  • The Shadow-Council doesn't like former agents who know too much. What do you know that makes you a liability?
  • The Lunar Queen may be operating in Port Damaris's economy. Do you know something?
Far-Trade Factor
Merchant · Cross-Cultural Intermediary

You managed trade relationships across cultural lines — you know how different peoples value different things and why. You have spent enough time with at least three Kindred cultures that you understand their social assumptions intuitively. NPCs from Kindreds you've traded with start at Friendly disposition rather than Neutral. Hooks

Coil-Adjacent Operative
Shadow · Ashen Coil (Former)

You worked for an organization that was — you now realize — either directly part of the Ashen Coil or one of its front operations. You didn't know at the time. Or you knew and chose not to look too closely. Either version of this story has consequences.

Discipline Masteries
Stealth, Deception
Tool / Language
Thieves' tools or Poisoner's kit (your choice) · Coil operational cipher
Origin Feature — Coil Pattern Recognition
Resonant Fortune on Insight and Investigation Active Resonances when investigating Coil activity, agents, or operations. Additionally: once per session, ask the Concordance whether a specific person, organization, or location has any Coil connection — answered based on what your operational knowledge would realistically reveal. Your knowledge has gaps and dates — this is not omniscience.
Equipment
Dark travel clothes, forged identity document (one alias), a Coil dead-letter drop location (Concordance decides if it's still monitored), 20 Gold Marks
Personality Traits
I understand the appeal of the Coil better than most people who fight it — this is useful and uncomfortable. I am careful about what I say I believe in, having believed in something that turned out to be wrong.
Ideals
I am not what I did. I am what I do next. The Coil's logic was coherent. Its conclusion was wrong. That distinction matters.
Bond
Someone I recruited is still inside. I owe them an attempt at extraction.
Flaw
I am waiting for the people I travel with to discover what I was and leave. Some part of me thinks they should.
Hooks
  • What was the operation that made you realize what you were actually working for?
  • The Coil doesn't retire operatives. What is the current status of your relationship with them?
  • Does anyone in your current party know this about you?
