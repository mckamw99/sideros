Sideros / Astrolan — Companion Systems

The Beast Companion System

The companion was not tamed. Its Fate Number drew it toward you. What it becomes depends on what you do together.

In Sideros, a Beast Companion is not a pet and not a tool. It is a bonded partner — a creature whose Fate Number falls close enough to its partner's that the Loom of Fate arranged their meeting. The companion didn't choose its partner in any conscious sense. Neither did the partner. The Loom wove them together because their combined frequency produces something neither could produce alone. What that something is depends entirely on what they become to each other.

Every Beast Companion has a zodiac sign — determined by the Concordance at the moment of first encounter, reflecting the creature's fundamental nature. A raven encountered at a crossroads might be Gemini-aspected. A wolf who appears during a winter storm might be Capricorn-aspected. A serpent found guarding a Ceque Line node is almost certainly Scorpio. The sign is not cosmetic. It is the entire architecture of what the companion will become.

Acquiring a Companion

A companion is not purchased, summoned, or created. It is encountered. The Concordance introduces potential companions as organic world events — a creature that behaves unusually, that appears repeatedly, that shows no fear when it should. The player recognizes the pattern. The encounter becomes a relationship. The relationship deepens until both parties acknowledge what has been happening between them.

The Fate Number Check
When a player character has a significant encounter with a creature and the Concordance determines it may be a companion candidate, roll 1d100 for the creature's Fate Number. If within 10 points of the character's Number — a natural Paired Bond range — the creature is a companion candidate. The Concordance does not reveal this roll. The player discovers it through the relationship's texture.
The Zodiac Determination
The Concordance assigns the companion's zodiac sign based on: the creature's species (a bear is naturally Taurus-aspected; an eagle naturally Sagittarius; a spider naturally Scorpio), the circumstances of meeting (a companion met during a Leo-aspected event may carry Leo resonance), or a d12 roll if neither applies. The sign is fixed at first encounter and does not change.
The Acknowledgment
The bond becomes active when both the character and the creature have made a deliberate choice — the character has named the creature and the creature has remained when it could have left. This is not a Working, not a ritual, not a roll. It is a narrative event. The Concordance marks it in the campaign record. The Loom marks it in the Fate Number system — the companion's Number is now linked to the character's in the world's ledger.
Base Companion Statistics

All companions begin at Stage I regardless of species. The zodiac sign determines the evolution path. The species determines cosmetic form and narrative flavor — a Scorpio-sign companion might be a spider, a scorpion, a snake, a raven, or a shadow-cat, all following the same mechanical tree.

The Companion Is Not The Partner's Property
The companion has its own Primal Essence (determined by the Concordance — usually Beta or Omega, occasionally Alpha). It has preferences, fears, strong responses to specific people and situations. It can be displeased. A companion that is consistently mistreated, put in danger recklessly, or used purely as a tool will withdraw its bond — dropping to Stage I regardless of evolution progress, until the relationship is genuinely repaired. The Concordance tracks this. The Loom tracks it. The companion's behavior makes it visible before the mechanical consequence arrives.

The companion's Fated Bond with its partner operates on the same tier system as all Fated Bonds — but with one critical difference. Because companion bonds are always naturally formed (no manufactured ritual, no Starbound process), they carry full Loom recognition. The Stellar Cartographer reads them as clean. The Fang of the Whisper treats them as a legitimate target — a companion bond is a vulnerability, and the Coil has learned to exploit it.

Bond Mechanics During Combat
Action Economy
The companion acts on the partner's Moment using the partner's Instinct. It may: move its speed; make one Strike Resonance or use one sign ability. This costs nothing else — the Instinct is the companion's, not an additional resource. A character with no other Instinct use still has this available. A character who needs their Instinct for something else must choose — the companion waits.
Companion Down
When the companion reaches 0 Vitality: it is Loom-Suspended (unconscious, stable — companions do not make Loom Tests, they simply stabilize). The partner takes psychic damage (see Shared Fate). The companion can be revived with any healing Working or spending the partner's Act to tend to it — restoring 1 Vitality. Companions cannot permanently die unless the partner is also killed, or the bond is deliberately severed.
Independent Positioning
Between the partner's Moments, the companion may move up to half its speed as a free action — repositioning without the partner's direction. It does not attack without the Instinct command. At Stage III Evolution, the companion gains full autonomous movement and may act without the partner's Instinct on turns when the partner is Resonance-Shattered or Loom-Suspended.
Pack Tactics
When the companion and partner both target the same creature in the same Pulse, both have Resonant Fortune on their Strike Resonances against that creature. This is not a special ability — it is the natural result of coordinated pressure from two bonded combatants. The creature cannot fully defend against simultaneous attacks whose timing it cannot predict.
Void-Touched Companions
If the Concordance rolls 13, 66, or 99 for the companion's Fate Number, the companion is Void-Touched. This is extraordinarily rare and should be treated as a significant campaign event. A Void-Touched companion: has all the standard Void-Touched bond amplification effects; can sense the Hollow Serpent's movements in its vicinity without training; and is actively sought by the Ashen Coil — Serath of the Closed Circle would consider a Void-Touched companion a high-priority intelligence asset. The Concordance should use this sparingly and deliberately. It is not a mechanical bonus. It is a story.

Evolution is not earned through accumulated experience. It is triggered by specific moments — events that deepen the bond, test it, or demonstrate something fundamental about the relationship. The Concordance tracks these. When the required feats for a stage are completed, the evolution occurs during the next Deep Attunement — the companion emerges from sleep changed. The partner always knows it has happened before they fully wake. The bond registers the shift.

On Feat Tracking
The Concordance tracks evolution feats, not the player. Players may know generally that their companion is evolving — the bond's texture changes subtly before a formal evolution occurs, and attentive players will notice behavioral shifts in the companion. But they do not have a checklist. The evolution feels like it happens because of the relationship, not because requirements were met. If a player asks directly whether their companion is close to evolving, the Concordance may answer honestly — but the answer should be given in terms of the relationship's state, not feat counts.

Each zodiac sign produces a distinct evolution path — three stages that trace the companion's progression from bonded animal to something the world does not have a word for yet. The species is flavor. The sign is structure. Click a sign to see its complete tree.

Any character may bond with a companion — the Fate Number proximity is sufficient. But certain Paths have deeper mechanical integration with the companion system, either through existing features that synergize or through specific abilities that enhance companion functionality.

Void-Hunter — The Beast Companion Calling
Primary Integration
The Void-Hunter's Fang of the Resistance Calling was noted as only a partial Beast Master equivalent — it became an intelligence network rather than an animal companion. With the Beast Companion system, a Void-Hunter can now take a companion alongside the intelligence network, creating the closest analog to the D&D Beast Master.

Void-Hunter + Companion specific rules: The Void-Hunter's Void Quarry designation extends to the companion — both the Void-Hunter and the companion have Resonant Fortune on Strike Resonances against the current Quarry. The companion gains +1d8 bonus damage against the Quarry (matching the Void-Hunter's own bonus). Additionally: the companion's Evolution Feat triggers count one level easier for Void-Hunters — they need 2 Stage I feats for Stage II (rather than 3), and 1 Stage II feat for Stage III (rather than 2). The Void-Hunter's lifestyle produces the right conditions for rapid bond deepening.
Primal Accord — Companion and Aspect Interaction
Deep Integration
An Accord-Walker with a companion creates a unique situation — the companion's zodiac sign and the Accord-Walker's Beast Resonance pools may overlap or diverge, producing interesting tactical combinations.

Same-sign companion: If the companion's zodiac sign matches one of the Accord-Walker's known Beasts, the companion's presence in combat generates +1 Beast Resonance Point for that Beast's pool at the start of each Pulse (maximum 1 per Pulse). The companion's proximity strengthens the Accord-Walker's access to that Beast's frequency.

Different-sign companion: When the Accord-Walker manifests a Full Aspect of their Primary Beast and the companion is within 30 ft, the companion gains a cosmetic aspect of that Beast as well — not mechanically significant, but the Concordance describes it. The frequencies harmonize visibly.

Keeper Calling + Companion: A Keeper Accord-Walker's companion also gains the Ecosystem Reading benefit in the Keeper's chosen terrain — the companion becomes an additional sensory node in the Keeper's environmental awareness network.
Primal Jarl — Beast-Soul Calling Companion
Calling Integration
The Beast-Soul Calling (Arcadian tradition) was designed with the companion system in mind — a Primal Jarl who walks the Beast-Soul path has a natural relationship with the animals their aspects connect them to.

Beast-Soul + Companion: When the Primal Jarl enters their Wrath State and activates a Beast Aspect, their companion gains the same Minor Aspect effects for the Wrath's duration — not full transformation, but the claws or hide or sense-enhancement associated with the aspect. The companion and Jarl briefly share the same Aetheric frequency.

Bestial Soul ability: The Bestial Soul Calling feature (communication with beasts while in Wrath) extends to Stage I and II companions of other players in the party — the Jarl in Wrath State can communicate with all bonded animals in the area, not just their own.
Accord-Maker — Homunculus vs. Companion
System Clarification
The Accord-Maker's Homunculus Servant and a Beast Companion are mechanically distinct and can coexist — an Accord-Maker may have both simultaneously.

The Homunculus uses an Infusion slot and is a constructed being with no Fate Number and no zodiac sign. It does not evolve. It does not have a Fated Bond. It is a tool — an excellent one, but a tool.

A Beast Companion is a living creature with a Fate Number, a zodiac sign, and genuine feelings about its partner. It evolves. It has a Bond. It can be displeased.

The interesting combination: An Accord-Maker with both a Homunculus and a Companion creates a three-way coordination system — the Homunculus handles delivery and precision tasks; the Companion handles perception and combat; the Accord-Maker handles construction and strategy. The action economy cost is significant (the Homunculus uses an Instinct; the Companion uses an Instinct; the Accord-Maker may want their own Instinct). A Blueprint Companion would require three separate Instinct sources — this requires either high Resonance Depth features or very careful planning.
Stellar Cartographer — Companion Fate Numbers
Deep Interaction
A Stellar Cartographer can read their companion's Fate Number as a Instinct — which produces fascinating information about the bond they share and the companion's cosmic position.

Companion reading: A companion's Fate Number reads differently from a person's — the Cartographer sees the Number and then a second resonance beneath it, the zodiac sign's frequency expressed through a living form rather than a soul-thread. It is like reading a Number written in a different hand than usual.

Bond Cartography + Companion: The Bond Cartography feature at Depth 3 works fully on companion bonds — the Cartographer can map the health of their companion's bond with perfect accuracy, including whether it is strained (companion has been mistreated) or strengthening (recent Evolution Feat completed). This is the most reliable way for a Stellar Cartographer to track their companion's emotional state beyond the basic Bond Sense.

Quint Bond implications: A Stellar Cartographer who notices their own companion's Fate Number falls within Quint Bond range of four other party members' companions has discovered something the Loom of Fate will need to be told about. This should not happen by accident.
Rāśi-Siddha — Sign Alignment
Tapas Interaction
A Rāśi-Siddha whose companion shares their zodiac sign has a particularly deep attunement — their Tapas practice and the companion's sign-frequency reinforce each other.

Matching sign bonus: When the Rāśi-Siddha uses a Mantra Strike within 10 ft of their companion, the strike deals +1d4 damage of their sign's elemental type — the companion's proximity amplifies the Tapas expression. This is not a Tapas cost. It is resonance.

Purification touch: A Kanya-temple Siddha with a Virgo-sign companion can use their companion as a delivery vector for the Purification Touch — the companion touches the target, the Siddha spends the Tapas from any distance within Bond Sense range (1 mile). The agreement between them extends the Siddha's reach.
Rules Clarifications
Multiple Companions
A character may have only one fully bonded companion at a time. A second bond cannot form while the first is active — the Loom will not maintain two Paired Bonds at the companion tier simultaneously for one character. If a companion dies permanently (an extraordinarily rare event requiring specific narrative circumstances), the character may eventually form a new bond — but not before a period of genuine grief that the Concordance tracks in campaign time.
Companion Death
A companion cannot permanently die as long as its partner is alive and the bond is intact. At 0 Vitality it stabilizes. Permanent death requires: the partner deliberately severs the bond (see Bond Severance), the companion is killed by a Legendary Beast, or the companion is consumed by Void-energy conversion (becoming a Void-Husk equivalent). If any of these occur, the partner gains the Bond-Severed condition for 1d6 months.
Bond Severance
The partner may deliberately release the companion — sending it into the wild, returning it to its natural context, or completing the relationship intentionally rather than maintaining it out of habit. This is not death. The bond weakens to a general awareness rather than an active connection. The companion remembers the partner. The partner remembers the companion. The Loom records it. Bond Severance done with genuine care for the companion's welfare does not trigger Bond-Severed condition.
Companion Separation
Partners and companions may be separated for extended periods — the bond does not break with distance. Bond Sense functions at any range on the same plane. The companion does not follow the partner into other planes automatically. A companion that has been separated for more than 30 days without contact begins to revert toward Stage I emotionally (not mechanically — the abilities remain, but the companion's behavior becomes more feral and less responsive to distant Bond Sense guidance).
The Companion in the World

Companions are not invisible to the world. NPCs notice them. Factions have opinions about them. The Ashen Coil recognizes companion bonds as vulnerabilities — threatening a companion is a well-documented Coil tactic for applying pressure to party members who have bonded animals. The Kallos-Sworn are fascinated by companions whose signs match the Legendary Beasts' domains — they send researchers. Lord Sagittarius of the Sagittarian Steppes has a Stage III Sagittarius-sign companion that has been with him for forty years. He does not discuss it.

The Most Important Rule
The companion is not a class feature. It is a character. The evolution system is not a progression tree. It is a relationship arc. Every mechanical threshold in this document exists to serve the fiction, not replace it. A companion that has completed all Stage II feats but whose bond feels shallow and transactional should not evolve to Stage III — the Concordance should wait until the relationship catches up to the mechanics. Conversely, a companion whose relationship with its partner is extraordinary but whose feat list is incomplete may evolve early if the Concordance feels the bond has genuinely reached that depth. The feats are a guide. The relationship is the truth.
