The Kallos-Sworn

Never Again · The Founding Vow

The world's institutional memory of catastrophe — organized into twelve zodiac houses, ranked by Circle, and bound to each other under the stars they study.

The Kallos-Sworn were not founded. They coalesced — in the years immediately following the Night of Kallos, when the survivors of the Precursor civilization were still trying to understand what had happened. The Four Architects of Kallos had held the only confirmed Quint Bond in recorded history. When one was assassinated, the resonance severance cascaded through the remaining three — a catastrophic discharge that ended an empire in a single night. The Aetheric shockwave was felt across the world. Its echoes are still measurable in the geological record of Elethorn's grid nodes.

The first Kallos-Sworn were simply survivors who had witnessed the severance and refused to let the knowledge of what caused it die with the civilization it destroyed. They were archivists before they were warriors, scholars before they were an order. The Argent Spire was built on the ruins of the Kallos capital's observatory — not as a monument to what was lost, but as a working instrument of ongoing vigilance. The founding vow was two words: never again. Everything else — the twelve houses, the Circle system, the Starbound bond, the suppressed archives — is infrastructure built around those two words.

The Kallos-Sworn's relationship with the Ashen Coil is not a rivalry. It is not even, technically, a war — though it has produced battles. It is an ontological opposition. The Coil believes the cosmos is running down and seeks to control that dissolution. The Kallos-Sworn believes the cosmos can be held together, and has dedicated four thousand years to proving it. Both organizations have access to the same suppressed Kallos records. They reached opposite conclusions from the same evidence. Aethar, who read those records before either organization had fully classified them, is the living proof that the evidence itself is dangerous regardless of which conclusion one reaches.

Founded
Approximately 40 years after the Night of Kallos, Era I. The exact founding date is disputed — the Kallos-Sworn do not celebrate anniversaries of catastrophe.
Headquarters
Argent Spire — built on the ruins of the Kallos capital's observatory. The Crystalline Core is sealed beneath it. The Arch-Oracle's chambers are directly above the seal.
Current Arch-Oracle
Lyra — 12th Circle, Aquarius house. Has held the position for 60 years. Has calculated the seal will fail within years without the Resonant Binding ritual.
Active membership
Approximately 400 Kallos-Sworn worldwide, across all twelve houses. An additional 200 Starbound partners. The order is small because the initiation requirement — swearing before a Legendary Beast — is genuinely dangerous.
Relationship with Ashen Coil
Active opposition. The Fang of the Whisper specifically targets Kallos-Sworn intelligence networks. Three Arch-Oracles have been assassinated by Coil operatives in the last 500 years.
Aethar's status
Former 12th Circle, Shifting Horizons Conclave (now House Sagittarius). His name has been formally excised from all Kallos-Sworn records. Arch-Oracle Lyra refuses to excise it from her personal correspondence.
The Suppressed Archives
Beneath Argent Spire, in a vault accessible only to 9th Circle and above, are the complete Kallos records — everything recovered from the Precursor civilization's final years, including the research the Four Architects were conducting when the assassination occurred. The Kallos-Sworn have classified most of it at the highest levels. Three documents are classified beyond Circle rank entirely — accessible only with the Arch-Oracle's direct authorization and the simultaneous presence of two 11th Circle witnesses. Aethar accessed all three before anyone realized he had. The order still does not know how he bypassed the access controls. Serath of the Closed Circle does — he helped him. This is the one secret Serath has never shared with Aethar.

The Kallos-Sworn's Circle system runs from 1st to 12th — one Circle per zodiac sign, representing not merely seniority but depth of attunement to both the Aetheric system and the order's founding purpose. The number twelve is not coincidental. The founders believed that if twelve practitioners could achieve full attunement across all twelve zodiac domains simultaneously, they could reconstruct something approximating the Quad Bond's stability — distributed across an organization rather than concentrated in four individuals. Four thousand years later, this has never been achieved. The theoretical work is still ongoing.

The Three Living Zodiac Masters
Besides Arch-Oracle Lyra, three 12th Circle Zodiac Masters are alive. Master Caellum (House Scorpio, 9th Circle equivalent in power — was elevated young) is believed to be operating somewhere in the Viridian Principalities. Master Thessaly (House Pisces) has not been seen in twelve years; the order officially lists her as missing, not dead. Master Vael-Kallos (House Aquarius, the Earthly Core Conclave's leader) is at Argent Spire but increasingly reclusive — Lyra suspects he has read the third ultra-classified document without authorization and has been unable to function normally since.

Each of the twelve houses represents one zodiac sign's domain applied to the Kallos-Sworn's founding purpose. The houses are not departments — they are philosophical traditions within the same institution, each offering a different answer to the question of how one prevents another Kallos. Houses collaborate constantly and argue almost as often. The Arch-Oracle's primary management challenge is preventing House Scorpio and House Libra from conducting their centuries-old methodological dispute somewhere the newer members can see it.

House Aries — The Vanguard
First response · Field action · Threat neutralization
Risk: Impulsive Surge

House Aries are the Kallos-Sworn's first responders — the ones who go when the order learns of a Coil incursion, a Void-Husk emergence, or a grid node reactivating with Ophiuchus energy. They are not subtle and they do not aspire to subtlety. Their founding argument: the Night of Kallos happened because no one acted fast enough when the first warning signs appeared. Conclave within Argent Spire: The Shifting Horizons Conclave houses many Aries members — they use celestial observation to identify threats early so they can respond to them fast.

Weaving risk (Impulsive Surge): Aries practitioners who channel under extreme urgency or anger risk opening Channels they didn't intend — spontaneous Working discharge that targets the nearest entity regardless of alignment. DC 13 Attunement Warding Resonance when weaving in combat while below half Vitality or fail and trigger an uncontrolled Working.

House Taurus manages what is worth keeping. House Cancer — The Wardens
Seal maintenance · Argent Spire defense · Maw monitoring
Risk: Over-Shielding

House Cancer maintains the seals. Not merely the seal on the Crystalline Core beneath Argent Spire, but every containment Working the order has established worldwide — Void-Husk isolation zones, corrupted grid nodes, the secondary seal on the Abyssal Leviathan's secondary access point in the Boreal Expanse. At 30+ days of continuous defensive Working maintenance, all Workings targeting the practitioner (including healing) require DC 16 Working Threshold check to penetrate the accumulated defensive resonance.

House Leo is the Kallos-Sworn's public presence — the face the order shows to the world when it chooses to show a face at all. Triggers when weaving while experiencing strong pride, anger, or elation.

House Virgo — The Analysts
Research · Blight detection · Kanya tradition liaison
Risk: Analytical Paralysis

House Virgo produces the order's research — studying Coil corruption patterns, Void-Husk behavioral analysis, grid node degradation rates, and the mathematical modeling of the seal's remaining stability. They work closely with the Kanya Siddha tradition because both are focused on purification and systematic analysis. Their founding argument: the Night of Kallos was preventable with sufficient data. House Virgo collects the data. Lady Virgo of the Order of Pure Reason was once a House Virgo member and left because she believed the Kallos-Sworn's analysis was not thorough enough. This remains a source of deep institutional embarrassment.

Weaving risk (Analytical Paralysis): Virgo practitioners who over-analyze their Working choices under pressure risk decision-lock — they cannot open an Aetheric Channel for 1 Pulse if they have spent more than one round weighing options without acting. The analysis becomes its own obstacle.

House Libra — The Adjudicators
Internal discipline · Ethics · Cross-order law
Risk: Resonant Deadlock

House Libra manages the order's internal discipline — investigating ethical violations, adjudicating disputes between houses, and maintaining the standards that separate the Kallos-Sworn from the organizations they oppose. They are not popular. They are respected, which is different. Their founding argument: the order that saves the world by becoming the thing it's fighting has accomplished nothing. House Libra is the conscience of the institution. House Scorpio thinks they are the brake on the institution. Both are correct.

Weaving risk (Resonant Deadlock): Libra practitioners who face genuine moral ambiguity while weaving risk their Aetheric field entering equipoise — all Workings they attempt require an additional DC 14 Attunement Active Resonance or fizzle as the practitioner's conflicted intent disrupts the Channel. Resolves when the moral question is resolved (or consciously set aside).

Covert operations · Infiltration · Coil counter-intelligence
Risk: Obsessive Threading

House Scorpio operates in the dark. They are the order's covert arm — running long-term infiltrations of the Ashen Coil, maintaining deep cover identities that last decades, and eliminating Coil operatives when the order determines elimination is necessary. House Libra believes House Scorpio has exceeded its mandate on multiple occasions. Notable connection: The Loom of Fate's Oya-aspected spy network has significant overlap with House Scorpio's operational methods. Neither organization officially acknowledges this.

Weaving risk (Obsessive Threading): Scorpio practitioners who track a single target obsessively for 30+ days risk their divination Workings becoming locked to that target — they cannot use scrying or detection Workings on any other subject until they achieve their objective or formally release the focus. DC 17 Attunement Warding Resonance to resist lock-in at the 30-day mark.

House Pisces — The Dreamers
Prophetic archives · Legendary Beast communication · Loom interpretation
Risk: Reality Erosion

House Pisces works the boundary between received knowledge and interpreted meaning — maintaining the order's prophetic archives (every verified prophecy the order has collected since founding), managing communication attempts with the Legendary Beasts, and interpreting the Loom of Fate's visible threading for strategic guidance. Master Thessaly, the missing 12th Circle Zodiac Master, is House Pisces. The house believes she is still alive because her prophetic archive annotation — a running commentary she maintained for forty years — continues to update itself. They cannot explain how. They have not told anyone outside the house.

Weaving risk (Reality Erosion): Pisces practitioners who engage in prophetic Working or dream-contact for extended periods risk the boundary between vision and reality becoming permeable — they experience prophetic imagery overlaid on the present moment, treating potential futures as current events. Long-term practitioners develop coping rituals. Practitioners without coping rituals develop reputations for unreliability.

In Jordan's original conception, male channelers went mad from a taint placed on the male half of the One Power. Sideros has no gender-based restriction — weaving ability is distributed across all genders, tied instead to zodiac sign and Primal Essence. But the risk is real and sign-specific. Each zodiac sign's domain carries a specific vulnerability in the weaving process — a way the cosmic force, if improperly handled, turns on the practitioner who invited it.

The Kallos-Sworn discovered this systematically over centuries of observation. They keep detailed records. The weaving risks are not punishments — they are natural properties of the forces involved, the same way fire burns the person holding it if they grip it wrong. The order teaches mitigation techniques for each sign's risk. A practitioner who knows their risk and works with it rarely triggers it. A practitioner who ignores it eventually does.

Omega Practitioners and Weaving Risk
Omega-aspected channelers access the Aetheric field most naturally — their soul-frequency is closest to the Aether's baseline resonance. This gives them higher ceiling potential but also greater vulnerability to their sign's risk: their signs's weaving risk triggers on a DC 2 lower than the table values above, because the deeper access cuts both ways. Alpha-aspected practitioners have slightly lower ceiling potential but their risks trigger on a DC 2 higher — the forceful nature of Alpha energy provides some insulation against the field's feedback. Beta practitioners experience the risks exactly as written — their adaptive nature is precisely calibrated to the middle ground.

Seven hundred years ago, House Aquarius began researching what they called the Manufactured Resonance Project — a systematic attempt to replicate the Fated Bond's Whisper Bond (the telepathic connection that emerges at Triad tier) through deliberate ritual rather than cosmically determined Fate Number proximity. The project ran for sixty years across three generations of researchers. It was the most rigorous study of the Fated Bond system ever conducted, and it produced something no one anticipated: not the Fated Bond, and not a mere enchantment, but a third thing entirely.

The Stellar Cartographers who have examined Starbound pairs report that the bond does not appear in the Fate Number system the way a natural Fated Bond does. Where a Fated Bond registers as two Numbers drawn toward each other, the Starbound bond registers as one Number with a shadow — a second resonance frequency that moves with the first but is not itself a Fate Number. The Loom of Fate does not appear to have anticipated this configuration. Whether that means the Loom cannot see the Starbound bond, or simply that the Loom has chosen not to acknowledge it, is a question House Pisces has been investigating for five centuries without resolution.

The Warrior
Carries the mage's Aetheric signature woven into their own field. Perceives the mage's condition at all times. The warrior's experience of the bond is more intense — they feel what the mage feels, often more acutely than the mage feels it themselves.
Bond Properties — What It Actually Does
Resonant Awareness — Always Active
Both Directions
Both bonded parties always know the other's current Vitality level (exact number), emotional state (a general quality — calm, distressed, afraid, determined — not specific thoughts), and whether they are currently Weaving. Range: unlimited on the same plane of existence. This persists through Aether-Dampening Ore zones — the bond is not Aetheric in origin and cannot be suppressed. In the Stillness of Dunmar, both parties experience the bond as more vivid, not less — the total magical silence makes the bond's non-Aetheric frequency more perceptible by contrast.
Whisper Thread — Limited Telepathy
Both Directions · 1 mile
Within 1 mile, both parties may transmit one-way impressions — not full sentences, but emotional weight, urgency, and simple directional intent. "Danger from the north." "Come now." "I am well." The impressions are felt rather than heard, which means they cannot be intercepted, recorded, or read by Stellar Cartographers. This is less than a true Whisper Bond (which transmits full language) but it is also undetectable in ways a true bond is not. Several House Scorpio members have made operational use of this property.
Asymmetric Intensity — The Warrior's Burden
Warrior-Side Only
The Starbound warrior experiences the bond more intensely than the mage. When the mage is in pain, the warrior feels it as pressure. When the mage channels at high intensity, the warrior experiences a sympathetic Aetheric resonance — not Weaving themselves, but feeling the field move through them secondhand. When the mage dies, the warrior experiences the severance as a physical event — not fatal, but incapacitating for 1d6 days as the Aetheric field that was woven into theirs dissolves. This asymmetry was not intended by the original researchers. They discovered it when the first test pair's mage was killed in a Coil incursion and the warrior was found unconscious three miles away.
Combat Synchronization
Both Directions · 30 ft
Within 30 ft of each other, bonded pairs operate with preternatural coordination. The warrior always knows where the mage intends to move before they move — no communication required. The mage always knows when the warrior is about to strike and can time Working effects to maximize the strike's impact. Mechanically: the warrior may use their Reflex to move into optimal protection position when the mage is targeted, without requiring line-of-sight or prior knowledge of the attack. The mage may delay the release of any Sustained Resonance Working by up to 1 Pulse to synchronize with the warrior's position — this does not count as dropping concentration.
The Accidental True Bond — Rare Event
Campaign Event
Approximately 1 in 40 Starbound pairings, the manufactured bond spontaneously deepens into a genuine Fated Bond — the ritual's attempt to manufacture resonance accidentally achieves what the Loom would have arranged anyway. The Kallos-Sworn have no way of predicting when this will happen. The signs: the Whisper Thread expands from impressions to full language without warning; Resonant Awareness extends beyond Vitality and emotion to include specific thoughts the other party is currently holding; the bond begins to register in the Fate Number system as a legitimate Paired Bond. When this happens, both parties are notified simultaneously — they wake from sleep, both at the same moment, aware that something has changed. The order keeps records of every accidental true bond. There are 23 documented cases. Three of them later became Triad Bonds when a third compatible Fate Number entered their lives. The order considers this the most significant finding of the Manufactured Resonance Project, and has never formally published it.
The Starbound Ritual

The Starbound ritual requires: both parties present and willing; a 6th Circle or above Kallos-Sworn officiating; a clear night sky (the zodiac must be visible — the bond is sealed under the stars that witnessed the founding vow); and three hours. The ritual cannot be performed under a roof. It has been performed in the rain seven times. It has been performed during battle once, in the Coil incursion of 847 Post-Fall, when a Keeper and her Starbound partner were separated and she performed an abbreviated version that the order later declared invalid and then quietly added to the valid ritual variants when the abbreviated version produced a functional bond anyway.

Breaking a Starbound Bond
The Starbound bond cannot be formally dissolved — the ritual that creates it has no reversal procedure. The bond ends only through death (either party's), through Void-Husk conversion (which dissolves the Fate Number and severs the bond's anchor), or through complete Aetheric field collapse (Stage 6 Aetheric Depletion resulting in the field's permanent dissolution — functionally, worse than death). The order has investigated reversal methods for four hundred years. The most recent finding: the bond cannot be reversed because it is not, technically, a Working. It is a state of being. You cannot undo being something the way you can undo doing something. Several former Starbound warriors who outlived their mage-partners have written extensively on this distinction. Their accounts are in House Pisces's archive, cross-referenced under both "bond theory" and "grief."

Becoming Kallos-Sworn requires two things: being found, and surviving the initiation. Finding happens through House Leo's recruitment network, through the Zodiac Priest tradition, through referral by existing members, or through the rare practitioner who demonstrates sufficient power and alignment that the order approaches them. The Kallos-Sworn do not advertise. They do not post notices. They watch, and when they see someone worth approaching, they approach.

The initiation is not a ceremony. It is an encounter. The candidate must travel to their sign's Legendary Beast and swear the founding vow in the Beast's presence. The Beast does not officiate. The Beast does not respond. The candidate stands in the presence of a fallen god — a creature of CR 18 to 30, capable of killing them without effort — and speaks two words: never again. If they survive the encounter (which requires either extraordinary caution, extraordinary luck, or the Beast choosing not to engage), they are Kallos-Sworn.

What the Beast does
Varies by sign. The Nemean Lion typically ignores initiates unless they demonstrate weakness. The Twin Dream-Fish encounter produces a vision regardless of the candidate's intent. The Adamant Crab has never attacked an initiate — it simply observes them until they leave.
What the vow actually is
"Never again." That is the complete founding vow. Everything else — house membership, Circle rank, specific duties — is organizational infrastructure layered on top of two words. The vow is to the world, not to the order. This distinction matters in cases of institutional corruption.
The vow's constraint
The vow is not magically enforced. There is no oath-rod, no binding Working, no external compulsion. The zodiac bond is the constraint — not because it prevents action, but because a Kallos-Sworn who acts against the founding vow is acting against the cosmic force they have attuned their Weaving to. The Aetheric field notices. Workings become less reliable. Channels take longer to open. The resistance is not punishment — it is friction between the practitioner's intent and the frequency they've invited into their body.
Initiation failures
Approximately 1 in 12 candidates do not complete the initiation — killed by the Beast, driven away by the encounter's proximity, or deciding upon approach that this is not the life they want. The order considers all three outcomes valid. Particularly the third.
Aethar's Initiation
Aethar swore before the Star-Metal Stag — the Sagittarius Legendary Beast — at age nineteen. By all accounts it was an unremarkable initiation. The Stag was present in the Grove of Fallen Stars. Aethar spoke the vow. The Stag looked at him for a long moment and then returned to grazing. House Sagittarius now debates whether the Stag saw something in that look that none of the human witnesses did. Three members believe the Stag was reading a future the others couldn't perceive. One member believes the Stag recognized what Aethar would become and simply decided it was not the Stag's problem. House Pisces has requested access to the initiation witnesses' accounts. House Sagittarius has not yet responded.

The conflict between the Kallos-Sworn and the Ashen Coil is not a war in any conventional sense. There are no fronts, no territorial objectives, no formal declarations. It is a centuries-long intelligence contest punctuated by violent incidents that neither side publicly acknowledges. The Kallos-Sworn's institutional existence is known to most governments. Their specific membership, operational methods, and intelligence holdings are not. The Coil's existence is known to most governments. Their specific leadership, operational cells, and strategic objectives are the most carefully guarded secrets in the world.

Both organizations have spent four centuries trying to understand what the other knows. The Coil's Triad — Aethar, Serath, and Drava — represent three different approaches to this problem. Aethar understands the Kallos-Sworn's theoretical foundations better than most of its current members do. Serath has read three of the twelve houses' classified operational files. Drava built the intelligence network that recovered them.

The Current State
What the Coil knows about the Kallos-Sworn
The membership of all twelve house councils (5th Circle and above). The Resonant Binding's five required components and their locations. That Arch-Oracle Lyra has calculated a timeline for the seal's failure. That Inti-kora's grid node work is related to the sixth component they don't have. They do not know that Serath has identified three Quint Bond cluster members and not reported it.
What the Kallos-Sworn knows about the Coil
The Triad's existence and approximate identities (Aethar confirmed, Serath suspected, Drava unknown). That the Fang of the Whisper is Drava's former intelligence network. The Coil's four-stage plan (stages 1–3 identified). They do not know Stage Five — preventing the Quint Bond. They do not know Grid-Point Seven is reactivating as a dissolution engine.
The Coil's structural vulnerability
Drava Nullsong. The Fang of the Whisper's loyalty is to Drava personally, not to Aethar's vision. If Drava were offered a genuine alternative — real belonging, real purpose — the Coil's operational capacity would collapse within weeks. The Kallos-Sworn has never identified this vulnerability because House Scorpio's files on Drava are incomplete. They know the network. They don't know the person holding it.
The Kallos-Sworn's structural vulnerability
The Resonant Binding ritual requires six components and six weeks without Coil interruption. The Fang of the Whisper knows four of the five confirmed component locations. If the ritual is interrupted after the third component is placed, the partial activation destabilizes the seal further rather than reinforcing it. Aethar has modeled this. The timing of his Stage Four grid reactivation is not coincidental.
Serath's Decision
Serath of the Closed Circle has identified three of the five Quint Bond cluster members. He has not reported this to Aethar. He has also identified that the Kallos-Sworn's House Aquarius — Lyra's house — has been researching the Quint Bond for three centuries and is closer to understanding it than the Coil realizes. Serath is the only person in the world who holds both pieces of information simultaneously. What he does with that knowledge is the most consequential decision currently pending in Sideros. He is aware of this. He has been sitting with it for three months. His Tapas journal (he maintained a Siddha practice before the Coil — he has not told Drava or Aethar) shows he began attempting to contact the Kanya tradition six weeks ago and stopped before completing the contact. The journal is in his chambers in the Maw facility. No one else knows it exists.
