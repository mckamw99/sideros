Kindred · Beast-Descended
The Beast-Descended
Replacing: Dragonborn · Also covers: Leonin, Minotaur (partial)
Medium Sign-Locked Lineage All Primal Essences

The Legendary Beasts are not celibate. The Pantheonic Fall distributed twelve gods into twelve forms, and those forms — vast, ancient, and operating at a frequency no mortal fully comprehends — occasionally produce offspring with mortals who encountered them during the great transition. The Beast-Descended carry that encounter in their blood four thousand years later. They are not half-animal. They are humanoid beings in whom a specific cosmic frequency expressed itself too strongly to be contained by ordinary mortal biology.

The result is visible. A Nemean-line Beast-Descended has gold in their irises and a warmth to their skin that does not fade in winter. A Stygian-line has skin that reads slightly wrong in peripheral vision — the eye slides off them even when they are standing still. A Star-Metal-line has hair that catches light from angles it shouldn't. These are not costumes. They are the world's record of what happened to a bloodline when the divine became animal and something got through.

Beast-Descended know their line. The lineage is named at birth through the Loom's recording — every Beast-Descended is born with their ancestral sign burning briefly visible on their skin, which fades within hours but is recorded by midwives trained to read it. A Beast-Descended whose Fate Number sign matches their lineage sign is called Aligned — the cosmos and the bloodline agree. A Beast-Descended whose signs diverge is called Split — carrying the Lion's body and the Scorpion's fate, or the Stag's speed and the Crab's purpose. The world has opinions about both.

The Aligned / Split Distinction — Campaign Notes
Aligned Beast-Descended (lineage sign = Fate Number sign) are considered auspicious in most cultures — the cosmos and the blood agree. They often find themselves treated as representatives of their lineage Beast's domain, for better or worse. The Kallos-Sworn have historically recruited them aggressively for the House matching their sign.

Split Beast-Descended (lineage sign ≠ Fate Number sign) are considered philosophically interesting — two cosmic forces in one body, neither canceling the other. The Stellar Cartographers study them. The Loom of Fate tracks them with extra attention. A Split Beast-Descended who forms a Fated Bond with someone whose sign matches their lineage (not their Fate Number) is a documented Loom anomaly. Three such anomalies are currently recorded. The Loom has not explained what they mean.
Kindred · Geode-Kin
The Geode-Kin
Replacing: Gnome (Rock/Forest) + Kobold · Jade Empire biological engineers
Small Jade Empire Adjunct All Primal Essences

The Geode-Kin are the smallest biological people in Sideros, and also the ones most responsible for the Jade Empire's infrastructure. They are not a sub-culture of the Gear-Forged — they are the engineers who understood Resonant Geode principles before the Gear-Forged existed, who built the first housings that made constructed sapience possible, and who have been quietly essential to every major technological development in the Empire for eight centuries while receiving a fraction of the credit. The Grand Prime Forgeman considers the Geode-Kin the most underestimated Kindred in the world. He has said this publicly. Twice.

Geode-Kin are born with a passive sensitivity to Resonant Geode energy — they can feel a Grade-A Geode through a stone wall at twenty feet, and they can read the emotional state of a Gear-Forged's Geode heart the way a physician reads a pulse. This sensitivity is the origin of their cultural role: before calibration instruments existed, a Geode-Kin's hands were the calibration instrument. Many still are.

Outside the Jade Empire, Geode-Kin are found in every city that has sophisticated craft infrastructure — they follow the work. Port Damaris has a significant Geode-Kin community in its engineers' quarter. Elethorn's grid-maintenance teams employ three. Ironhearth's relationship with them is complicated — the Dunmarim respect craft excellence but the Geode-Kin's Aetheric sensitivity sits awkwardly against the Order of Pure Reason's preferences. A Geode-Kin in the Stillness of Dunmar feels the Ore's suppression as a specific physical discomfort, like standing in a room where the pressure is wrong. They can function. They do not enjoy it.

Lifespan
350–400 years. The longest-lived biological Kindred after the Eldren. The Geode-Kin do not know why they live this long — their own theoretical framework suggests their Aetheric sensitivity produces a stabilizing effect on biological aging. The Grand Prime Forgeman has a theory. He has not shared it.
The Geode-Kin and the Gear-Forged
The relationship is collegial and occasionally tense. Gear-Forged rely on Geode-Kin expertise for maintenance; Geode-Kin are paid well and treated as essential; neither culture fully trusts the other's motives. A Gear-Forged Geode-Pilot and a Geode-Kin Accord-Maker working together is the most technically sophisticated combination the Jade Empire produces. The Empire encourages this pairing. The individuals involved usually have to work out the power dynamics themselves.
Kindred · Beast-Kin
The Beast-Kin
Replacing: Tabaxi, Leonin, Minotaur, Harengon, Tortle · Twelve zodiac morphologies
Medium Zodiac-Aspected Morphology All Primal Essences

Where the Beast-Descended carry a Legendary Beast's frequency in their blood while presenting as largely humanoid, the Beast-Kin carry it in their form. They are unmistakably animal-aspected — fur, scales, feathers, chitin, or shell expressed through a humanoid skeletal structure. The morphology is not random. It follows the zodiac. A Cancer-aspected Beast-Kin has the Adamant Crab's coloring, its carapace-like skin, and the way it moves that suggests something that would be more comfortable moving sideways if pressed. A Sagittarius-aspected Beast-Kin has antlers and the stag's ranging, searching quality of attention.

The Beast-Kin are found across the world in scattered communities — they have no single homeland because no single culture produced them. The best current scholarship suggests they emerged in the centuries after the Pantheonic Fall as the zodiac's newly distributed power found unexpected expression in bloodlines that had sustained contact with Legendary Beast territories. The Arcadian naturalists call this Resonant Drift. The Kallos-Sworn call it fascinating. The Beast-Kin call it Tuesday.

Beast-Kin are distinct from Wolf-Shifters in a crucial way: the Wolf-Shifter's animal nature is fluid, shifting between Omote and Ura states. The Beast-Kin's animal nature is fixed — they are always fully themselves, with no suppressed form and no threshold to maintain. What you see is what they are. The Primal Accord tradition finds this philosophically significant. The Beast-Kin community finds the Primal Accord tradition's interest mildly flattering and occasionally exhausting.

Kindred · Orc-Kin
The Orc-Kin
Replacing: Orc / Half-Orc · Stateless, dispersed across the Duchy Compact
Medium No Fixed Homeland Alpha-aspected (common)

The Orc-Kin have no nation, and this is not an accident of history — it is the result of a specific historical event that the Duchy Compact's official records describe in three sentences and the Orc-Kin oral tradition describes in three hours. The short version is that the Orc-Kin held a territory in the pre-Compact era, held it well, and lost it not to war but to a treaty that the other signatories honored in legal terms and violated in practical ones. The Orc-Kin response to this was not to fight — they had already won the war — but to disperse. If a homeland can be taken, do not have one.

Four hundred years later, Orc-Kin are found in every major settlement in the Duchy Compact as soldiers, laborers, guards, pit-fighters, and mercenaries. They are consistently paid less than their skill warrants and consistently perform above what their employers expected. This has produced a specific cultural attitude: work harder than anyone expects, trust the people you work alongside, and know that the institution you're working for will find a way to shortchange you eventually. Plan accordingly.

What the Compact underestimates, consistently, is the Orc-Kin's endurance. Not just physical — ideological. A people who lost their homeland and chose dispersal over decline have a specific relationship with survival that manifests in combat, in negotiation, and in the long view of any situation. The Orc-Kin who joins an adventuring party has thought about how it ends. They have planned for when it goes wrong. They are usually the last one standing, and they are rarely surprised to be.

The Ashen-Sworn: Not all Orc-Kin chose the path of dispersal and integration. A significant population — called the Ashen-Sworn by other cultures, and something unprintable by the Dunmarim — rejected the dispersal strategy entirely and instead chose consolidation under Ashen Coil influence. Operating as warbands in the Aries March and the contested frontier zones of Elethorn, the Ashen-Sworn fight for territory, resources, and the Coil's strategic objectives simultaneously. They are not ideological converts to the Hollow Serpent's philosophy — most care nothing for Aethar's four-stage plan. They fight because the Coil offers what the Duchy Compact never did: a homeland, a banner, and an enemy they're permitted to fight openly. This makes them effective and unpredictable allies for the Coil, and a complicating factor for Orc-Kin characters who must navigate a world that assumes their Kindred's martial reputation comes entirely from the Ashen-Sworn. The Orc-Kin who fights alongside the party carries the weight of a reputation they didn't build and didn't choose.

The Long View — Campaign Notes
The Orc-Kin have a specific relationship with every major institution in the Duchy Compact: they have worked for most of them, been shortchanged by most of them, and maintained relationships with all of them because burning bridges when you have no homeland is impractical. An Orc-Kin player character knows people in most cities. Not powerful people — reliable people. The guard who will look the other way for an old colleague. The innkeeper who will hold a message. The pit-fight promoter who owes a debt. This network is not a formal system. It is the natural result of a people who have been everywhere and remembered everyone worth remembering.
Kindred · Giant-Kin
The Giant-Kin
Replacing: Goliath · Boreal Expanse, Norse giant-tradition
Medium (Large-adjacent) Boreal Expanse Alpha-aspected (predominant)

The Giant-Kin stand between seven and eight feet tall and weigh between 280 and 340 pounds, and they carry this mass with an ease that suggests their skeleton is doing something other skeletons are not. The Boreal naturalists have a theory — that the Giant-Kin's bone density is partially Aetheric, a residual effect of whatever giant lineage produced them. The Giant-Kin do not find this theory interesting. They are aware their bones are unusual. They have always been aware. The bones were there before the theory.

The Boreal Expanse's Norse cultural tradition treats the Giant-Kin as the oldest people of the north — not the first to arrive, but the first to be there, in the sense that the landscape itself seems to have been made for people their size. The Runic Knight tradition emerged specifically from Giant-Kin warriors who discovered that the rune-marks appearing naturally on their skin at puberty could be activated rather than merely observed. Every Giant-Kin carries their runic inheritance in their skin. Not all of them choose to wake it up.

In the Duchy Compact outside the Boreal Expanse, Giant-Kin occupy an unusual social position — too large to be ignored, too rare to be common, and too self-contained to be easily categorized. They are not aggressive by default. They are simply operating at a scale that others find confrontational whether or not confrontation is intended. A Giant-Kin who has spent time in cities has learned to make themselves smaller in manner if not in body — to move carefully, speak quietly, and choose their moments. The Primal Jarl tradition is disproportionately populated by Giant-Kin who got tired of making themselves smaller.

The Runic Knight Connection
Giant-Kin Runic Knights have access to their natural runic inheritance in addition to the Runic Knight Calling's learned rune-carving. Their natural marks and carved runes operate separately — a Giant-Kin Runic Knight at Depth 10 has both systems active simultaneously. The Boreal tradition considers this the fullest expression of the rune-smith practice: the marks that the body already knows, supplemented by the marks that the mind has learned.
Social Position
Giant-Kin are never invisible in a crowd. This has produced two broad cultural responses: those who lean into the visibility (Alpha-aspected, often Primal Jarl or Solar Guard, using presence as a tool) and those who develop extraordinary subtlety in every domain except the physical one (Beta-aspected, often Stellar Cartographers or Resonance Seekers, working through other people). The distinction is a genuine cultural fracture within Giant-Kin communities and the source of most of their internal debate.
Kindred · Elemental-Kin
The Elemental-Kin
Replacing: Genasi (Air/Earth/Fire/Water) · Zodiac-aspected elemental expression
Medium Zodiac-Aspected All Primal Essences

Where the Beast-Descended carry a Legendary Beast's frequency in their blood and the Elemental-Kin carry a zodiac sign's elemental expression in their body — not morphologically like the Beast-Kin, but energetically. An Aries Elemental-Kin runs warm. Warmer than fever. Their skin is never cold, their breath carries faint heat on the coldest day, and when they are angry the temperature in the room rises measurably. They are not on fire. They contain fire. The distinction matters to them and is lost on everyone who has ever stood too close to one in a small room.

The elemental expression follows the zodiac's physical associations rather than its personality domains. Aries and Leo carry fire; Cancer, Aquarius, and Pisces carry water; Gemini and Libra carry air; Taurus and Capricorn carry earth; Scorpio and Sagittarius carry a hybrid the Comet-Magi classify as Aetheric-elemental (lightning/void energy).

Elemental-Kin exist everywhere the Aetheric grid runs close to the surface — they are most common near Ceque Line nodes, and scholars of the Shifting Horizons Conclave have documented a statistically significant correlation between Elemental-Kin birth rates and grid node activity. The Ceque-Attuned Star-Born and the Elemental-Kin sometimes share a village, and the locals find this understandably alarming. Two forms of grid-expression in close proximity produces unusual Aetheric readings that the Conclave researchers find professionally exciting and the locals find uncomfortable.

Those who master the control can modulate it freely — warming a cold person's hands, cooling a fever, providing light in darkness from the fire that is always present. Those who do not master it are a fire hazard in specific emotional states.

Elemental-Kin and the Zodiac System
Unlike the Beast-Descended, whose lineage sign is fixed at birth independently of their Fate Number, the Elemental-Kin's elemental nature always matches their Fate Number sign — the elemental inheritance and the cosmic fortune are the same expression. An Aries Elemental-Kin always rolls an Aries Fate Number. An Aquarius Elemental-Kin always rolls Aquarius. The Loom and the body agree, completely, in every Elemental-Kin born. This makes them unusually legible to Stellar Cartographers — there is no divergence to interpret, no Split to account for. The Kallos-Sworn find this either reassuring or suspicious depending on which House is doing the interpreting.
