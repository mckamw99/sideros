Mountain's Heart

Rāśi-Siddha · Mithuna Temple
Mithuna Temple ♊ — Duality
Gemini domain · Two mantras, two expressions, one breath
Twin Mantra
Depth 3 · Rajasic 2 TP
Make two Mantra Strikes as part of one Act — each at a different target, each dealing full damage. The Mithuna tradition emphasizes simultaneous expression: the mantra strikes in two directions at once without losing force in either.
Dual Form
Depth 6 · Rajasic 2 TP
For 1 minute: once per Pulse when you make a Mantra Strike, make a second strike against the same or different target as part of the same action (no additional Tapas cost for the second strike). The Mithuna Siddha fights in pairs — the body expresses the mantra's duality at full efficiency.
Perfect Division
Depth 11 · Tamasic 4 TP
Create a psychic duplicate of yourself in an adjacent space — the duplicate acts on your Moment using your Instinct, can make one Mantra Strike per Moment, and has 1 Vitality (any damage destroys it). For 1 minute or until destroyed.
Tamasic note: The Mithuna tradition considers this maneuver ethically complex — you are temporarily expressing a version of yourself that exists separately from your consciousness. The Temple teaches that this Tapas expenditure should only occur when both expressions serve a genuine purpose, not tactical efficiency alone. The Tapas Depletion risk reflects this: forcing division for trivial reasons carries consequence.
Rāśi-Siddha · Karkata Temple
Karkata Temple ♋ — Protection
Cancer domain · The body as shelter for others
Shell Mantra
Depth 3 · Satvic 1 TP
As a Reflex when an ally within 10 ft is targeted by a Strike Resonance, use your Instinct to interpose — the attack targets you instead. The Karkata Siddha's training orients the body toward absorbing harm that would otherwise reach those they protect.
Ward of Tides
Depth 6 · Rajasic 2 TP
Grant one creature you touch temporary Vitality equal to your Tapas pool's current maximum for 1 hour. While they have this temporary Vitality, they have Resonant Fortune on all Warding Resonances — the Karkata mantra extends the Siddha's Guard Rating outward as a gift.
Adamant Shell
Depth 11 · Rajasic 3 TP
For 1 minute, create a shell of Aetheric force around up to Attunement modifier creatures within 10 ft — all damage dealt to shelled creatures is halved; they have immunity to the Corrupted condition; they cannot be forcibly moved. The Karkata Temple's highest expression: the practitioner becomes the Adamant Crab, distributed among those who needed shelter.

Pride's Surge

Arrow Mantra

True Shot

Stag's Stride

Rāśi-Siddha · Makara Temple
Makara Temple ♑ — The Summit
Capricorn domain · The mantra that does not stop until it reaches the top
Summit Mantra
Depth 3 · Satvic 1 TP
At the start of each encounter, declare one objective (defeat this creature, reach this location, protect this person). While working toward the declared objective: Resonant Fortune on all Active Resonances directly serving it; cannot be Dread-Struck. The Makara Temple trains the will to be as immovable as the mountain it is named after.
Perseverance
Depth 6 · Satvic 1 TP
When you fail a Warding Resonance, use your Instinct to spend 1 Tapas (Satvic) and immediately reroll — taking the better result. Once per Pulse. The Makara Siddha's practice produces a specific relationship with failure: it is information, not conclusion.
The Unconquerable
Depth 11 · Rajasic 3 TP
Once per encounter: when reduced to 0 Vitality, instead drop to 1 Vitality and immediately restore Tapas equal to your Attunement modifier. The Makara Temple's highest teaching — that the summit is not a place but a decision, made again at every moment the practitioner might otherwise stop.

Aquarius domain · The body as container for others' burdens
Community Mantra
Depth 3 · Satvic 1 TP
As an Instinct, share Tapas with one ally within 30 ft — transfer up to Attunement modifier Tapas Points to them. They may spend these points as if they were their own Tapas for 1 minute. The Kumbha Temple teaches that Tapas is not a personal resource. It is water in a communal vessel — drawn where needed.
Network Sense
Depth 6 · Satvic 0 TP
Passive. Always know the Vitality level (exact number) and current emotional state of all allies within 60 ft — no roll, no Tapas cost. The Kumbha Siddha has extended the body's awareness to encompass those in their care. This is the Bee-Speaker's Hive Sense expressed through ascetic practice rather than the Hive's network.
Collective Tapas
Depth 11 · Rajasic 3 TP
Once per Deep Attunement, create a Tapas Field centered on yourself — a 30-ft radius that lasts for 1 minute. While within the field: all allies share a pool of temporary Tapas equal to twice your Attunement modifier; any ally may spend from this pool for any Tapas-powered ability they have (if any) or for the basic Rāśi-Siddha Ambient Workings (the communal practice temporarily grants access); when one ally benefits from the pool, all allies within 5 ft of them restore 1 Vitality.
Rāśi-Siddha · Meena Temple
Meena Temple ♓ — The Dream
Pisces domain · The mantra as current, the mind as ocean
Dream Mantra
Depth 3 · Rajasic 2 TP
Your Mantra Strike does not require line of sight — if you have heard or sensed the target this Pulse, the strike lands as if you were adjacent. Additionally: when you hit, the target must make an Attunement Warding Resonance (DC = 8 + Mastery + Attunement) or be briefly dream-touched — Resonant Discord on their next Active Resonance as their perception briefly crosses into dream-state.
Prophetic Reflex
Depth 6 · Satvic 1 TP
Once per Pulse, when a creature within 30 ft makes a Strike Resonance, use your Reflex to spend 1 Tapas — you have already seen this strike in the dream-state and act accordingly. If the target hits, reduce the damage by 1d10 + Attunement modifier. If the target misses, your next Mantra Strike against them has Resonant Fortune — you know where they're going to be.
Twin Fish Mantra
Depth 11 · Tamasic 4 TP
Once per Deep Attunement, spend 4 Tapas to reach briefly into the prophetic space the Twin Dream-Fish inhabit. The Concordance delivers one completely accurate vision of something that will happen within the next 24 hours — not a warning, but a complete scene. The Meena Siddha does not ask what they will see. The Dream-Fish show what they consider important. This is the deepest Tamasic expenditure in the Rāśi-Siddha tradition — it risks the self not through physical corruption but through prophetic dissolution, the potential loss of the boundary between what is and what will be.
