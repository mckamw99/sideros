Sideros / Astrolan — Advanced Progression

Legendary Depth

Beyond Depth 20, the Loom stops measuring. Something else keeps track.

Post-Depth 20 · Campaign-Defining Events Required · Maximum 4 Legendary Depths

Resonance Depth 20 is not the end of growth. It is the end of the system's ability to describe growth in terms it was built for. At Depth 20, a character has mastered their Path, deepened their zodiac bond to its fullest expression, and become something the world's existing vocabulary handles imperfectly. What happens next cannot be earned through practice. It can only be received — from the Legendary Beasts, from the Loom of Fate itself, from the Quint Bond's cosmic resonance, or from the completion of the Resonant Binding ritual.

These are not level-ups. They are acknowledgments. The world has noticed what this character has done and has decided that the existing framework is insufficient to contain them. The Legendary Depth system is the world's response to that insufficiency.

Legendary Depths are rare. In the current era of Sideros, fewer than a dozen living beings have achieved even Legendary Depth I. The Grand Prime Forgeman is one. The Bee-Priestess lineage's oldest member is another. Legendary Depth IV has never been achieved in the current era. The last documented Ascendant-state being was a Kallos-era practitioner whose name was erased from every record the Ashen Coil could reach. The Loom still carries the thread. The thread is the brightest in the active record.

Critical Design Principle
Legendary Depths cannot be pursued. A character who sets out to achieve Legendary Depth I by engineering an encounter with their sign's Legendary Beast will fail — the Beast reads intent, and the intent of someone seeking acknowledgment for its own sake is legible and uninteresting. The acknowledgment happens when the character has done something genuinely significant in the world and the Beast encounters them in the course of that significance. The Concordance does not announce that a Legendary Depth is available. The world simply changes around the character in a specific way, and the Concordance describes it, and the player understands what has happened.
Legendary Depths are not cumulative in the standard sense
LD I does not require LD II to be achieved in order — the three triggers (Beast, Loom, Bond) can occur in any sequence, and characters in the same party may achieve different Legendary Depths from each other. A party member who achieves LD II (Loom Inscription) before LD I (Beast Acknowledgment) has had their thread made load-bearing before the Beast recognized it — which is an unusual situation the Loom of Fate researchers find professionally significant.
LD IV requires LD I, II, and III first
The Ascendant state requires all three prior Legendary Depths. The Resonant Binding ritual cannot be completed by a character who has not been Acknowledged, Inscribed, and Resonant — the ritual recognizes and requires all three states simultaneously. A character who has achieved only two of the three will find the ritual's final stage does not respond to them. The Loom enforces this, not the rulebook.
Legendary Depth I
The Acknowledged
Trigger
The Legendary Beast of your zodiac sign formally recognizes you — not as a worthy opponent, not as an interesting mortal, but as something operating at a frequency it has not encountered in a living being since the Pantheonic Fall.
Boon Type: Path Expansion

The Legendary Beast's acknowledgment is not a gift. It is a recognition — the Beast has perceived, in this practitioner, a degree of attunement to its cosmic frequency that it considers significant. The Beast does not explain this. It does not congratulate. It looks at the character and the character feels the full weight of what the Beast is — not its physical form, but the divine force distributed through that form — and in that moment the character's Path deepens past the ceiling it previously had. The craft grows because something very large has confirmed that there is more craft available.

What this looks like varies by Beast and by character. The Nemean Lion may simply hold the character's gaze for a moment longer than it holds anyone else's. The Twin Dream-Fish may surface in the character's dreams for three consecutive nights before vanishing. The Stygian Scorpion may leave a mark on the character's skin that fades within hours but is recorded in the Aetheric field permanently. The Concordance determines the specific form. The mechanical result is the same.

This ability is not a continuation of the Path's existing features — it is a new register entirely, something the Path's training was pointing toward but could not reach without external confirmation.

Legendary Depth II
The Inscribed
Trigger
The Loom of Fate writes the character's name into its active record — not the historical record of threads completed, not the prophetic record of threads anticipated. The present record. The one that the Loom updates in real time as the world moves.
Boon Type: Loom Gift

The Loom of Fate maintains three records: the historical (all threads completed), the prophetic (all threads anticipated), and the active (the threads currently load-bearing — the ones whose removal would change the structure). Most mortals appear in the historical and prophetic records. The active record contains, at any given time, between forty and sixty threads. Most of those are Legendary Beasts, whose threads are structural by definition. The remainder are mortals whom the Loom has determined are currently necessary to the world's coherence.

Being Inscribed does not mean the character is protected from death. The Loom does not protect its active threads — it records them. But it does mean that a Stellar Cartographer who reads the character's Fate Number now sees something different: the Number surrounded by the faint additional notation that indicates active-record status. This is visible to anyone trained to read it. The Ashen Coil tracks the active record specifically, because removing an active-record thread is the most efficient way to destabilize the world's structure. Being Inscribed makes the character essential. It also makes them a priority target.

Loom Gifts — Choose One

Upon Inscription, the character receives one Loom Gift — an ability that no Path or Kindred provides, drawn directly from the Loom's active-record access. These are not magical abilities in the traditional sense. They are permissions — things the Loom now allows this character to do with the fabric of fate that it does not allow most mortals to touch.

Thread Reading
Loom Gift I
Once per Pulse as a free action, read the Fate Number and bond-status of any creature within 60 ft — not just the Number, but the full bond record. Who they are Fated to, what tier the bond has reached, whether any bonds are strained or inverted. This information comes instantly and requires no roll. The Loom considers this information available to its active-record members as a professional courtesy. Stellar Cartographers who encounter an Inscribed character using this ability and are able to perceive it report seeing the character's eyes briefly hold the same visual texture as the Loom's fabric — not metaphorically. Literally. For approximately one second.
Thread Anchoring
Loom Gift II
Once per Deep Attunement, when a creature within 30 ft would die — reaching 0 Vitality and failing their third Loom Test — use your Reflex to anchor their thread. They stabilize at 1 Vitality. The Loom holds the thread open for one additional Moment while the character's intervention is processed. This does not prevent death indefinitely — it provides one Moment's reprieve. If that Moment does not produce healing or stabilization, the thread closes as it would have. The Loom permits this intervention exactly once per thread, per Inscribed character. It does not permit the same life to be anchored twice.
Probability Sight
Loom Gift III
At the start of each encounter, the Concordance privately informs you of one thing that will definitely happen in this encounter if nothing changes — not might happen, will happen. You may act on this information. Your action may change it. The Loom shows you the thread's current trajectory, not its final position. This is the most useful and most disturbing Loom Gift. Inscribed characters who select it report that over time the visions begin arriving outside encounters — brief flashes of what is about to happen in ordinary situations. The Loom does not have an off switch. It is always running. Active-record members receive the broadcast whether or not they are in the relevant moment.
Thread Severance
Loom Gift IV — The Difficult One
Once per campaign, deliberately sever a Fated Bond — any Fated Bond, anywhere in the world, at any range. The bond does not weaken. It ends. Both parties experience the severance simultaneously as a physical sensation and then as absence. The Loom records the act in the historical record with a notation that no other act in the current era carries: Deliberate Thread Cutting by Active-Record Member.

This ability exists because sometimes a bond is the problem. Aethar's manufactured Starbound bonds in the Kallos-Sworn can be severed with it. A coercive bond used to control someone can be severed with it. A Void-Husk's residual bond to a living person, which the Coil uses as a tracking mechanism, can be severed with it.

It can also be used to sever genuine Fated Bonds between willing people. The Loom permits this. It does not approve. The distinction is subtle and important. Concordance guidance: this gift should only be selected by players who have thought about what they would use it for and are prepared for the Loom's notation to be permanent.
The Loom's Voice
Loom Gift V
Once per session, ask the Concordance one yes/no question about the current state of the world's Aetheric record — not what will happen, not what has happened, but what is currently true in the Loom's active record. "Is Grid-Point Seven currently reactivating?" "Does Serath of the Closed Circle currently know who the Quint Bond members are?" "Is the Resonant Binding ritual currently achievable?" The Loom answers accurately. It does not elaborate. It does not explain. Yes. No. The character works out the implications.
On Being Inscribed
The Loom Inscription is visible to those trained to read it and to all Legendary Beasts. The Ashen Coil's intelligence apparatus specifically tracks the active record. Serath of the Closed Circle knows the current active-record members by name. Aethar knows them by Fate Number. Being Inscribed is simultaneously the highest recognition the world's cosmic framework can offer a mortal and a specific kind of danger that most mortals do not face. The Concordance should use both.
Legendary Depth III
The Resonant
Trigger
The Quint Bond forms — all five members achieve full resonance simultaneously, their five Fate Numbers producing the five-point chord that the Loom's prophetic record has been building toward for four thousand years.
Boon Type: Kindred Evolution

When the Quint Bond forms, something happens to the five people involved that the world's existing biology cannot fully account for. The five Fate Numbers, resonating simultaneously at full depth, produce an Aetheric chord of sufficient power that it expresses itself physically. The Kindred evolves. Not into a different Kindred — the Human does not become something other than Human, the Eldren remains Eldren — but the Kindred's expression deepens past its own ceiling.

This happens to all five Quint Bond members simultaneously, regardless of whether they were present in the same location when the Bond formed. The evolution is immediate, complete, and permanent. The Stellar Cartographers who have modeled the Quint Bond's formation predict that an observer reading all five Fate Numbers at the moment of formation would see something unprecedented: five Numbers forming a perfect geometric figure in the Aetheric field, each one amplifying the others, the whole pattern briefly as bright as a Legendary Beast's thread.

Serath of the Closed Circle has identified three of the five Quint Bond members. She has not reported this to Aethar. She is watching to see what happens when the Bond forms. She has a theory about what the evolution produces, and she wants to know if she is right.

Legendary Depth IV
The Ascendant
Trigger
Completion of the Resonant Binding ritual — or an equivalent act of world-scale Aetheric commitment that the Loom classifies in the same register. The character must have achieved Legendary Depths I, II, and III before this trigger can resolve.
Boon Type: The Ascendant State

There is no precedent for this in the current era. The Loom's historical record contains two instances of the Ascendant state — one from the Kallos civilization's final years, whose practitioner's name was erased by the Ashen Coil from every written record they could reach, and one from Era II's opening decades, whose practitioner chose to become one of the Legendary Beasts and is therefore no longer technically a mortal. The Loom still carries both threads. They are the brightest entries in the historical record. The active record has never contained an Ascendant-state thread. If it did, the Loom's researchers believe the Loom itself would reorganize around it.

What the Ascendant state produces is not power in the conventional sense. A Depth 20 character with all three Legendary Depths and the Path Expansion, Loom Gift, and Kindred Evolution is already operating at a level most mortals cannot comprehend. The Ascendant state does not add to that. It changes the character's relationship to the world's fundamental architecture. They are no longer subject to it in the way mortals are subject to gravity — they understand it from the inside, and that understanding produces effects that look like power but are actually something more disquieting than power.

The Ascendant State — What It Provides
Loom Access
Ascendant · Permanent
The Ascendant may read the Loom's active record directly — not one question per session, not one flash of probability sight. Full, continuous access to the Loom's present-moment record. They always know the current state of every active thread, every Fated Bond at every tier, every Legendary Beast's current position and status, and the Aetheric health of the Ceque Line grid. This is not comfortable. The Loom processes an extraordinary amount of information simultaneously. The Ascendant's mind adapts — it has to — but the adaptation takes time and the Concordance should describe the early period as genuinely overwhelming. The Grand Prime Forgeman, who has been adjacent to this state for four centuries without reaching it, says he suspects it feels like suddenly being able to hear every conversation in a city simultaneously, then learning to focus.
Thread Weaving
Ascendant · 1/Session
Once per session, alter one thread in the Loom's active record — not sever it (that is the Loom Gift), but redirect it. Change one currently-true thing about a living being's Fate: their Fate Number moves by up to 5 points in either direction; an existing Fated Bond moves one tier in either direction (deeper or shallower); a bond that was strained becomes stable, or vice versa. The change is permanent. The Loom records it as an authorized alteration by an Ascendant-record member.

This ability changes what is cosmically true about a person. It is the closest any mortal has come to the Loom's own function. The Ascendant does not use it lightly. The Loom does not permit it to be used lightly — each use is recorded with the same notation as Thread Severance, and the Loom's active record reflects the change in a way that Stellar Cartographers at any distance can eventually perceive.
Aetheric Presence
Ascendant · Permanent
The Ascendant's Aetheric field has expanded to a radius of 300 ft — everything within that radius is subtly aware of them in a way that does not translate to conscious thought. Animals become calm or alert depending on the Ascendant's emotional state. Plants grow toward them. Water in containers moves toward the Ascendant-facing side. People within the field make all Warding Resonances against fear and compulsion with Resonant Fortune — the Ascendant's presence is stabilizing, not because they are doing anything, but because the world's fabric is denser near a point of Ascendant-level Aetheric coherence.

Enemies who attack the Ascendant feel the Aetheric field as physical resistance — all Strike Resonances against the Ascendant have Resonant Discord. This is not a shield. It is the world briefly reconsidering whether attacking this specific point in the fabric is a good idea.
The Name
Ascendant · Narrative
The Ascendant names themselves — not a new personal name, but a title that the Loom inscribes alongside their thread in the active record. This name becomes the way the world's Aetheric framework refers to them. Legendary Beasts know it. The Loom of Fate uses it. Stellar Cartographers reading the active record see it glowing next to the thread.

The last Ascendant to name themselves chose a name that the Ashen Coil subsequently erased from every written record. The Loom kept it. It is still there. The Concordance knows what it is. The players do not, unless their character has Loom Access and looks.

The name has no mechanical effect. It is the world's acknowledgment that this thread is no longer merely a mortal's thread. It is something the world will remember — not as history, but as present reality — for as long as the Loom runs.

The Loom runs forever. The Concordance should let the players sit with this for a moment before moving on.
What the Ascendant State Is Not
The Ascendant is not a god. The gods of Sideros lost their concentrated divine form during the Pantheonic Fall — they distributed their power into the Legendary Beasts rather than maintaining individual divine coherence. The Ascendant has done something different: a mortal has accumulated sufficient cosmic acknowledgment that the Loom has reclassified their thread. They are still mortal. They can still be killed. They have families, preferences, opinions, and bad days.

What they are is the world's most load-bearing mortal thread — the one the Loom's active record considers most necessary to the current structure. This is simultaneously the greatest recognition the world's cosmic framework can offer a living being and a specific kind of loneliness that no other mortal can fully understand.

The Grand Prime Forgeman, who has been adjacent to this state without reaching it, says this is probably fine. He has been saying this for four hundred years. The Concordance may decide whether to believe him.

The Legendary Depth system requires the Concordance to do something the standard rules do not: make unilateral decisions about when the world has decided a character is significant enough to acknowledge. This is a substantial creative responsibility. The following guidance is designed to make that responsibility feel manageable rather than paralyzing.

Legendary Depth I — The Beast Acknowledgment
What constitutes Beast Acknowledgment
The Beast has encountered the character doing something that expresses the Beast's domain at a level the Beast considers genuine. This is not about combat. A Nemean Lion Acknowledgment might come after the character protected someone genuinely innocent at significant personal cost. A Star-Metal Stag Acknowledgment might come after the character navigated something genuinely unmappable. The Beast is recognizing craft, not power.
What does not constitute Beast Acknowledgment
Defeating the Beast in combat. Completing a quest specifically designed to impress the Beast. Seeking out the Beast with acknowledgment as the stated goal. The Beast knows the difference between someone doing their work and someone performing for an audience. It has been a god. It is not impressed by performance.
Legendary Depth II — The Loom Inscription

Loom Inscription happens when the character's actions have made them structurally necessary to the world's current state — when removing them would change the shape of something important. The Concordance does not announce this. The Concordance simply describes, at an appropriate moment, that a Stellar Cartographer nearby has noticed something unusual about the character's Fate Number. The character may or may not understand what this means. The world has already changed.

Typical triggers: Preventing Grid-Point Seven's full reactivation. Disrupting a Coil operation that the world's Aetheric record considered likely to succeed. Forming a genuine Triad-tier Fated Bond that the Loom's prophetic record had not anticipated. The Loom Inscription is the world's acknowledgment that this character is not moving through history — they are shaping it.

Legendary Depth III — The Quint Bond

The Concordance controls when the Quint Bond forms. It forms when all five members have achieved the depth of relationship with each other that the Loom considers sufficient — not when the players have identified all five members, not when the party has been together for a certain number of sessions. The Bond forms when it is ready. The Concordance will know when this is — the five-person dynamic will have reached a quality that feels different from ordinary party cohesion. When it does, the Concordance describes the moment, all five players experience the Kindred Evolution simultaneously, and the world changes.

Legendary Depth IV — The Ascendant

The Resonant Binding ritual is a campaign-defining event. The Concordance should treat LD IV as the campaign's endpoint — not because the campaign must end there, but because the Ascendant state changes the world significantly enough that the campaign that existed before it cannot continue in the same form. A party containing one or more Ascendant-state characters is no longer in the same story they started in. The Concordance should begin planning the post-Ascendant campaign long before the ritual completes.

The Fundamental Rule of Legendary Depth
Legendary Depth is never awarded. It is recognized. The Concordance does not decide that a character deserves LD I and then arrange the Beast Acknowledgment encounter. The Concordance watches the character doing their work in the world, recognizes when that work has reached the threshold, and describes the world's response. The distinction matters: awards can be engineered and anticipated. Recognition arrives when it arrives, and its arrival is meaningful precisely because it was not scheduled.

When a player asks "how do I get Legendary Depth?" the correct answer is: do the work. The world is watching.
