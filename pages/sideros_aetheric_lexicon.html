Sideros / Astrolan — Original Game Terminology

The Aetheric Lexicon

Every mechanical term replaced with language native to the world. The rules and the fiction are now the same thing.

The Governing Principle
Every term should teach the world
In Sideros, magic is not an abstract resource — it is Aetheric resonance with cosmic forces. A character does not "roll an Active Resonance" — they test their Resonance against the difficulty of aligning with or overcoming a cosmic force. When that test succeeds narrowly, the Loom held the thread. When it fails, the thread slipped.

This is not cosmetic renaming. The terminology determines how players think about what they're doing. A player who rolls a "Warding Resonance" is passively reacting. A player who makes a Warding Resonance is actively aligning their soul-frequency against a threatening force. Same mechanic. Completely different relationship to the fiction.

The principle throughout: terms derived from Sideros lore take precedence over neutral descriptors. Where a D&D term is purely functional ("Instinct"), the Sideros replacement reflects what that action actually is in-world ("swift strike," "reflex," "instinct"). Where a D&D term carries embedded assumptions ("Aetheric Channel" implies a container), the replacement carries Sideros assumptions ("Aetheric channel" implies flow and resonance).
The Three-Layer System
Sideros runs in three mechanical modes, all using the same terminology:

Full Resonance Mode — the complete system for dedicated TTRPG campaigns. All terms below apply. Six Resonance Attributes, full Aetheric Channel system, three Character Tracks, complete Condition suite.

Condensed Resonance Mode — for one-shots, new players, or faster play. Three Character Tracks (Body, Mind, Soul) replace the full HP/resource economy. Simplified Resonance Tests with fixed difficulties.

Loom Narrative Mode — pure fiction-forward play. Resonance Tests only for pivotal moments. No tracking. The Concordance (GM) drives pacing entirely by the world's logic.
How the Game Resolves Anything
The Resonance Test — replacing "Active Resonance" and "Warding Resonance"
Every test in Sideros is a Resonance Test — the character aligns their soul-frequency with a cosmic force and discovers whether the alignment holds. The d20 represents the Loom's thread: how well fate is cooperating in this moment.

There are two kinds of Resonance Test:

Active Resonance — the character initiates. They are attempting to align with a cosmic force to achieve something. Replaces Active Resonance.

Warding Resonance — the character responds. A force moves against them and they must align against it. Replaces Warding Resonance. The difference matters in fiction: a Warding Resonance is not passive failure — it is the active assertion of one's soul-frequency against something that would displace it.

vs. RESONANCE THRESHOLD set by the Concordance

WARDING RESONANCE = d20 + Warding Attribute + Mastery Bonus
vs. WARDING THRESHOLD set by the source of the threat
Resonant Fortune (replaces Resonant Fortune) — the character rolls two d20s and takes the higher. Occurs when cosmic forces align behind the character's intent — a Fated Bond member nearby, a Zodiac Feat active, the right cultural tradition being applied.

Resonant Discord (replaces Resonant Discord) — the character rolls two d20s and takes the lower. Occurs when cosmic forces work against the character — wrong zodiac season, Void-Touched Exposure accumulated, acting against the Loom's evident intent.
The Six Resonance Attributes — replacing Ability Scores
Every character has six Resonance Attributes, each measuring a different dimension of how their soul aligns with cosmic forces. Each has a modifier (called Resonance Modifier) equal to (score − 10) ÷ 2, rounded down — identical math, original framing.
Replaces Strength
Forcefulness
Raw physical power and Alpha-aspected momentum. The force with which you move through the world. Used for lifting, breaking, striking with power, grappling, overcoming physical resistance. The Primal Jarl's primary Attribute.
Replaces Dexterity
Swiftness
Speed, precision, and the Beta-aspected grace of function. How fluidly you move through space and execute complex physical sequences. Used for ranged attacks, stealth, sleight of hand, avoiding danger.
Replaces Constitution
Endurance
Physical resilience and the body's capacity to sustain cosmic force. How much the mortal form can bear before it breaks. Determines Vitality pool size. The Khalkotauros's gift to those born under Taurus.
Replaces Intelligence
Acuity
The analytical mind's capacity to read patterns and apply knowledge. How precisely you perceive and reason. Used for arcane tests, history, nature, investigation. The Comet-Mage's primary Attribute.
Replaces Wisdom
Attunement
Omega-aspected cosmic perception — the soul's sensitivity to the Aether and to other people. How clearly you feel what is true beneath what appears. Used for healing, insight, perception, warding resonances vs. mental forces. The Rāśi-Siddha's primary Attribute.
Replaces Charisma
Resonance
The soul's magnetic frequency — how powerfully your presence affects others. The Alpha-aspected force of personality. Used for persuasion, performance, deception, intimidation, and all Fated Bond Active Resonances. The Stellar Cartographer's primary Attribute.
Mastery Bonus — replacing Mastery Bonus
The Mastery Bonus reflects how deeply a character has attuned to their path — not their raw experience, but the depth of their cosmic alignment. It increases at the same progression as D&D Mastery Bonus (starts at +2, increases at levels 5, 9, 13, 17).

Skills, weapons, and abilities are described as Mastered (full Mastery Bonus), Half-Mastered (half Mastery Bonus), or Unmastered (no bonus). Replaces Mastery, Half-Mastery, and no Mastery.
Resonance Thresholds — replacing Difficulty Classes (DC)
The Resonance Threshold is the cosmic resistance a character must overcome. It is set by the Concordance based on how aligned or opposed the task is to the character's Aetheric nature.

Standard thresholds: Simple (8) · Moderate (12) · Demanding (16) · Formidable (20) · Legendary (25) · Void-Touched (30). The names reflect what the task demands of the soul, not just the difficulty.
What a Character Is and Has
D&D Term
Vitalitys
→
Sideros Term
Vitality
From Latin vita — life. The body's finite reservoir of physical integrity.
Vitality is not abstract "health" — it is the literal vitality that flows through a mortal form. When it depletes, the body is failing, not "taking damage." Recovering Vitality is not healing damage — it is restoring the flow of life-force to damaged tissue. Maximum Vitality = (Endurance modifier × character level) + Endurance score. Identical math to D&D Vitality calculation.
Old: "You take 8 Vitalitys of damage."
New: "Your Vitality drops by 8 — the blow found something real."
D&D Term
Temporary Vitalitys
→
Sideros Term
Aetheric Buffer
A shell of Aetheric energy that absorbs damage before it reaches Vitality — not life itself, but a resonant shield borrowed from the cosmic field.
Aetheric Buffers don't stack. The highest applies. They don't restore Vitality — they simply delay its loss. When the Buffer is gone, all subsequent damage strikes Vitality directly. Bond abilities, zodiac feats, and Tapas expenditures can generate Aetheric Buffers. The Nemean Lion's feat generates a Buffer for all nearby allies when a creature is reduced to 0 Vitality.
Old: "You gain 8 temporary Vitalitys."
New: "An Aetheric Buffer of 8 surrounds you — it won't last, but it will hold for now."
D&D Term
Guard Rating
→
Sideros Term
Guard Rating
The threshold an attacker must overcome to land a meaningful strike. A combination of physical armor, Aetheric deflection, and trained reflexes.
Guard Rating reflects the total difficulty of landing a meaningful blow — not just armor, but positioning, awareness, and cosmic attunement. An Omega Rāśi-Siddha without armor has a high Guard Rating because their Attunement allows them to shift out of harm's way before it arrives. Guard Rating = 10 + armor value + Swiftness modifier (light armor), Endurance modifier (heavy armor), or Attunement modifier (unarmored Siddha).
Old: "What's your AC?"
New: "What's your Guard Rating?"
D&D Term
Level
→
Sideros Term
Resonance Depth
How deeply the character has attuned to their cosmic path. Not experience accumulated but resonance achieved.
Resonance Depth 1 is a novice whose soul-frequency has barely begun to align. Resonance Depth 20 is a mortal who has achieved near-Avatar-tier cosmic attunement. Epic Tier begins at Resonance Depth 21 — the point where the character's alignment becomes recognizable to the Legendary Beasts and the Loom itself. "Gaining a level" is "deepening your Resonance."
Old: "I just hit Level 5."
New: "My Resonance Depth reached 5 — the Bond abilities are changing."
D&D Term
Class
→
Sideros Term
Path
The cosmic road a character walks — not a profession, but a fundamental orientation of the soul toward a type of cosmic force.
A character doesn't "choose a class" — they find, or are found by, their Path. The Rāśi-Siddha Path finds those whose souls resonate with internal discipline. The Comet-Mage Path finds those whose minds naturally systematize cosmic knowledge. The Path is not a job. It is recognition. "Path features" replaces "class features." "Path ability" replaces "class ability."
Old: "What class are you?"
New: "Which Path have you been called to?"
D&D Term
Subclass
→
Sideros Term
Calling
The specific expression of a Path — the more particular cosmic force the character has been called to serve within their broader alignment.
A Comet-Mage has a Calling to one of the Three Conclaves. A Rāśi-Siddha has a Calling to one of the twelve Scattered Temples. A Primal Jarl has a Calling to a specific ancestral tradition (Pine Shadow, Deep Earth, Norse Hearth, etc.). The Calling is the narrowing of the Path into a specific relationship with the Aether.
D&D Term
Feat
→
Sideros Term
Attunement
A permanent deepening of the character's cosmic alignment — not a skill learned, but a resonance recognized and integrated.
The twelve Zodiac Attunements are the primary Attunements in Sideros. Cultural Variant Attunements are advanced versions. Other Attunements exist for weapon mastery, bond deepening, and Path-specific cosmic alignment. The word "feat" implies achievement through effort. "Attunement" implies the character has become able to receive something the cosmos was already offering.
D&D Term
Skill
→
Sideros Term
Discipline
A specific area of trained Aetheric alignment — the narrowing of an Attribute into a particular domain of application.
Disciplines are grouped under their governing Attribute. Forcefulness governs: Athletics, Intimidation. Swiftness governs: Acrobatics, Stealth, Sleight of Hand. Attunement governs: Perception, Insight, Medicine, Animal Handling, Survival. Acuity governs: History, Arcana (now: Aetheric Lore), Investigation, Nature. Resonance governs: Persuasion, Deception, Performance. Endurance has no Disciplines — it is expressed through Vitality and Warding Resonances.
Old: "I'm Proficient in Arcana and Perception."
New: "I've Mastered Aetheric Lore and Perception."
D&D Term
Background
→
Sideros Term
Origin
The thread of the character's life before the Path found them — where they came from, what shaped them, and what the Loom was doing with them before they knew to look.
The five Origin categories from the Player's Guide become: Celestial/Academic → Scholarly Origin; Silk Road/Trade → Merchant Origin; Urban/Intrigue → Shadow Origin; Wilderness/Frontier → Wanderer Origin; Branded/Haunted → Marked Origin. Each Origin grants two Mastered Disciplines, one tool or language, and a starting Origin Ability.
D&D Term
Race / Species
→
Sideros Term
Kindred
The cosmic lineage a character was born into — the specific Aetheric frequency their people carry through blood, culture, and ancestral resonance.
"Race" carries real-world baggage. "Species" is clinical. "Kindred" captures that these are not biological classifications but cosmic lineages — the Eldren Kindred carry Kallos-era resonance, the Dunmarim Kindred carry the deep-earth frequency, the Wolf-Shifter Kindred carry the Omote-Ura dual nature. The Celestial Synergy system becomes the Kindred-Zodiac Resonance table — specific combinations of Kindred and zodiac sign that produce unique Attunements unavailable through any other combination.
D&D Term
Mastery (in weapons/tools)
→
Sideros Term
Mastery
The depth of alignment between the character and a specific instrument of cosmic force. A weapon Mastered by a character resonates with their Aetheric frequency — it becomes an extension of their will.
Characters have Mastery in weapon categories (bladed, blunt, ranged, etc.), tools, vehicles, and instruments. Full Mastery adds Mastery Bonus to attack and damage. Half-Mastery adds half. Unmastered weapons are used with Resonant Discord on attack rolls.
D&D Term
Passive Perception
→
Sideros Term
Ambient Attunement
The constant low-level cosmic awareness a character maintains without effort — the background hum of their soul reading the Aether.
Ambient Attunement = 10 + Attunement modifier + Mastery Bonus (if Perception is Mastered). It represents what a character notices without trying — not a test, but a threshold the world must meet to sneak something past them. An Omega character with high Attunement has a naturally high Ambient Attunement — they are simply more sensitive to the cosmic field.
What a Character Does in a Moment
D&D Term
Action
→
Sideros Term
Act
Kept simple and direct. "Your Act" is the primary thing you do in a Moment. It frames agency without jargon. "Take an action" becomes "use your Act." "Action economy" becomes "Act economy." Clean enough that it requires no explanation to a new player.
D&D Term
Instinct
→
Sideros Term
Instinct
Something done without deliberation — the body or the Aether moving before the mind decides.
"Instinct" implies something extra. "Instinct" implies something that happens because the character is attuned enough to do two things simultaneously. Wolf-Shifters shifting form is an Instinct — not a bonus, but a reflex of their nature. A Rāśi-Siddha spending Tapas on a quick purification uses their Instinct. This reframes the resource as cosmic attunement rather than an action budget surplus.
Old: "I use my Instinct to..."
New: "As an Instinct, I..."
D&D Term
Reaction
→
Sideros Term
Reflex
The soul responding to an external event before conscious decision intervenes.
A Reflex is the Aether moving through the character in response to a trigger. The Cancer Zodiac Attunement's protective ability is a Reflex. Opportunity attacks become Reflex Strikes. Shield spells and similar abilities become Warding Reflexes. One Reflex per round, refreshed at the start of each Moment.
D&D Term
Turn / Round
→
Sideros Term
Moment / Pulse
A Moment is one character's slice of a Pulse — the full cycle of action and response that constitutes one beat of combat.
A Pulse is one full round of combat — every character acts once. A character's Moment is their portion of the Pulse. Initiative order becomes Pulse Order. "At the start of your turn" becomes "at the start of your Moment." "End of turn" becomes "end of your Moment." The Pulse/Moment distinction mirrors the Bond system — a Pulse is the world breathing; a Moment is one soul's contribution to that breath.
D&D Term
Initiative
→
Sideros Term
Pulse Order
The sequence in which the Loom's threads pull each participant through the Pulse — determined by cosmic attunement, not just reflexes.
Pulse Order = d20 + Swiftness modifier. Ties broken by Attunement modifier (the more cosmically attuned character senses the Pulse first). The Aries Zodiac Attunement guarantees first position in the first Pulse of any encounter — a mechanical expression of Aries as the force of initiative.
D&D Term
Attack Roll
→
Sideros Term
Strike Resonance
The test of whether the character's force-intent aligns with the cosmic opening to land a meaningful blow.
A Strike Resonance is not "did I hit" — it is "did the Aether allow this strike to find its mark." Against a skilled opponent, even a perfectly aimed blow can fail if their Guard Rating exceeds your Strike Resonance. Ranged Strike Resonance uses Swiftness. Melee Strike Resonance uses Forcefulness or Swiftness (character's choice based on fighting style). Spell Strike Resonance uses the casting Attribute.
Old: "I roll to attack. That's a 17."
New: "Strike Resonance — 17. Does that break their Guard?"
D&D Term
Critical Hit
→
Sideros Term
Resonant Strike
A moment when the Aether fully aligned with the character's intent — the strike found not just the body but the cosmic vulnerability behind it.
A Resonant Strike occurs on a natural 20. Double all damage dice. Additionally: the Concordance describes a meaningful narrative consequence beyond the mechanical damage — a weapon finding a weak point in armor, a strike that disrupts the target's Sustained Resonance on a ritual, a blow that sends the target stumbling into a more dangerous position. A Resonant Strike is never just numbers.
D&D Term
Death Warding Resonance
→
Sideros Term
Loom Test
When Vitality reaches zero, the character is at the Loom's mercy — each Moment, the Loom decides whether their thread holds or cuts.
A Loom Test is a d20 roll with no modifiers — the character's personal attributes mean nothing at this threshold. The Loom decides. Three successes (10 or higher) before three failures stabilizes the character. Three failures cuts the thread. A natural 20 restores 1 Vitality and the character regains consciousness. A natural 1 counts as two failures. A Bonded partner within range converts one Loom Test failure to a success per Pulse — the bond's weight on the Loom.
D&D Term
Brief Attunement / Deep Attunement
→
Sideros Term
Brief Attunement / Deep Attunement
Resting in Sideros is not passive recovery — it is active re-attunement to the Aether. The body heals faster when the soul is properly resonating.
A Brief Attunement (1 hour, no combat) allows spending Vitality Dice to restore Vitality, and restores resources marked "Brief Attunement" (Tapas Points if meditation is maintained, certain Instinct abilities). A Deep Attunement (8 hours, includes sleep) fully restores Vitality, all Aetheric Channels, all Tapas Points, and resets the Omote-Ura Gauge to clan default. Siddha who spend their Brief Attunement in genuine meditation (not just resting) restore Tapas at the meditative rate.
D&D Term
Legendary Resistance
→
Sideros Term
Cosmic Resilience
The capacity of a being of sufficient cosmic weight to simply refuse a Warding Resonance failure — the thread is too thick to cut by ordinary means.
Legendary Beasts have 3–5 uses of Cosmic Resilience per Pulse cycle (resets at start of each Pulse). When a Legendary Beast fails a Warding Resonance, it may expend one use to treat the failure as a success. This does not feel like magic to observers — it feels like the creature being fundamentally unwilling to be affected by that force. The Concordance describes it in narrative terms, never mechanically.
How the Aether Moves Through a Character
D&D Term
Aetheric Channel
→
Sideros Term
Aetheric Channel
A conduit through which the character draws cosmic force and directs it. Not a container that holds power — a channel that opens and closes.
"Aetheric Channel" implies a storage unit. An Aetheric Channel implies a conduit — when you open a Channel, the Aether flows through you in a structured way. Channels close after use and reopen after Deep Attunement. Higher-tier Channels require deeper attunement with the cosmic force — they're not "bigger containers" but "wider conduits" that let more of the Aether through at once. Channel tiers are numbered identically to Aetheric Channels (1st through 9th, then 10th–12th for Epic Tier Zodiac Priests).
Old: "I expend a 3rd-level Aetheric Channel."
New: "I open a third-tier Aetheric Channel."
D&D Term
Spell
→
Sideros Term
Working (varies by tradition)
Each tradition has its own term for what it does when it channels the Aether — because they understand the act differently.
Comet-Mage: Formulation (a precise calculation of cosmic forces)
Rāśi-Siddha: Mantra (a vibrational alignment with a zodiac domain)
Orisha Ashé-Medium: Invocation (calling the Orisha's aspect forward)
Tuathan Fade-Guardian: Rite (a seasonal act of Otherworld bargaining)
Zodiac Priest: Benediction (a cosmic law being expressed through a mortal vessel)
Scion-Pledged: Blight (a corruption of Aetheric flow into entropic force)
Star-Born: Surge (an involuntary expression of the character's cosmic nature)

When speaking across traditions, the neutral term is simply Working. "Cast a spell" becomes "enact a Working."
D&D Term
Spellcasting Ability
→
Sideros Term
Weaving Attribute
The Resonance Attribute through which a character's tradition directs Aetheric force.
Each Path has a designated Weaving Attribute: Comet-Mage uses Acuity; Rāśi-Siddha uses Attunement; Ashé-Medium uses Resonance; Zodiac Priest uses Attunement; Star-Born uses Resonance; Scion-Pledged uses Resonance (or Acuity — chosen at Path selection). Working Resonance Threshold = 8 + Mastery Bonus + Weaving Attribute modifier. Working Strike Resonance = d20 + Mastery Bonus + Weaving Attribute modifier. The naming is intentional: the Loom weaves fate; practitioners weave the Aether. Both are acts of giving structure to raw cosmic force. Aethar wants to unweave both. The Resonant Binding is the Kallos-Sworn's attempt to re-weave what is fraying.
D&D Term
Concentration
→
Sideros Term
Sustained Resonance
A Working that requires the character to maintain their Aetheric Channel open — actively holding the cosmic force in shape rather than releasing it in a burst.
Sustained Resonance Workings are marked (Sustained). Taking damage while Sustaining requires a Warding Resonance (Endurance) — Resonance Threshold 10 or half the damage taken, whichever is higher. Failure collapses the Sustained Working. Only one Sustained Resonance at a time, unless a Zodiac Attunement or Epic Tier ability states otherwise. "Break Sustained Resonance" becomes "collapse the Sustained Resonance."
D&D Term
Ambient Working
→
Sideros Term
Ambient Working
A Working so aligned with the character's natural Aetheric frequency that it requires no Channel to enact — the Aether simply responds to the intent.
Ambient Workings are free — no Aetheric Channel required, unlimited uses. They represent the character's deepest alignment with their Path's cosmic domain. A Comet-Mage who has studied fire formulations for a decade can produce a minor fire Ambient Working without effort. "Ambient Working" implies trivial magic. "Ambient Working" implies magic so deeply integrated it is simply how that character relates to the world.
D&D Term
Attunement (to magic items)
→
Sideros Term
Resonance Binding (to an artifact)
When a character and an Aetheric artifact align their frequencies — the artifact becomes an extension of the character's cosmic alignment rather than an external tool.
Resonance Binding to an artifact requires a Brief Attunement spent in contact with the item. Maximum 3 Resonance Bindings at once — more than 3 means the character's Aetheric field is fragmented across too many objects to maintain coherence. "Attuning to a magic item" becomes "establishing Resonance Binding with an artifact." Note: this term now has double meaning — heroes should note the distinction between Resonance Binding to an artifact vs. Arch-Oracle Lyra's Resonant Binding ritual.

Condensed Resonance Mode — Three Character Tracks
Replacing the HeroQuest Body/Mind Point System
For streamlined play, each character tracks three pools instead of the full Vitality system. This is not a lesser mode — it is a different focus. The three tracks reflect what Sideros believes a mortal is: a body that acts, a mind that perceives, and a soul-thread that holds them together.
First Track
Body
Physical integrity. Reduced by weapon strikes, environmental hazards, and physical harm. Equal to 8 + Endurance modifier per character depth level (simplified). Restored by Kanya-tradition healing, Vitality Dice spent during Brief Attunement, or magical restoration.
At 0: Loom-Suspended. Loom Tests each Pulse until stabilized or thread cuts.
Second Track
Mind
Mental integrity and Aetheric clarity. Reduced by psychic attacks, Mycelian Resonance exposure, Void-Touched Exposure accumulation, and witnessing certain cosmic horrors. Equal to 6 + Attunement modifier per depth level. Restored by Deep Attunement and certain Oshun-tradition workings.
At 0: Resonance-Shattered for 1 hour, then gain a Madness (Concordance designs specific to the source).
Third Track
Soul
Aetheric integrity — the thread's strength in the Loom. Reduced by Lead-Scale Corruption accumulation, Bond-Severed events, direct Void-energy exposure, and certain Ashen Coil rituals. Equal to Fate Number ÷ 10 (rounded up) per depth level. Restored only by significant narrative acts of Aetheric alignment.
At 0: Fate Number begins dissolving toward Void-Husk. The character is not dead — they are becoming something else.
Simplified Resonance Thresholds
In Condensed Resonance Mode, all Active Resonances use three thresholds instead of the full Discipline system:
BODY RESONANCE: d20 + Forcefulness or Swiftness modifier
MIND RESONANCE: d20 + Acuity or Attunement modifier
SOUL RESONANCE: d20 + Resonance modifier + Fate Number bonus*

* Fate Number bonus = (Fate Number ÷ 20), rounded down, maximum +5

The Language in Use — Before and After
Before — D&D 5e Language
The rogue uses her Instinct to dash behind the pillar. She has Resonant Fortune on her attack roll since the target can't see her — a 19, which beats the guard's AC of 14. She deals 2d6+4 piercing damage and the target must make a DC 14 Endurance Warding Resonance or be stunned until the end of its next turn. The guard fails with a 9. Meanwhile, the wizard expends a 3rd-level Aetheric Channel to cast Fireball, targeting the Sustained Resonance on his Hold Person spell.
After — Sideros Aetheric Lexicon
The Fate-Breaker uses her Instinct to slip behind the pillar. She has Resonant Fortune on her Strike Resonance since the sentry cannot perceive her — a 19, which breaks the sentry's Guard Rating of 14. She deals 2d6+4 piercing damage and the target must make a Warding Resonance (Endurance, Threshold 14) or be Resonance-Shattered until the end of its next Moment. The sentry fails with a 9. Meanwhile, the Comet-Mage opens a third-tier Aetheric Channel to enact a fire Formulation, careful to maintain his Sustained Resonance on the binding Working he currently holds.
Character Creation — Sideros Language
Choose your Kindred — the cosmic lineage you were born into. Then select your Path — the cosmic road the Aether has called you toward. Your Resonance Depth begins at 1. Assign your six Resonance Attributes: Forcefulness, Swiftness, Endurance, Acuity, Attunement, and Resonance. Your Mastery Bonus begins at +2. Choose your Origin — the life before the Path found you — and receive two Mastered Disciplines. Finally, roll your Fate's Mate Number (1d100) and your Primal Essence (1d6: 1 = Alpha, 2–5 = Beta, 6 = Omega). Record your zodiac sign. At Resonance Depth 4, you may take the Zodiac Attunement corresponding to your sign — provided you have encountered your sign's Legendary Beast at least once.
Concordance (GM) Guidance Text
When a character fails a Loom Test, do not describe it neutrally. The Loom is deciding. Each failed test is the thread thinning. Each success is the thread holding against something that wants to cut it. At three failures, the thread does not break violently — it simply releases. The character's soul-frequency withdraws from the body the way a tide withdraws from sand. There is no drama in it. The drama is in the Bonded party members who felt the moment their partner's Resonant Awareness went still.
