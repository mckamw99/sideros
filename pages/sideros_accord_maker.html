The Accord-Maker

Dunmarim · Jade Empire · The Living Machine Tradition

They do not channel the Aether. They negotiate with it — building agreements between what the Aether wants to do and what physical matter can sustain.

★ Original Path Vitality Die: d8 Beta (Alchemist · Architect) Alpha (Armorer · Siege Engineer) Dual Resource

Every other Path in Sideros approaches the Aether with some form of submission — the Comet-Mage studies it, the Zodiac Priest serves it, the Star-Born was born into it, the Scion-Pledged bargains with things that channel it. The Accord-Maker does none of these things. They approach the Aether the way a Dunmarim master-smith approaches a difficult alloy: with precise knowledge of its properties, respect for what it will and won't do, and no illusions about who is in charge of the conversation. The Aether is not in charge. The Accord-Maker is.

The tradition emerged from two independent sources that arrived at the same conclusion by different routes. The Dunmarim apothecaries discovered, centuries after the Pantheonic Fall, that certain mineral combinations created chemical reactions that produced Aetheric effects without requiring a practitioner to weave — the Aether simply responded to the right physical conditions. They called this Accord: the Aether agreeing to participate in a pre-arranged arrangement. Meanwhile, the Jade Empire's Gear-Forged engineers were discovering the same principle at the macro scale — a Resonant Geode in the right physical housing didn't need a weaver to direct it. The housing itself was the direction.

The Accord-Maker tradition formalized when Dunmarim travelers and Jade Empire engineers met in the Stone Kingdom's deep workshops, recognized what the other was doing, and spent three generations arguing about methodology before producing the first unified practitioners. Modern Accord-Makers carry both traditions simultaneously — the Dunmarim precision with physical materials and the Jade Empire's understanding of how Resonant architecture sustains Aetheric agreements without continuous weaving. An Accord-Maker is the only practitioner in Sideros who can work effectively inside the Stillness of Dunmar — because their power doesn't require the Aether to cooperate in real time. The agreement was made before they arrived. It holds.

The Core Distinction
Accord-Makers are the only Weave-capable Path that can function in Aether-Dampening Ore zones. Their Infusions and Blueprints are pre-negotiated agreements — the Aether committed to them before the suppression field existed. The field suppresses active weaving. It cannot un-make a deal already struck. This makes Accord-Makers uniquely valuable in Dunmarim territory and uniquely threatening to the Order of Pure Reason's anti-magic strategy.
Relationship to Gear-Forged
Every Gear-Forged who is not a Geode-Pilot should strongly consider the Accord-Maker Path. The two traditions share deep roots — an Accord-Maker Gear-Forged can Infuse their own frame components, create Blueprints for Geode-based constructions, and access the Grand Prime Forgeman's Precursor knowledge through a shared technical language. The Grand Prime Forgeman himself walks this Path. He has been an Accord-Maker for four centuries. No one knows which Calling he follows.
Relationship to Dunmarim
Dunmarim who take the Accord-Maker Path are unusual — their Kindred trait prevents them from weaving Workings, but Accord-Maker Infusions and Blueprints are not Workings in the traditional sense. The Concordance should rule that Dunmarim Accord-Makers can use the full Path, since they are negotiating with the Aether rather than weaving it. This is the one exception to the Dunmarim's no-weaving constraint — and it is the subject of significant theological debate in Ironhearth.
Tools as focus
An Accord-Maker's artisan tools are their Weaving focus — they cannot produce Infusions or activate Blueprints without their tools present. Unlike a staff or crystal, the tools are not decorative. They are load-bearing. A Dunmarim smith's tools set and a Jade Empire calibration kit serve the same function through different cultural expressions. Losing your tools mid-adventure is a genuine problem — not just a narrative inconvenience.
Dunmarim Tradition
Infusions
Pre-negotiated Aetheric agreements applied to worn or carried objects — weapons, armor, tools, and focus items. An Infusion is not a permanent enchantment and not a temporary Working. It is a sustained contract between the object and a specific Aetheric property, maintained by the Accord-Maker's ongoing technical attention. Refreshed on Deep Attunement. Maximum active Infusions scales with Resonance Depth.
⚙
Jade Empire Tradition
Blueprints
Larger-scale Aetheric constructions — turrets, constructs, alchemical stations, defensive installations — built during rest from prepared schematics. A Blueprint produces a physical object that operates autonomously according to the agreement built into its design. Blueprints are permanent until destroyed; their Aetheric agreements don't require the Accord-Maker's presence to continue.
Infusion Rules
INFUSION SLOTS = Resonance Depth ÷ 2 (rounded up) · Maximum 10 at Depth 20
Each Infusion applied to one item. One item can hold only one Infusion.
All Infusions end simultaneously on Deep Attunement — then reapplied fresh.
An Infusion applied to an item you don't own requires the owner's willing consent.
In Aether-Dampening Ore zones: Infusions persist. New Infusions cannot be applied.
Applying an Infusion
Requires 1 hour of work with artisan's tools and physical contact with the item throughout. The Accord-Maker must have the Infusion in their known list (see Infusion List tab). No Aetheric Channel cost — Infusions are pre-negotiated, not actively woven. During a Brief Attunement, the Accord-Maker may apply Infusions instead of resting — they cannot do both simultaneously.
Infusion Limits
The Accord-Maker knows a number of Infusion types equal to their Resonance Depth + Acuity modifier. They may apply a number of active Infusions equal to their Infusion Slots. Knowing an Infusion and having an active slot for it are separate resources — an Accord-Maker may know 12 Infusion types but only have 6 active slots.
Blueprint Rules
BLUEPRINTS KNOWN = Resonance Depth ÷ 3 (rounded up) · Maximum 7 at Depth 20
BLUEPRINTS ACTIVE = Resonance Depth ÷ 4 (rounded up) · Maximum 5 at Depth 20
Construction time: 1 hour per Blueprint tier during Deep Attunement or downtime.
Constructed objects persist until destroyed — they do not expire.
Replacing a destroyed Blueprint costs materials (see Blueprint List) but not additional time.
Building from a Blueprint
Requires artisan's tools, the specified materials (listed per Blueprint), and uninterrupted work time. The result is a physical object with Aetheric properties built in. The Aetheric agreement is in the object's structure — anyone can use it, not just the Accord-Maker, though some Blueprints are calibrated to the Accord-Maker's specific frequency and require their presence to activate.
Blueprints vs. Infusions
Infusions enhance existing objects. Blueprints create new ones. An Accord-Maker can apply an Infusion to a Blueprint-created object — the two systems stack, at the cost of using both an Infusion slot and a Blueprint active slot for the combined item. A fully Infused Blueprint construct is the Accord-Maker's highest expression of craft — a new object that both exists and is enhanced beyond its base design.
The Stillness Exception — Critical Rule
Inside the Stillness of Dunmar (maximum Aether-Dampening Ore Sustained Resonance): existing Infusions persist, existing Blueprints function normally, and existing constructs continue operating. The Accord-Maker cannot apply new Infusions, construct new Blueprints, or use Path Features that require active weaving. But everything already built continues to work. A well-prepared Accord-Maker who enters the Stillness is not significantly weakened. An unprepared one loses access to all their tools in a single step. Preparation is the entire Path.

The Homunculus Companion — Extended Rules
The Homunculus Servant is the Accord-Maker's answer to the animal companion and familiar. Unlike the Primal Accord's Beast Resonance or a conventional Working familiar, the Homunculus is a constructed being — it has no cosmic lineage, no Fate Number, no zodiac sign. It is the purest expression of Accord-Maker philosophy: a thing that works because it was built to work.

Statistics: Tiny construct. Vitality = 1 + Accord-Maker's Acuity modifier (increases by 1 at Depths 5, 9, 13, 17). Guard Rating 13. Immune to poison, disease, and the Corrupted condition. Does not need to eat, breathe, or sleep.

Actions: Acts on the Accord-Maker's Moment as an Instinct. Can: move up to 40 ft (fly or walk); deliver a touch Working the Accord-Maker has prepared; make a Force Strike (1d4 force, +Mastery Bonus to Strike Resonance); use its Perception to extend the Accord-Maker's senses (the Accord-Maker may see and hear through it as an Instinct).

Destruction and replacement: When the Homunculus is destroyed, the Accord-Maker can create a replacement during a Deep Attunement without consuming another gem — the Aetheric agreement was with the Accord-Maker, not with the specific physical form. The Homunculus remembers everything from its previous existence.

Blueprint Materials — Procurement
Blueprint materials are not abstract GP costs — they are specific physical components that must be sourced. The Concordance should require players to actually acquire the materials rather than simply spending gold. Relevant sources: Dunmarim trading houses (metals, precision tools), Jade Empire merchants (crystal arrays, Resonant components), Serpent-Kin Petal-Weavers (Weep-Stone), rare material specialists in Port Damaris. An Accord-Maker who establishes trade relationships with these suppliers is doing campaign infrastructure work — the supplier relationships become plot hooks, and the materials become leverage in faction negotiations.
Aetheric Tools & Infusion System
Depth 1
The Accord-Maker's artisan tools become Aetherically attuned — functioning as a Weaving focus for all Path Features and Workings. The Infusion system becomes active. Begin with 2 known Infusion types (chosen from the Depth 1 list) and 2 active Infusion slots. Learn 2 additional Infusion types at each Resonance Depth.

Weaving from tools: The Accord-Maker may weave Workings with a casting time of 1 Action by incorporating the Working into their tool use — the Weaving is part of the crafting motion. They must have their tools in hand. This does not require a separate Act for the focus — it is part of the same action.
Blueprint System & First Construction
Depth 1
The Blueprint system becomes active. Begin with 2 known Blueprints (chosen from the Tier I list) and 1 active Blueprint slot. Additional Blueprint slots unlock at Depths 3, 6, 9, and 14.

The First Construction: At Depth 1, the Accord-Maker constructs their first Aetheric Turret or Aetheric Lantern at no material cost — a practice model, produced during the session that establishes the character. This represents the training construction that completed their initiation into the tradition. The Concordance should make this moment a scene.
Aetheric Knowledge
Depth 1
The Accord-Maker gains Mastery in two Disciplines from: Aetheric Lore, History, Investigation, Medicine, Nature, or Sleight of Hand. They also gain Mastery in artisan's tools of their choice (the tools that function as their focus).

Additionally: the Accord-Maker always knows whether an object they examine has been Infused, Blueprinted, or otherwise Aetherically worked — and approximately when, by what tier of practitioner, and whether the agreement is still stable. This is professional recognition, not a Working.
Right Tool for the Job
Depth 2
During a Brief Attunement, the Accord-Maker may produce any nonmagical artisan's tool from their portable kit without having specifically packed it — the agreement between their kit and their needs produces whatever is required. This covers any tool worth 25gp or less. It does not cover materials — only the instruments for working with them.

Additionally: when the Accord-Maker succeeds on a crafting Active Resonance using their Mastered tools, they may immediately begin a second related crafting task as part of the same time period — two things built in the time one normally takes, if both use the same tool set.
Aetheric Channels — Limited Access
Depth 1
Unlike most Weave-capable Paths, the Accord-Maker has a restricted Aetheric Channel pool — they are engineers, not weavers. They gain Channels as follows:

Depths 1–4: Two 1st-tier Channels per Deep Attunement
Depths 5–8: Two 1st-tier + two 2nd-tier
Depths 9–12: Two each of 1st, 2nd, 3rd-tier
Depths 13–16: Two each of 1st through 4th-tier
Depths 17–20: Two each of 1st through 5th-tier

The Accord-Maker does not gain higher tiers through natural progression. Infusions and Blueprints do not cost Aetheric Channels — they are pre-negotiated. Channels are used only for active Workings from the Accord-Maker's Working list, which is curated rather than comprehensive.

Aetheric Flash of Genius
Depth 7
When a creature within 30 ft of the Accord-Maker makes an Active Resonance or Warding Resonance, the Accord-Maker may use their Reflex to add their Acuity modifier to the roll — identifying the precise physical or Aetheric adjustment that would improve the outcome and communicating it instantly. This is not a Working. It is expertise. It cannot be suppressed by Aether-Dampening Ore.

Usage: Once per Pulse maximum. The Accord-Maker must be able to perceive the situation — they cannot Flash of Genius for a roll they cannot observe.
Aetheric Infusion Mastery
Depth 11
When the Accord-Maker applies an Infusion, the Aetheric agreement is now so efficient that the item effectively counts as an Aetheric Artifact of Attuned tier — without requiring the recipient to spend an Attunement/Resonance Binding slot. Up to three Infused items may benefit from this simultaneously. Items beyond three still require normal Resonance Binding.

Additionally: Infusions no longer end on Deep Attunement automatically. The Accord-Maker may choose to refresh them during Deep Attunement — but an Infusion maintained without refreshing gradually improves: after 30 days of continuous maintenance, the Infusion's bonus increases by 1 (a +1 weapon becomes +2, etc.). Maximum improvement through this method: +3.
Aetheric Soul
Depth 14
The Accord-Maker's constant work with Aetheric agreements has integrated a persistent Aetheric field into their body. They gain the following permanent properties:

• Resistance to force and Aetheric damage — the body has learned to absorb rather than receive.
• Immunity to disease — the Aetheric field maintains biological stability.
• The Accord-Maker does not age — time continues, but biological degradation has been negotiated away. They can still be killed. They simply won't die of old age.
• Their Infusion limit increases by 2 permanently — the Aetheric Soul provides additional bandwidth for active agreements.

The Grand Prime Forgeman discovered this feature after two centuries of practice. He did not consider it remarkable — it was simply the logical conclusion of maintaining enough agreements simultaneously for long enough.
Aetheric Perfection
Depth 20
The Accord-Maker's mastery has reached a point where the agreements they negotiate are so efficient that they require minimal maintenance. Once per Deep Attunement, when the Accord-Maker rolls any die for an Active Resonance, Warding Resonance, damage roll, or Working effect and dislikes the result, they may use their Reaction to reroll — keeping the new result regardless. Additionally: all Infusions the Accord-Maker maintains now function at +1 above their stated values. A +2 Infusion becomes effectively +3. A resistance Infusion grants immunity instead of resistance. The agreements are simply that good.

The four Callings of the Accord-Maker represent the four most developed specializations within the tradition — each one taking the core Infusion and Blueprint systems in a specific direction and adding tools the base Path does not provide. All four are chosen at Depth 3.

The Dunmarim Alchemist
Beta-aspected · Dunmarim Tradition

The Alchemist is the Accord-Maker's most distinctly Dunmarim expression — solutions that work through chemistry rather than Aetheric agreements directly, making them the only practitioner tradition that can function equally well with or without Aetheric cooperation. The Dunmarim apothecaries who developed this line of practice considered it the most honest form of the tradition: if the Aether doesn't want to cooperate, use better chemistry.

Alchemical Savant (Depth 3): The Alchemist gains Mastery in the Alchemist's Supplies tool and adds their Acuity modifier to the damage and healing of any Working they deliver through a potion, elixir, or alchemical preparation — including healing Workings administered as a liquid. Additionally: when the Alchemist uses a Healing Potion, it heals maximum Vitality rather than rolling. The agreement is pre-calibrated to be as efficient as possible.
Experimental Elixir (Depth 3): At the end of each Deep Attunement, the Alchemist produces a number of Experimental Elixirs equal to their Acuity modifier (minimum 1). Roll d6 for each: 1 = Healing (2d4+2 Vitality restored), 2 = Swiftness (Resonant Fortune on Swiftness Active Resonances for 1 hour), 3 = Resilience (1d6+1 Aetheric Buffer), 4 = Boldness (immune to Dread-Struck for 1 hour), 5 = Flight (fly 10 ft per Pulse for 10 minutes), 6 = Transformation (choose one Minor Aspect from the Primal Accord's Aspect table — lasts 10 minutes). The Alchemist cannot choose the results — they can only control the quality of the reagents. The Aether agrees to participate on its own terms. Elixirs expire at the next Deep Attunement if unused.
Restorative Reagents (Depth 5): When the Alchemist casts a healing Working by administering an elixir or potion, they may remove one of the following conditions simultaneously at no additional Channel cost: Corrupted, Dread-Struck, or one level of Aetheric Depletion. The chemistry addresses both the symptom and its Aetheric cause simultaneously.
Chemical Mastery (Depth 9): The Alchemist gains immunity to poison and the Corrupted condition — years of working with volatile substances have produced comprehensive biological adaptation. Additionally: once per Deep Attunement, the Alchemist may produce one dose of Greater Restoration or Raise Dead as a 1-hour alchemical process requiring 300gp in reagents. These are not Workings — they are chemical solutions to biological problems. They function inside the Stillness of Dunmar.
The Runic Armorer
Alpha-aspected · Dunmarim Rune-Smith Tradition

The Runic Armorer takes the Dunmarim's master craft tradition and applies it to personal protection — infusing a suit of armor so thoroughly that it becomes a second body rather than an external layer. The rune-smith tradition predates the Accord-Maker's formal synthesis with Jade Empire engineering. It is the oldest continuous line of the practice and the one most Dunmarim recognize as legitimate.

Arcane Armor (Depth 3): The Runic Armorer designates one suit of armor as their Arcane Armor — spending their Deep Attunement Infusion work on it specifically. The Arcane Armor: always fits perfectly (no disadvantage from mismatched size); can be donned or doffed as an Act rather than requiring the standard time; and functions as the Armorer's Weaving focus, meaning they do not need separate artisan's tools to apply Infusions while wearing it. The armor is always Infused — the agreement is built into the armor's fundamental structure rather than sitting on top of it.
Armor Model (Depth 3): Choose one of two configurations for the Arcane Armor. This can be changed during a Deep Attunement:

Guardian Model: Integrated weapon (the armor itself can deliver melee strikes — 1d8 + Forcefulness modifier thunder damage). Thunder Gauntlet ability: a struck creature has Resonant Discord on Strike Resonances against anyone except the Armorer until the start of their next Moment.

Infiltrator Model: Integrated ranged weapon (lightning launcher built into the forearm — 1d6 + Acuity modifier lightning damage, 90-ft range). The armor enhances Stealth rather than impeding it — no Resonant Discord on Stealth Active Resonances regardless of armor type. +1 to Strike Resonance and damage while Aether-Veiled.
Armor Modifications (Depth 3 + every 2 Depths): At Depth 3 and every even Depth thereafter, choose one Armor Modification to integrate permanently into the Arcane Armor:
• Gemini System: Arcane Armor can split into two suits (duplicate armor for an ally, each with half the bonuses)
• Aetheric Propulsion: Fly speed 30 ft
• Reinforced Defense: +1 Guard Rating (may be taken twice)
• Blazing Beacon: Emit bright light 30 ft radius; immune to radiant damage
• Dampening Field: Resistance to one damage type (chosen when taken)
• Perfected Armor: +1 Strike Resonance to all attacks made by the Armorer while wearing it
Perfected Armor (Depth 15): The Arcane Armor's Infusion bonus increases to +2. Additionally: when the Armorer takes damage while wearing the Arcane Armor, they may use their Reflex to reduce that damage by 1d6 + Acuity modifier — the armor's agreements absorb and redistribute incoming force. Usable once per Pulse.
The Siege Engineer
Alpha-aspected · Jade Empire War-Machine Tradition

The Jade Empire developed its Accord-Maker tradition primarily for military applications — constructs, turrets, and deployed battlefield systems that function without requiring a weaver in the line of fire. The Siege Engineer Calling is the most combat-focused expression of the tradition: an Accord-Maker who builds their battlefield before the enemy arrives and then exploits every advantage the construction provides.

Turret Mastery (Depth 3): The Siege Engineer's Aetheric Turrets are significantly enhanced. Turret damage increases to 2d8 + Acuity modifier. Turrets can now be activated from up to 120 ft rather than 60 ft. Additionally: the Siege Engineer may deploy two Turrets simultaneously (using the same Blueprint slot) — this represents a dual-turret configuration that the standard Blueprint cannot achieve without the Engineer's specialization. The Engineer may use their Instinct to command both simultaneously.
Flash of Tactical Genius (Depth 3): Once per Pulse, when an ally within 30 ft makes a Strike Resonance roll and misses, the Siege Engineer may use their Reflex to grant that ally an immediate reroll using Resonant Fortune — identifying the precise adjustment needed in the weapon's angle, timing, or target approach. This is the Flash of Genius feature (normally Depth 7) applied specifically to combat rolls, available early to the Siege Engineer because battlefield optimization is their entire practice.
Fortified Position (Depth 5): The Siege Engineer can establish a Fortified Position during a Brief Attunement — deploying up to their Acuity modifier in Aetheric devices (Turrets, defensive constructs, Aetheric Locks, alarm glyphs) within a 60-ft radius as a single coordinated deployment rather than individually. The coordinated deployment costs the same Blueprint slots as individual deployment but requires only 10 minutes rather than hours. The Position remains active indefinitely but can only be established once per Brief Attunement.
Aetheric Cannon Expertise (Depth 9): The Siege Engineer can fire their Aetheric Cannon as an Instinct rather than an Act — the pre-aimed targeting system responds to thought-level commands rather than requiring physical manipulation. Additionally: the Cannon's recalibration time between shots drops from 1 minute to 1 Pulse — it fires every round if the Engineer wishes. The cooldown represents physical heat management, not Aetheric recovery. The Engineer has solved the heat problem.
The Resonant Architect
Beta-aspected · Grid-Integrated Construction

The Resonant Architect represents the Accord-Maker tradition's most ambitious extension — the application of Infusion and Blueprint principles to large-scale permanent constructions, and the integration of those constructions with the existing Aetheric grid. Where the Alchemist solves immediate problems and the Siege Engineer wins battles, the Resonant Architect builds things that last centuries. They are, in some meaningful sense, the most Kallos-influenced Calling — they are doing what the Precursor civilization did, with better understanding of why it went wrong.

Extended Blueprint Capacity (Depth 3): The Resonant Architect's Blueprint active slots increase by 2. Additionally: they gain access to Tier IV and V Blueprints one Depth earlier than the standard progression (Depth 9 rather than 10 for Tier IV; Depth 12 rather than 14 for Tier V). The Architect's specialization compresses the learning curve for complex constructions.
Grid Integration (Depth 3): The Resonant Architect can integrate any Blueprint-constructed object with a nearby Ceque Line node — if constructed within 300 ft of an active node, the object draws ambient power from the node rather than requiring the Accord-Maker's ongoing maintenance. Integrated objects: never expire, function at +1 above stated values, and alert the Architect (regardless of distance) when tampered with or damaged. This feature is why the Accord-Maker tradition's most accomplished Architects are found in Elethorn — and why the Coil considers them priority targets.
Permanent Construction (Depth 5): The Resonant Architect can construct permanent structures — buildings, bridges, fortifications — with Aetheric properties integrated into their architecture. Construction requires the standard Blueprint time × the structure's scale (a room: standard time; a building: 1 week; a fortification: 1 month). Completed structures gain one Infusion-equivalent property per 10 ft of height or 100 sq ft of floor area (chosen at construction). The Resonant Binding ritual's sixth component — the Ceque-Walker circuit — is essentially a Resonant Architect's work performed at continental scale. An Architect who realizes this has understood something important about what they are.
★ Resonant Node Stabilizer Access (Depth 9): The Resonant Architect automatically learns the Resonant Node Stabilizer Blueprint at Depth 9 without requiring the Grand Prime Forgeman's instruction or Precursor documents — their deep understanding of grid integration produces the same knowledge through applied practice. This is the earliest any Accord-Maker Calling can access this Blueprint. The Concordance should consider what it means when a player character Accord-Maker reaches Depth 9 while Grid-Point Seven is reactivating.
The Grand Prime Forgeman — NPC Mentor
Every Accord-Maker player character should consider their relationship to the Grand Prime Forgeman — the ancient Gear-Forged who escaped the Jade Empire four centuries ago and has been practicing the Accord-Maker tradition longer than any living being. He knows things about Infusion and Blueprint theory that the formal tradition has lost or never developed. He does not teach freely. He teaches when he determines a practitioner is asking the right questions — and he determines this through a technical examination that most applicants find humbling. An Accord-Maker character who establishes a relationship with the Grand Prime Forgeman gains access to advanced Blueprints (★ items) and technical knowledge that no other source provides. The Concordance should use this relationship as a long-term character development arc rather than a single encounter.
