Primal Jarl
Base: Barbarian · Alpha-aspected · Vitality Die: d12
Alpha-dominant Highest Vitality Die

The Primal Jarl walks the edge between civilization and the force that existed before it. For Wolf-Shifters, this edge is the Omote-Ura Gauge — the tension between human discipline and wolf instinct. For all other Primal Jarls, it is the Wrath State — a surge of primal cosmic force that overwhelms rational restraint and channels raw Aetheric momentum directly through the body.

Core Mechanic — Sideros Original
The Wrath State (replaces Rage)
Entering Wrath is not losing control — it is releasing the filter that normally stands between the character's will and the full force of their Aetheric nature. As an Instinct, enter the Wrath State for up to 1 minute. Uses per Deep Attunement: 2 (increases to 3 at Depth 6, 4 at Depth 12, unlimited at Depth 17).

While in Wrath State: Resonant Fortune on Forcefulness Active Resonances and Warding Resonances; +2 to melee damage (increases to +3 at Depth 9, +4 at Depth 16); resistance to slashing, piercing, and bludgeoning damage from non-magical sources; cannot enact Workings or use Weaving abilities — the Aetheric field is fully physical.

Wrath ends if the character hasn't attacked or taken damage since their last Moment, or if they choose to end it. After Wrath ends: Resonant Discord on all Active Resonances for 1 Pulse (the filter resettles).

Wolf-Shifters replace the Wrath State entirely with the Omote-Ura Gauge system — their primal surge is culturally and biologically distinct. A Wolf-Shifter Primal Jarl never uses Wrath; they deepen their Ura expression instead.
Signature Path Features
Primal Endurance
Depth 2
While in Wrath State, the Primal Jarl may add their Endurance modifier to damage rolls in addition to their Forcefulness modifier. The body's resilience becomes offense — every hit they can take, they can also give. Reckless Strike: before making a Strike Resonance while in Wrath, choose to have Resonant Fortune on that Strike Resonance — all Strike Resonances against you have Resonant Fortune until your next Moment.
Primal Anchor
Depth 7
While in Wrath State, the Primal Jarl cannot be moved against their will by any force short of a Legendary Beast. Grapples, shoves, Workings that push or pull — none of them work. The Primal Jarl has planted themselves in the world. Additionally: if a creature tries to grapple the Primal Jarl while they are in Wrath, the Primal Jarl may immediately attempt a counter-grapple as a Reflex.
Eternal Wrath
Depth 15
The Primal Jarl may choose to enter a Persistent Wrath State — one that does not end between encounters and lasts until they choose to exit or take a Deep Attunement. While in Persistent Wrath: all Wrath State benefits apply at all times; cannot take Deep Attunement without first ending Persistent Wrath; Forcefulness Active Resonances are always made with Resonant Fortune. The cost: social Disciplines (Persuasion, Deception, Performance) all have Resonant Discord while Persistent Wrath is active. The Aether is fully physical. Words have no weight.
Callings
Path of the Berserker
Norse Tradition
Frenzied Strike (Depth 3): Once per Wrath State, make one additional melee Strike Resonance as an Instinct. After Wrath ends, gain one level of Aetheric Depletion — the frenzy burns the body's reserves.
Mindless Wrath (Depth 6): While in Wrath State, immune to Dread-Struck and Soul-Swayed. The Hollow Serpent's whispers cannot find purchase in a mind burning this hot.
Retaliation Strike (Depth 10): When you take damage in Wrath State, you may use your Reflex to immediately make one melee Strike Resonance against the attacker.
Path of the Steppe Sovereign
Centaur Tradition
Battle Cry (Depth 3): When entering Wrath State, all allies within 30 ft gain +1d4 to their next Strike Resonance damage roll. The Jarl's surge pulls the pack with it.
Pack Wrath (Depth 6): While in Wrath State, you deal +1d6 damage per ally within 10 ft who is also engaged in melee combat. Maximum +3d6. You fight better surrounded by your own.
Sovereign's Challenge (Depth 10): When entering Wrath State, choose one creature within 60 ft. That creature has Resonant Discord on Strike Resonances against anyone except you until your Wrath ends — the sovereign has claimed priority.
Path of the Beast-Soul
Arcadian Tradition
Beast Aspect (Depth 3): When entering Wrath State, choose one Beast Aspect: Ram (charge deals +2d6, target knocked prone); Claw (unarmed strikes deal 1d8 + Forcefulness modifier); Pelt (natural armor, Guard Rating = 13 + Endurance modifier while unarmored). The aspect lasts for the Wrath's duration.
Bestial Soul (Depth 6): While in Wrath State, you can communicate with beasts and they will not attack you unless you attack first. The primal frequency is universal.
Legendary Beast Empathy (Depth 10): When you encounter a Legendary Beast while not in Wrath State, the Beast registers your Beast-Soul aspect and does not immediately classify you as prey. You have one Pulse of safe contact before normal Beast behavior resumes.

Resonance Seeker
Base: Bard · Omega-aspected · Vitality Die: d8
Omega-dominant Bond-Adjacent Path

The Resonance Seeker understands something the Comet-Magi spent centuries trying to formulate: the Aether responds to harmony. Not musical harmony specifically — though music is one of the most reliable ways to produce it — but the resonance between frequencies that allows one thing to amplify, sustain, or transform another. The Resonance Seeker's art is finding that harmony and exploiting it. They are the Path most naturally aligned with the Fated Bond system because bonds are, in the most literal sense, two frequencies resonating.

Core Mechanic — Sideros Original
Resonant Chord (replaces Bardic Inspiration)
As an Instinct, the Resonance Seeker strikes a Resonant Chord — a specific harmonic frequency directed at one creature within 60 ft. That creature gains a Resonant Chord Die they may use within the next 10 minutes.

Using the Chord Die: Add the Chord Die to one Active Resonance, Strike Resonance, Working Threshold roll, or Warding Resonance roll — after seeing the initial result, before the Concordance determines success. The die is then spent.

Chord Die size: d6 at Depths 1–4; d8 at Depths 5–9; d10 at Depths 10–14; d12 at Depths 15–20.

Chords available per Deep Attunement: Equal to Resonance modifier (minimum 1). Recharge on Deep Attunement — except: if a creature uses a Chord Die and rolls the maximum value, the Resonance Seeker regains one Chord use immediately. The harmony was perfect. It gives back.

Bond Chord: When a Resonant Chord is directed at a Bonded creature, the die size increases by one step (d6→d8, etc.) — the existing harmonic connection amplifies the new one.
Signature Path Features
Frequency Mastery
Depth 2
The Resonance Seeker becomes Mastered in any Discipline after observing an expert use it once in their presence — they don't learn the skill, they harmonize with the frequency of someone who already has it. This Mastery lasts until the next Deep Attunement, at which point it either deepens into permanent Half-Mastery or fades entirely (player's choice whether to maintain through continued contact with that expert).
Countersong
Depth 6
When a creature within 60 ft is targeted by a Working that relies on sound or spoken language, the Resonance Seeker may use their Reflex to produce a countering frequency. The targeted creature has Resonant Fortune on their Warding Resonance against that Working. This covers Soul-Swayed (Charm) effects, Dread-Struck triggered by spoken compulsion, and any Working with a verbal component. Against Auroran communication being disrupted: Countersong doubles in effectiveness — the Seeker can temporarily stabilize an Auroran's frequency in an Aether-Dampening field.
Harmonic Resonance Field
Depth 14
The Resonance Seeker's mastery reaches a point where their presence itself harmonizes the Aetheric field around them. All allies within 30 ft may add the Seeker's Resonance modifier to their Warding Resonances against Dread-Struck, Soul-Swayed, and Resonance-Shattered conditions. This is passive, permanent, and does not require Sustained Resonance. Additionally: all Bonded allies within 30 ft have Resonant Fortune on rolls to use their Bond abilities — the Seeker's harmonic field amplifies the Bond's existing resonance.
Callings
The Lore-Keeper
Scholar-Performer
Cutting Frequency (Depth 3): Once per Pulse, when a creature within 60 ft makes an Active Resonance or Strike Resonance, you may use your Reflex to impose Resonant Discord on that roll — you've identified and broadcast the dissonant frequency in their technique.
Frequency Archive (Depth 6): You retain the harmonic impression of every Working you have witnessed — you know the frequency and can reproduce it as a Working of equivalent tier, once, before the impression fades. This is not learning the Working; it is mimicking the frequency one time.
Lore Mastery (Depth 14): Mastered in all knowledge Disciplines (Aetheric Lore, History, Nature). You are the walking record of the world's frequencies. When you recite knowledge relevant to the current situation, all allies who hear you have Resonant Fortune on their next relevant Active Resonance.
The Valor-Singer
Combat-Performer
Battle Chord (Depth 3): You may use Resonant Chords in combat without sacrificing your Act. Chords granted as Instinct during combat persist through the entire encounter rather than expiring in 10 minutes.
Armor of Frequencies (Depth 6): While you can hear and speak, your Resonance Seeker Workings that require Sustained Resonance do not break when you take damage — the frequency holds because your body reinforces it in real time.
Combat Inspiration (Depth 14): Creatures who use your Resonant Chord on a Strike Resonance may add the die result to the damage roll as well as the attack roll — the Chord amplifies the entire strike, not just the aim.
The Bond-Harmonist
Fated Bond Specialist
Bond Sight (Depth 3): You perceive Fated Bonds as visible threads of light between Bonded individuals — always active, no Instinct required. You see tier, health, and whether the bond is acknowledged, same as the Stellar Cartographer's Bond Cartography but without reading Fate Numbers.
Bond Amplification (Depth 6): Once per Deep Attunement, by performing 10 minutes of focused harmonic work directed at a Bonded pair or group, you amplify their Bond for 24 hours: all Bond abilities operate at one tier higher than their current tier. The harmony you create supplements the existing resonance.
Resonant Weaving (Depth 14): You may attempt to introduce two unBonded individuals whose Fate Numbers are within range to each other — facilitating a Paired Bond formation as a 1-hour ritual. Both individuals must be present and willing. The Loom decides whether the bond takes; your work creates the conditions but cannot force the recognition. Success rate: Resonant Fortune on the Loom Test for both individuals.

Zodiac Priest
Base: Cleric · Omega-aspected · Vitality Die: d8 · Epic Tier to Depth 30
Omega-dominant Epic Tier Path

The Zodiac Priest does not worship a god. Every god is a beast now — stripped of divinity, wandering the earth it once governed from the sky. The Zodiac Priest tends the memory of what those gods were. They serve the zodiac domain itself — the cosmic force that persists even after its divine embodiment has fallen. A Leo-domain Priest serves heroic courage and solar majesty not because the Nemean Lion ordained them, but because those forces are real, and someone has to hold them.

Core Mechanic — Sideros Original
Aetheric Benediction (replaces Channel Divinity)
Twice per Deep Attunement (increases at Depth 6), the Zodiac Priest may channel their sign's domain force directly through themselves — not a Working, but a Benediction, an act of cosmic law expressing through a mortal vessel.

Benediction of Restoration: Touch one creature. Restore Vitality equal to 5 × Mastery Bonus. Remove one condition. Cannot be used in combat on yourself.

Benediction of the Domain: Express your sign's domain force in an encounter-reshaping way. The specific effect is determined by the Zodiac Calling chosen (see below) — each sign's domain benediction is unique. This is not a Working; it cannot be suppressed by Aether-Dampening Ore.

Aetheric Recovery: Once per Deep Attunement, regain one expended Aetheric Channel of any tier up to half the Priest's Resonance Depth (rounded down). The domain gives back what it uses. Replaces Arcane Recovery's equivalent function for this Path.
Signature Path Features
Domain Discipline Mastery
Depth 1
Each Zodiac Priest gains Mastery in two Disciplines determined by their sign's domain. These Disciplines always benefit from the domain's Aetheric enhancement — the Priest's connection to their sign amplifies even mundane application of these skills. Additionally: Zodiac Priests prepare their Workings from the full list of their sign's domain, plus the universal Priest list — they do not prepare individual Workings but rather have access to any Working in their domain at any time. The domain is always open to them.
Legendary Beast Communion
Depth 7
The Zodiac Priest may attempt direct communion with their sign's Legendary Beast once per week — a 1-hour meditation ritual. The Beast is not controlled and may refuse. On a successful Attunement Active Resonance (Threshold 18, Resonant Fortune), the Priest receives one communication from the Beast — not language, but intention, image, and emotional weight. The Beast's cognitive state (confused, angry, serene, alert) is always clear; what it is responding to often requires interpretation. A Zodiac Priest who has achieved Depth 21 can communicate two-directionally — the Beast begins to respond to them specifically rather than simply broadcasting.
Domain Embodiment
Depth 17
For 1 minute per Deep Attunement, the Zodiac Priest may fully embody their sign's domain force. During Embodiment: all Benediction effects double; all domain Workings cost no Aetheric Channels; the Priest's appearance shifts to reflect the domain (a Leo Priest's eyes become solar gold, a Scorpio Priest's shadow moves independently). Creatures witnessing Embodiment must make a Resonance Warding Resonance (Threshold 18) or be Dread-Struck for 1 Pulse — not from malice, but from proximity to a mortal fully expressing divine force.

♊ Gemini Domain — Duality & Knowledge

Domain Workings include: Identify, Detect Thoughts, Sending, Arcane Eye, Legend Lore. All gather, transmit, or process knowledge across distance or minds.

Fade-Guardian
Paladin Subclass: Oath of the Fading Veil · Omega-aspected · Vitality Die: d10
Veil-Walker Paladin Subclass

The Fade-Guardians take the Oath of the Fading Veil — the oath to stand at the threshold between the mortal world and the Otherworld and ensure nothing crosses in either direction without reason. They are not gatekeepers of death. They are watchers of permeability — the places where the veil thins, the moments when something bleeds through, the creatures that exist partly on both sides. The Fae-Blooded Kindred makes the most natural Fade-Guardian, but any Kindred who has spent time in the Fade-Bleed zone of Argentum, the Otherworld border-zones of the Tuathan Archipelago, or Deep Anthos City's lower caverns may be called to this oath.

Core Mechanic — Sideros Original
Veil Walk & Veil Smite (replaces Channel Divinity + Divine Smite)
Veil Walk (Channel Divinity, 1 use): As an Act, step halfway into the Otherworld for up to 1 minute. While Veil-Walking: translucent and partially visible to both worlds; attacks against you have Resonant Discord; you move through creatures as difficult terrain; you can perceive and interact with Otherworld entities invisible to others; you cannot make physical attacks against fully material creatures. Ending Veil Walk: Attunement Warding Resonance (Threshold 12) — on failure, you step fully through and require allies or an Otherworld entity's assistance to return.

Veil Smite (replaces Divine Smite): When you hit with a melee Strike Resonance, expend one Aetheric Channel to deal bonus radiant damage: 2d8 per Channel tier, +1d8 against Void-Husks and creatures with dissolved Fate Numbers. The damage is not fire or force — it is the Otherworld's light, which Void-energy cannot absorb.
Oath Features
Seasonal Power
Depth 3
The Fade-Guardian's connection to the Tuathan Otherworld tradition aligns them with the Celtic Tree Zodiac. Their Oath powers are enhanced during their birth-tree's season: both Channel Divinity uses recharge at sunrise during the season; Veil Walk duration doubles; Veil Smite deals +1d8 additional damage. Outside their season, one Channel Divinity use per Deep Attunement instead of two. At solstices and equinoxes: both effects (seasonal and off-season) become available simultaneously for 24 hours.
Otherworld Sight
Depth 7
The Fade-Guardian no longer needs to Veil Walk to perceive Otherworld entities — they always see the full spectrum of both worlds simultaneously. This grants: automatic detection of Fae-Blooded Kindred using their Twilight Step; awareness of active Otherworld incursions (something crossing the Veil) within 300 ft; the ability to communicate with Otherworld entities without entering the Otherworld. The cost: the Guardian occasionally experiences intrusive Otherworld sensory overlaps — the Concordance describes these as narrative flavor during moments of stress or significance.
Veil Seal
Depth 15
Once per week, the Fade-Guardian may permanently seal a specific Otherworld threshold — a Faerie Mound, a Fade-Bleed zone boundary, or a known crossing point. The seal lasts for one year and one day (a traditional Tuathan binding period), after which it must be renewed. While sealed: no entity may cross the threshold in either direction without the Guardian's explicit permission. A sealed threshold registers on Fae-Blooded perception as an unnatural absence — they feel the doorway and cannot open it.
Oath Spells (added to Oathed Vanguard spell list)
Depths 3–9
Depth 3: Speak with Animals, Faerie Fire
Depth 5: Moonbeam, Pass without Trace
Depth 9: Conjure Animals, Plant Growth
Depths 13–17
Depth 13: Guardian of Faith, Hallucinatory Terrain
Depth 17: Tree Stride, Commune with Nature

Solar Guard
Base: Fighter · Alpha/Beta-aspected · Vitality Die: d10
Alpha or Beta Most Adaptable Path

The Solar Guard is the world's most versatile warrior — not because they have cosmic gifts or inherited power, but because they have mastered the discipline of preparation. Every Solar Guard has a Fighting Discipline (their chosen combat tradition) and an Action Surge equivalent they call the Surge of Will — the ability to push beyond their body's normal limits through sheer trained determination. Where other Paths weave the Aether through cosmic lineage or divine connection, the Solar Guard channels it through will.

Core Mechanic — Sideros Original
Surge of Will (replaces Second Wind + Action Surge)
Vitality Surge (Instinct, 1/Short or Deep Attunement): Restore Vitality equal to 1d10 + Resonance Depth. This is not magical healing — it is trained stress response. Works in anti-magic fields. Works in The Stillness. Will does not require the Aether.

Combat Surge (Act, 2/Short or Deep Attunement — increases at higher Depths): On your Moment, you may take one additional Act. Not an Instinct — a full additional Act. Replaces Action Surge. A Solar Guard in Combat Surge can: attack twice and use an Instinct and still have an Act remaining. This represents trained efficiency reaching a point of genuine superhuman performance without cosmic aid.

Fighting Discipline (chosen at Depth 1): Select one fighting tradition — Archery, Defense, Dueling, Great Weapon, Protection, Throwing, or Unarmed. Each grants a specific passive benefit. Multiple Disciplines may be taken at higher Depths.
Signature Path Features
Tactical Reading
Depth 3
The Solar Guard has studied combat long enough to read it. At the start of each encounter, they may use their first Instinct to assess all hostile creatures within 60 ft — learning each creature's approximate Vitality (full/bloodied/near death), primary attack method (melee/ranged/magical), and one tactical vulnerability (a specific positioning weakness or a condition they have not yet resisted this encounter). This is observation, not magic. It cannot be suppressed.
Extra Strikes
Depth 5, 11, 20
When the Solar Guard uses the Strike Act, they may make two Strike Resonances instead of one (Depth 5). This increases to three at Depth 11 and four at Depth 20. The Solar Guard is the Path with the highest raw strike volume — not because of cosmic power, but because they have trained every day since they could hold a weapon and they have simply gotten very, very good at this.
Indomitable
Depth 9
Once per Deep Attunement, when the Solar Guard fails a Warding Resonance, they may reroll it with Resonant Fortune. The reroll must be used. At Depth 13: twice per Deep Attunement. At Depth 17: three times. This is not cosmic resilience — it is the refusal of a trained warrior to accept what just happened to them. A Solar Guard who has failed a save looks at the result and decides it is unacceptable.
Callings

Know Your Enemy (Depth 7): After observing a creature for 1 minute, or after one combat encounter with it, you know its exact current Vitality, all resistances and immunities, and its Primal Essence.
Relentless (Depth 10): When you are reduced to 0 Vitality but not killed outright, you may make one Strike Resonance before falling. The Solar Chivalry's code requires one last act of defiance.
Psi-Warrior

Psionic Strike (Depth 3): Once per Pulse, when you hit with a Strike Resonance, add 1d6 psychic damage. The Jade Empire's warrior tradition trains the mind as a weapon.
Telekinetic Movement (Depth 7): Move one object or creature within 30 ft up to 30 ft as an Instinct. Creatures make a Forcefulness Warding Resonance (Threshold = 8 + Mastery + Attunement) to resist.
Bulwark of Force (Depth 10): Once per Deep Attunement, create an invisible barrier of psychic force within 30 ft — a 10-ft cube that blocks all movement through it for 1 minute.
Runic Knight
Norse Tradition
Rune Carving (Depth 3): Learn two runes from the Elder Futhark tradition. Each rune may be inscribed on one weapon or piece of armor per Deep Attunement, granting a specific passive benefit (Cloud: Resonant Fortune on Swiftness; Stone: +2 Guard Rating; Fire: +1d6 fire on strikes; Frost: Warding Resonance immunity to cold).
Giant's Might (Depth 7): As an Instinct, grow to Large size for 1 minute. Gain Resonant Fortune on Forcefulness Active Resonances, +1d6 damage, and can grapple creatures up to Huge size.
Runic Shield (Depth 10): When an ally within 60 ft is hit by a Strike Resonance, use your Reflex to impose Resonant Discord on that roll — the runes extend their protection through the warrior's will.

Rāśi-Siddha
Base: Monk · Omega-aspected · Vitality Die: d8 · Most complete Path
Omega-dominant Tapas Points (fully built)

The Rāśi-Siddha is the most complete Path in the game — Tapas Points are fully defined, all three expenditure tiers (Satvic/Rajasic/Tamasic) are built, and the Kanya exception (curing Ashen Coil blight-rot) is established. This entry defines the remaining elements: the Unarmored Guard Rating formula, the Resonance Depth progression, and the twelve Scattered Temple Callings.

Core Mechanic — Already Built
Tapas Points (fully defined in Mechanics Codex)
Pool = Mastery Bonus × Attunement modifier (minimum 2). Three expenditure tiers: Satvic (purification, lower cost), Rajasic (active, standard cost), Tamasic (corrupting, higher cost, Tapas Depletion risk). Regeneration: full on Deep Attunement; partial on meditative Brief Attunement.

Unarmored Guard Rating: 10 + Attunement modifier + Mastery Bonus while at full Tapas pool (Ascetic Stillness ability). Without a full pool: 10 + Attunement modifier + Swiftness modifier.

Siddha Strike: Unarmed strikes count as Aetheric Artifacts for the purpose of overcoming damage resistance — the mantra in the body makes the fist a Working. Unarmed damage: 1d6 at Depths 1–4; 1d8 at 5–10; 1d10 at 11–16; 1d12 at 17+.
The Twelve Scattered Temple Callings

Each Temple aligns to one zodiac sign. The Calling shapes which Tapas abilities the Siddha emphasizes.

Mesha Temple (♈ Aries)

Community Shield (Depth 11): Passive. All allies within 30 ft have Resonant Fortune on Warding Resonances against Corrupted and disease conditions while you have 4+ Tapas remaining.
