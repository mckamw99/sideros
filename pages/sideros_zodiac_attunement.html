Sideros / Astrolan — The Living Zodiac

Zodiac Attunement

The Legendary Beasts are the fallen gods, walking the earth. To encounter one is to touch the Aether at its source. To survive that encounter is to be changed.

The twelve Legendary Beasts were once the twelve gods of Sideros — the Pantheonic Council that governed the world in Era I. When Ophiuchus was corrupted by the Hollow Serpent's influence and shattered its own divine form in Era II, the remaining twelve gods chose to follow, shattering themselves into vast Aetheric presences that could survive the Fall without dissolving into the Void. They became the Legendary Beasts: each carrying a fragment of divine consciousness, each attuned to one zodiac sign's cosmic domain, each wandering the world they once governed.

They are not approachable. They are not friendly. They are not gods in the active sense — they no longer govern. But they remember what they were, and they recognize mortals whose soul-frequency resonates with their domain. A mortal who encounters a Legendary Beast and survives the recognition is changed. That change, formalized, is the Zodiac Attunement system.

How Attunement Works — The Three Requirements
1. Encounter
The character must be in the physical presence of their sign's Legendary Beast — within 300 ft, aware of its presence, and recognized by the Beast in turn. The Beast must perceive the character. This cannot be faked, simulated, or achieved through recorded Aetheric impressions. The recognition must be real.

What counts as an encounter: Direct proximity; a deliberate attempt to approach the Beast that the Beast acknowledges; being discovered by the Beast in its territory. Witnessing from a distance (greater than 300 ft) does not count unless the Beast actively turns its attention toward the character.

What does not count: Viewing through Scrying; viewing through Arcane Eye or similar; reading accounts; dreaming about the Beast.
2. Survival
The character must survive the encounter with their Fate Number intact — not as a Void-Husk, not corrupted. A character who survives an encounter with their sign's Beast having been Void-Touched during it does not qualify; the Void-Touched exposure interferes with the recognition frequency. The Kanya Siddha can purify Void-Touched Exposure before an attempted second encounter.
3. Resonance Depth 4
The Zodiac Attunement Feat is available at Resonance Depth 4. A character who encounters their Beast before Depth 4 still qualifies for the prerequisite — the encounter does not need to happen at Depth 4, only before the Feat is taken. Characters may encounter their Beast at Depth 1 and wait until Depth 4 to formalize the Attunement.

Exception — Gear-Forged: Gear-Forged are calibrated at creation to a zodiac sign and receive their Zodiac Attunement Feat automatically at Depth 1 without requiring an encounter. Their Geode heart is the encounter, in a sense.
Attunement of Another Sign's Beast
A character may encounter and be recognized by a Beast that is not their sign. This does not grant the Zodiac Attunement Feat (which is sign-locked) but does grant a Secondary Attunement — a single, specific ability from the other Beast's domain, available once per Deep Attunement. The Concordance determines the specific ability based on the encounter's circumstances and what the Beast appeared to offer. Secondary Attunements cannot be trained toward or predicted — they are gifts, and Beasts give what they choose.

The Cross-Cultural Feat Variant at Resonance Depth 8 is a formalized version of Secondary Attunement — representing a character who has invested in the second tradition deeply enough for it to become reliable.
What the Attunement Feat Provides

The full mechanical details of each Zodiac Attunement Feat are in the Mechanics Codex. Summarized here: each Feat provides a passive benefit (always active), an active ability (once per Pulse or once per encounter), and a unique cosmic recognition (the Beast of your sign does not aggress toward you by default — you have been seen). The Feat scales at Resonance Depth 8 through the Cross-Cultural Feat Variant.

On Legendary Beasts and Violence: The Legendary Beasts are not encounter bosses. They are world-scale entities whose combat statistics, if they existed, would be irrelevant to any mortal party. A fight with a Legendary Beast is not a fight — it is a catastrophe. The Concordance should treat encounters with Legendary Beasts as narrative events, not combat encounters. What the party does, how the Beast responds, and what survives are all Concordance decisions shaped by the session's needs. The Beasts are not plot obstacles. They are the world's heartbeat.

Not all Legendary Beast encounters are the same. The Concordance has multiple encounter types to deploy depending on what the session needs — from a distant glimpse that still counts as recognition to a full active encounter where the Beast has specific intent.

What Happens During an Encounter
The Recognition Moment
When a Legendary Beast is within 300 ft of a character whose sign matches its own, both the character and the Beast feel the resonance. The character feels it as a physical sensation — similar to Fated Bond recognition but more overwhelming, less personal. More like being struck by a bell tone than meeting a person. The Beast responds based on its current state (territorial/calm/curious/dormant).

Stellar Cartographers within range automatically perceive the encounter as a massive Aetheric event — the Beast's presence reads as a Fate Number so large it nearly pushes off the scale. They cannot read the Beast's specific Number, only its vastness.
Endurance Warding Resonances During Encounter
Characters in the presence of a Legendary Beast make an Attunement Warding Resonance DC 15 each Pulse or become Dazed for that Pulse as the Aetheric pressure overwhelms their perceptual capacity. Characters whose sign matches the Beast have Resonant Fortune on this Warding Resonance. Accord-Walker Beast Companions of the same sign as the Beast are immune — they have been calibrated to this frequency.

Failure does not disqualify the encounter. A Dazed character still counts as present and perceived by the Beast.
The Departure
Legendary Beasts do not linger unless they have specific intent. Most encounters end with the Beast departing — it has seen what it came to see, assessed the Aetheric situation, and moved on. The aftermath is: all characters within 300 ft make Attunement Active Resonances DC 12 or spend the next hour processing the encounter (effectively non-functional for complex tasks). Characters whose sign matched the Beast's automatically succeed this check — for them, the encounter feels like recognition rather than intrusion.

Twelve Legendary Beasts walk the world — each a fallen god, each attuned to one zodiac domain, each aware of the world they once governed at a level that makes mortal comprehension of their interior lives impossible. What follows is what can be known about each.

The Ashen Ram — Mesha (Aries)
First-to-charge, Fire-Walker, the Initiating Force
Volcanic Caldera Range, near Mesha Temple
Appearance
A ram the size of a small building, coat perpetually smouldering as if the wool has nearly caught fire but never quite does. Hooves leave scorched impressions in stone. The eyes are two points of white-hot intensity that read to Stellar Cartographers as Number 1 — the beginning of everything.
Behaviour
The Ashen Ram is always moving. It charges — not at anything in particular, just perpetually forward. Encounters happen because you are in its path, not because it sought you out. It is the only Legendary Beast that will run directly through an obstacle rather than around it. The Mesha Temple was built where the Ram charges, not where it rests.
Attunement Gift — Mesha's Charge
See Mechanics Codex — Zodiac Attunement Feats. The Ram's gift is initiative and momentum: always act first, and your charges knock foes prone.
Current Status
Active and uncontested in its territory. Lord Aries has ongoing friction with the Mesha Temple over whether the Ram's charge patterns have changed — the Temple says no; Lord Aries' surveyors say the charge trajectories have shifted 12 degrees southeast over the last century, toward Grid-Point Seven.

Khalkotauros — Vrishabha (Taurus)
The Bronze Bull, Earth-Keeper, the Patient One
Taurian Plains (active range) · Minotaur-Warden's Demesne, Arcadia Major (deep lair)
Appearance
A bull of living earth and stone, slow-moving, massive. The horns are actual granite. When it breathes, the ground above it trembles. It is always underground except during the Spring Attunement — a three-day period when it rises and walks the Plains before returning below.

Arcadia Major: The Minotaur-Warden's Demesne — the Khalkotauros's true labyrinthine lair — is located in Arcadia Major, the frontier continent of natural and beast territories distinct from both Elethorn (Precursor ruins) and the mainland. The Bull's influence is felt throughout the Taurian Plains (its active range), but its physical presence is primarily in Arcadia Major.
Behaviour
Patient to a degree that humans find disconcerting. Khalkotauros can remain motionless for years. When the Mycelian Brood's network began disturbing its underground territory, it did not respond immediately. It has been watching for a decade. Lady Taurus's hidden agricultural network accidentally aligns with the Bull's own territorial monitoring patterns — the Bull knows about the Triad and is permitting it, for now.
Attunement Gift — Khalkotauros's Endurance
Massively expanded Vitality maximum and the ability to refuse death once per Deep Attunement. The Bull does not die easily; neither does its Attuned.
Current Status
Underground. The Mycelian Brood situation has made it increasingly active — tremors in the Taurian Plains that the Order of Pure Reason attributes to geological activity are actually Khalkotauros repositioning. If it surfaces off-schedule, something has gone badly wrong.

The Twin-Serpents — Mithuna (Gemini)
Two Beings as One, the Mirror-Pair, the Debate Without End
Scribe's Folly ruins · Twin Republics · Aetherian coastline
Appearance
Two enormous serpents, apparently identical, that are never more than 30 ft apart and never less than 10 ft apart. They do not touch. When one is perceived as stationary, the other is moving. When one is above water, the other is below. Scholars debate whether they are one consciousness or two — the Mithuna Temple says the debate is the point.
Behaviour
The Twin-Serpents are the most communicative of the Legendary Beasts, though what they communicate is always ambiguous. They appear to be in constant argument with each other through behavior — one approaches, one retreats; one displays a territory, the other ignores it. Encounters with Gemini-sign characters produce what witnesses describe as "being shown both sides of something" simultaneously.
Attunement Gift — Twin-Serpents' Duality
Maintain two Sustained Resonance Workings simultaneously; reroll any failed Active Resonance once per Deep Attunement. The gift of holding opposites without resolution.
Current Status
The Twin-Serpents have been sighted three times near Port Damaris in the last year — unusual for their range. Port Damaris's Shadow-Council considers their presence an omen and has been attempting to identify what they are watching.

Scribe's Folly: The Twin-Serpents' actual lair is the Scribe's Folly — the unstable, ruined levels of a magically collapsed library in Aetheria. The library collapsed approximately sixty years ago when experimental gravity Working shattered; the Twin-Serpents moved into the ruins shortly afterward, drawn by the magical instability. The gravity anomaly disrupting the Aetherial Skypaths is not passive environmental damage — it is the Aetheric weight of the Twin-Serpents' combined presence. When both Serpents are in residence at Scribe's Folly, the gravity fields around the ruins destabilize and cargo losses on the Skypaths run at approximately 8% per transit. When they range away (as they currently have toward Port Damaris), the anomaly lessens but does not fully clear — the ruins retain residual Aetheric imprinting. Merchants who want the Skypath anomaly resolved need the Twin-Serpents to either leave the ruins permanently or return to them. Neither outcome is negotiable through conventional means.

The Adamant Crab — Karkata (Cancer)
Shell-Bearer, the Warden of the Shore, Tide-Walker
Sundered Coast tidal zones (active territory) · Regency of the Snapper / Carapace Archipelago, Abyssal Sea (resting grounds)
Appearance
A crab the size of a city block, shell dense enough that it has accumulated coral, smaller sea life, and in one case a small shipwreck on its back over centuries. It moves between the tidal zone and open sea on a rhythm only the Karkata Temple can predict — and only approximately. Its shell is one of the five Resonant Binding components.
The Carapace Archipelago
The Adamant Crab migrates between two territories. Its active territory is the tidal zones of the Sundered Coast — where it patrols and guards. Its resting territory is the Carapace Archipelago in the Abyssal Sea of Tiamat to the south — a cluster of islands formed over millennia from shed shell fragments and accumulated rock around old molting sites. The living Crab visits the Archipelago during its resting cycle; between visits, the islands stand unoccupied and serve as a navigational waypoint for the Court of the Sunken Stars — a culture of hardy, deeply superstitious seafarers who inhabit the Abyssal Sea and navigate by the leviathans of the deep rather than the stars.
Behaviour
The Adamant Crab is protective. It does not aggress unless something it is protecting is threatened. Its protected territory shifts — it appears to be guarding something that moves, though what that something is has never been identified. The Karkata Temple believes it is guarding the threshold between the living world and the ancestor-space — the liminal zone where the dead linger before dissolving.
Attunement Gift — Adamant's Ward
Impose Resonant Discord on attacks against adjacent allies as a Reflex; always know the exact position of every creature within 30 ft. The Crab's gift is awareness and protection of those nearby.
Current Status
Active. A fragment of its shell shed during the last molting cycle is held by the Bee-Priestess of the Golden Hive — one of the five Resonant Binding components. It does not know this. If it did, it might care.

The Nemean Lion — Simha (Leo)
The Unconquerable, Solar-Maned, King-Maker
Leonine Empire, Aurelia savannah (active range) · Lion-Champion's Demesne, Arcadia Major (den)
Appearance
A lion whose mane is sunlight — not yellow fur but actual radiant light, shifting from dawn gold to noon white to dusk amber depending on time of day. The roar physically resonates — those within 500 ft feel it in their chest. Arcadia Major: The Lion-Champion's Demesne — a sun-bleached, bone-strewn cavern in Arcadia Major's chaparral — is the Nemean Lion's true den. It ranges through the Leonine Empire's savannah south of Aurelia but returns to Arcadia Major to rest. The savannah encounters are the Lion on patrol, not the Lion at home. The Nemean Lion watches the Leonine Empire the way a parent watches a child who has grown into something unexpected. It does not interfere. It observes. When the Solar King's legitimacy was last fully genuine (three kings ago), the Lion was seen at the palace gates at dawn. It has not been seen there since. The succession crisis has made it restless — it has been pacing further into human territory than it has for generations.
Attunement Gift — Nemean's Glory
When you reduce a creature to 0 Vitality, allies gain temporary Vitality and Resonant Fortune. You become immune to Dread-Struck; fear effects reverse onto their sources. The Lion's gift is sovereign radiance.
Current Status
Restless. The Mantra of Divine Right at the Simha Temple is growing quieter — the Lion is aware of this and does not like it. Finding the Lunar Queen (the legitimate Omega-line heir) would likely bring the Lion back to the palace gates. Failing to find them will not.

The Queen Bee — Kanya (Virgo)
Golden Hive-Mother, Purifier, the Bee-Priestess's Patron
Kanya Temple sacred grove, Kanya region (Golden Hive outpost) · Golden Hive Mountain Labyrinth, Arcadia Major (true lair)

A bee the size of a horse, exuding a faint honey-warmth that reduces pain within 30 ft. Its hive — the Golden Hive — is the source of Celestial Honey, one of the five Resonant Binding components. The hive is tended by the Bee-Priestess, who is the only mortal the Queen Bee permits within 10 ft of her. Arcadia Major: The Queen Bee's true lair is the Golden Hive Mountain Labyrinth — a perfectly ordered hexagonal cave system in Arcadia Major. The Golden Hive tended by the Bee-Priestess at the Kanya Temple is a sacred outpost; the true hive, and the true Celestial Honey production, is in Arcadia Major and has never been visited by a non-Bee-aspected mortal. Methodical. The Queen Bee has been tending the same patterns for 400 years. She is aware of every mortal who enters the sacred grove, assesses their Aetheric frequency, and decides within moments whether they belong here. She has never harmed a Kanya-sign character. She has expelled from the grove, without violence, everyone the Ashen Coil has sent to steal the Celestial Honey — eleven times in the last decade.
Attunement Gift — Queen Bee's Precision
Add Attunement modifier to damage once per Moment; Help grants Resonant Fortune on two rolls instead of one; diagnose any Aetheric condition in 1 minute. The Bee's gift is precise and complete perception.
Current Status
Stable. The Bee-Priestess knows who has received Celestial Honey — one person in the last 500 years — and has told no one. The Queen knows the Resonant Binding ritual is approaching. She has been producing more Honey than usual. The Concordance decides whether this is preparation or coincidence.

The Primal Accord Path's Accord-Walker Calling has the deepest mechanical integration with the Zodiac Attunement system. While all characters can gain a Zodiac Attunement Feat through encounter, the Accord-Walker builds a lifetime practice of cross-sign Beast attunement that goes beyond the Feat system entirely.

Accord-Walker Beast Resonance Pools

At Resonance Depth 3 (when the Accord-Walker Calling is selected), the character begins accumulating Beast Resonance Pools — one per Beast they have meaningfully encountered. Each pool starts at 5 points and can be built up through ongoing encounter and practice, to a maximum of 20.

Building Beast Resonance Pools
Initial encounter: Pool opens at 5 points automatically.
Ongoing encounters: Each subsequent meaningful encounter with the same Beast adds 1d6 to the pool (maximum 20).
Walking the Beast's Ceque Line: Full-line walk adds 1d4 to that Beast's pool permanently.
Deep Attunement at a Beast's node location: Adds 1 point (maximum once per month per location).
Beast Companion of the same sign (if applicable): The companion's presence adds 1 point to that Beast's pool per week of companionship.
Spending Beast Resonance
Beast Resonance Points are spent to activate Accord-Walker features associated with that Beast's sign — sign-aspected Wild Shape forms, Ceque Line abilities, cross-sign attunement effects. Specific costs are detailed in the Primal Accord Path document.

Points refresh on Deep Attunement: roll 1d6 for each active pool and restore that many points (maximum the pool's current maximum, not 20).

The Concordance Note on Encounter Frequency

Legendary Beast encounters are campaign events, not session filler. A Concordance who deploys them too frequently devalues them; one who withholds them entirely frustrates players whose characters are built around Accord-Walker or Beast-Descended features. The following pacing is suggested:

Campaign StageEncounter FrequencyNotes

Late (Depths 11–16)Communion possible; multiple Beasts may respond to the same event simultaneouslyThe Quint Bond's formation, the Resonant Binding ritual progress, or Aethar's Stage 4 will draw Beast attention.
Endgame (Depths 17–20)The Beasts respond to the campaign's climax collectivelyA Quint Bond fully formed, in the presence of the Resonant Binding's five components, may attract all twelve Legendary Beasts simultaneously. The Concordance decides what that looks like.
